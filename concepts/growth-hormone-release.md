# Growth Hormone Release

## Definition
Growth hormone (GH) release is the process by which the anterior pituitary secretes somatotropin into circulation, primarily regulated by the hypothalamic hormones growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) and somatostatin (growth hormone-inhibiting hormone, GHIH). Ghrelin, produced in the stomach and other tissues, also stimulates GH release via the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a).

## Mechanism
- **GHRH pathway:** GHRH binds to GHRH receptors on pituitary somatotrophs, activating adenylyl cyclase and increasing intracellular cAMP, leading to GH synthesis and pulsatile release.
- **Somatostatin inhibition:** Somatostatin acts on somatostatin receptors (SSTR2/5) on somatotrophs to suppress GH release, creating a regulatory oscillation.
- **Ghrelin / GHS-R1a pathway:** Ghrelin and synthetic GH secretagogues (GHRPs, e.g., ipamorelin) bind GHS-R1a to trigger GH release, partly by suppressing somatostatin tone and directly stimulating somatotrophs.
- **IGF-1 negative feedback:** Elevated circulating IGF-1 inhibits GH release via hypothalamic and pituitary feedback loops.

## Peptides that Modulate GH Release
- **GHRH analogues:** [[tesamorelin]] (FDA-approved, 44-aa), [[modified-grf-1-29]] (1-29 fragment, research chemical), sermorelin (discontinued)
- **Ghrelin mimetics / GH secretagogues:** [[ipamorelin]] (selective pentapeptide), GHRP-2, GHRP-6, hexarelin
- **Combinations:** GHRH analogue + GHRP are commonly co-administered in research contexts to produce synergistic GH pulses

## Clinical Relevance
- GH is essential for linear growth in children, and in adults maintains body composition, bone density, lipid metabolism, and cardiovascular health.
- GH deficiency in adults is associated with increased visceral adiposity, reduced muscle mass, impaired quality of life, and adverse metabolic profiles.
- Supraphysiological GH elevation (via exogenous GH or secretagogues) carries risks: insulin resistance, fluid retention, carpal tunnel syndrome, and theoretical neoplasm promotion via IGF-1.

## Controversies & Open Questions
- **Pulsatile vs. continuous GH elevation:** Physiological GH is pulsatile; continuous elevation (e.g., CJC-1295 DAC) may carry different metabolic and receptor-desensitization risks than pulsatile stimulation.
- **GHS-R1a selectivity:** Whether selective GH secretagogues like ipamorelin (which spare ACTH/cortisol) have better long-term safety profiles than non-selective GHRPs remains unstudied in humans.
- **Long-term safety of secretagogues:** No human trial has assessed >1 year of continuous GHRH analogue or GHRP use.
