Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) is a 28-amino acid peptide found in many tissues.
Mechanisms: Acts via VPAC1, VPAC2, and VPAC1/2 receptors. 
Functions: 
- Digestive: Inhibits gastric acid secretion, relaxes smooth muscle.
- Cardiovascular: Potent vasodilator.
- Neurological: Modulates neurotransmission, circadian rhythm in the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN).
- Immunomodulation: Generally anti-inflammatory, inhibits T-cell activation and cytokine production.
Evidence: Strong animal data for vasodilation and immune suppression. Human data is limited to specific clinical applications such as COPD or potential roles in autoimmune disease, but systemic exogenous use is not standard clinical practice.
Safety: Generally considered safe in endogenous levels; exogenous use risk involves hypotension (due to potent vasodilation) and tachycardia.
