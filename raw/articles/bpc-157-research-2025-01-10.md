# BPC-157 — Raw Source Material

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide consisting of 15 amino acids derived from a protective protein found in human gastric juice. It was first investigated by Croatian researchers and has been studied extensively in animal models for its regenerative and cytoprotective properties.

## Key Facts from Multiple Sources

### Discovery
- Isolated from gastric juice by Croatian researchers
- Derived from larger Body Protection Compound (BPC)
- Sequence: Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val

### Mechanism Overview
- Upregulates VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor) → angiogenesis
- Interacts with NO-synthase pathway → nitric oxide modulation
- Stabilizes gut mucosa and tight junctions
- Promotes tendon/ligament collagen remodeling via FAK-paxillin pathway
- Anti-inflammatory: modulates TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β

### Rat Model Studies (Most Common)
- **Tendon healing:** Significantly improved Achilles tendon transection healing (Sikiric et al.)
- **Gastric ulcers:** Healed various GI lesions, protected against NSAID-induced damage
- **Liver damage:** Protected against alcohol/chemical-induced liver injury
- **Nerve regeneration:** Improved sciatic nerve crush recovery
- **Wound healing:** Accelerated skin wound closure
- **Bone healing:** Enhanced fracture healing in some models
- Common experimental dose in rats: 10 μg/kg/day to 10 ng/kg/day (wide range)

### Human Evidence
- **Clinical trials:** Very limited. Small open-label or retrospective studies only. No large RCTs registered as of 2025.
- **Anecdotal:** Extensive community use reports (Reddit r/Peptides, bodybuilding forums) for injury recovery, gut health. Self-reported benefits: faster healing, reduced inflammation, gut symptom improvement. Side effects: occasional mild nausea, lethargy, injection site reactions.
- **Route:** Subcutaneous near injury site OR oral (unusual for peptides but BPC-157 is stable in gastric juice, hence oral bioavailability may exist, though debated)

### Safety Concerns
- **Cancer/angiogenesis:** VEGF upregulation raises theoretical concern about promoting tumor growth. No direct evidence in animal studies, but contraindicated in known/suspected cancers.
- **Unknown long-term effects:** No multi-year human safety data.
- **Regulatory status:** Research chemical only. Not approved by FDA, EMA. Some countries have banned sale.
- **Drug interactions:** May potentiate bleeding risk if combined with anticoagulants (theoretical, via angiogenesis/VEGF). NSAID interaction paradox: protects against NSAID GI damage but may slow NSAID healing effects.

### Dosing (Reported in Communities — Not Medical Advice)
- Subcutaneous: 250-500 mcg/day, sometimes split into 2 doses
- Duration: 2-4 weeks for acute injury; 4-8 weeks for chronic issues
- Oral: 500 mcg/day (unstable in gastric acid unless stabilized formulation)
- Often stacked with TB-500 for synergistic healing

### Comparison with TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)
- BPC-157 more localized healing (tendons, gut, specific injury sites)
- TB-500 more systemic (whole-body recovery, flexibility, immune modulation)
- Many users stack both for injury recovery
- TB-500 has wider human clinical data (HIV-associated muscle wasting trials)
