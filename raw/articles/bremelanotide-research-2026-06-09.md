# Bremelanotide (PT-141) — Raw Research Compilation

## Wikipedia Summary
Bremelanotide (Vyleesi) is an FDA-approved melanocortin receptor agonist for HSDD in premenopausal women. SC injection, 1.75 mg, autoinjector. Approved 2019. Developed by Palatin Technologies. First-in-class per FDA. Nausea 40%, flushing 20.3%, headache 11.3%, injection site reactions 13.2%. Transient BP increase (SBP +6, DBP +3 mmHg), HR decrease. Focal hyperpigmentation risk. Contraindicated in uncontrolled HTN/CVD. Half-life 2.7h. Cyclic heptapeptide analogue of α-MSH. Active metabolite of melanotan II.

## FDA Label (DailyMed, Updated Nov 2025)
- **Indication**: Premenopausal women with acquired, generalized HSDD. NOT for postmenopausal women, men, or performance enhancement.
- **Dose**: 1.75 mg SC via autoinjector, abdomen or thigh, ≥45 min before sexual activity. Max 1 dose/24h, max 8 doses/month. Discontinue after 8 weeks without improvement.
- **Contraindications**: Uncontrolled hypertension or known cardiovascular disease.
- **Warnings**:
  - Transient BP increase (SBP +6, DBP +3 mmHg peak 2-4h post-dose, resolves ~12h). HR decrease ~5 bpm.
  - Focal hyperpigmentation: 1% at ≤8 doses/month; 38% with daily dosing for 8 days. May not resolve. Higher risk in darker skin.
  - Nausea: 40% of patients; 13% needed anti-emetics; 8% discontinued. Improves with second dose. Ondansetron pretreatment did NOT reduce nausea (Phase 4 study).
- **Adverse Reactions**: Nausea 40%, flushing 20.3%, injection site reactions 13.2%, headache 11.3%, vomiting 4.8%, cough 3.3%, fatigue 3.2%, hot flush 2.7%, paresthesia 2.6%, dizziness 2.2%, nasal congestion 2.1%.
- **Discontinuation**: 18% VYLEESI vs 2% placebo due to AEs.
- **Serious AEs**: 1.1% VYLEESI vs 0.5% placebo.
- **Drug Interactions**: Slows gastric emptying → reduces absorption of oral drugs (naltrexone, indomethacin). Avoid with oral naltrexone for addiction treatment.
- **Pregnancy**: Animal studies show fetal harm at ≥16x human AUC (dogs). Pregnancy registry exists. Use effective contraception.
- **Lactation**: No human data. Phase 4 lactation study (NCT06867835) completed 2025, results pending.
- **Carcinogenicity**: No tumor increase in 2-year rat/mouse studies.
- **No REMS program** (unlike flibanserin which had alcohol REMS, later removed).
- **Alcohol**: No clinically significant interaction (studied with 20 mg intranasal, 2.5x Cmax of approved SC dose).

## PubMed Key Papers

### Mechanism & Pharmacology
- **Pfaus et al. 2022 (PMID 33455598)**: Neurobiology review. Bremelanotide activates MC4R in mPOA of hypothalamus → increased dopamine release → excitatory pathway for sexual desire. MC1R binding → melanin expression/pigmentation.
- **King et al. 2007 (PMID 17584130)**: Melanocortin receptors and penile erection review. MC3R and MC4R key for sexual function.
- **Molinoff et al. 2003 (PMID 12851303)**: PT-141 activates neurons in hypothalamus (c-Fos). Penile erections in rats and nonhuman primates. Dose-dependent erectile activity in normal men and ED patients.
- **Sweeney et al. 2023 (PMID 37365323)**: Central melanocortin system review. Notes FDA approvals of bremelanotide, setmelanotide, afamelanotide demonstrate safety of melanocortin peptide class.

### Phase 3 RECONNECT Trials
- **Kingsberg et al. 2019 (PMID 31599840)**: Two identical Phase 3 trials (n=1267). Statistically significant improvements: FSFI-D +0.35 (p<.001), FSDS-DAO Q13 -0.33 (p<.001). Nausea, flushing, headache ≥10%. Safety profile favorable.
- **Clayton et al. 2022 (PMID 35147466)**: Safety profile across entire development program (3500 subjects, 43 studies). Nausea 40%, flushing 20.3%, headache 11.3%. Focal hyperpigmentation rare at label dosing but >1/3 with daily dosing. Small transient BP increases.
- **Simon et al. 2022 (PMID 35230162)**: Subgroup analyses. Bremelanotide effective across age, weight, BMI, testosterone quartiles, hormonal contraceptive use.

### Efficacy Critiques
- **Spielmans 2021 (PMID 33678061)**: Re-analysis. 72.72% of protocol-listed outcomes not reported. AE-induced discontinuation OR=11.98, NNH=6. Participants preferred placebo (OR=0.30, NNH=4). Modest benefits on post-hoc measures of questionable validity.
- **Spielmans & Ellefson 2023 (PMID 36809187)**: Validity evidence for FSFI and FSDS-DAO in HSDD women is questionable. 8 of 11 clinicaltrials.gov-specified outcomes unpublished. Effect sizes nil to small.
- **Mintzes et al. 2021 (PMID 34642243)**: Flibanserin led to 1 additional enjoyable sexual experience every 2 months; bremelanotide to none. Contested indication. Politicized approval process.
- **Cipriani et al. 2022 (PMID 36242769)**: Moderately safe and well-tolerated but overall clinical benefit modest. Significant placebo effect and outcome measures susceptible to expectation biases.

### Weight Loss / Metabolic
- **Spana et al. 2022 (PMID 35170192)**: Two Phase 1 RCTs in obese women (BMI >30). Bremelanotide 3x daily for 16 days: -1.3 kg vs placebo (p<.0001). Caloric intake reduced ~400 kcal/day. MC4R agonism may aid weight loss.

### Oncology (In Vitro)
- **Suzuki et al. 2024 (PMID 39197897)**: Bremelanotide reduced survivin expression and induced cell death in glioblastoma cell lines. Sensitized to temozolomide. MC3R/MC4R as potential glioblastoma targets. In vitro only.

### Other
- **Dhillon & Keam 2019 (PMID 31429064)**: First approval summary. Milestones in development.
- **Mayer & Lynch 2020 (PMID 31893927)**: Review. Safe, limited drug-drug interactions. Clinical benefit may be modest.
- **Edinoff et al. 2022 (PMID 35076581)**: Review. Improvements in desire, arousal, orgasm scores.
- **LiverTox 2021 (PMID 34436837)**: Mild serum enzyme elevations; rare acute liver injury (1 case, transaminases >40x ULN).
- **LactMed 2025 (PMID 31369224)**: No clinical lactation data. MW 1025 → milk levels likely low, probably destroyed in infant GI tract.
- **Hedlund 2004 (PMID 15134289)**: Early development as nasal spray for ED and FSD.
- **Diamond et al. 2006 (PMID 16839319)**: Phase 2a, 18 premenopausal women with FSAD. Intranasal 20 mg. More women reported moderate/high desire vs placebo (p=0.0114). Vaginal vasocongestion not significantly changed.
- **Althof et al. 2019 (PMID 31277966)**: Phase 2b responder analyses. MCID established. 1.75 mg dose achieved statistical significance on all 7 endpoints.
- **Shadiack et al. 2007 (PMID 17584134)**: Melanocortins for male and female sexual dysfunction. Animal models show pre-copulatory behaviors in female rats.

## Key Historical Notes
- Derived from melanotan II (developed at University of Arizona)
- Intranasal formulation halted by FDA in 2007 due to BP increases
- Reformulated as SC injection; Phase 3 trials launched 2014
- Palatin licensed to AMAG Pharmaceuticals (2017) for $60M upfront + milestones
- FDA approved June 2019
- AMAG later sold to Cosette Pharmaceuticals (current marketer)
