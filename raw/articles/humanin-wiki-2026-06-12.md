Humanin is a mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP) encoded within the 16S rRNA gene (MT-RNR2). 
It acts as a cytoprotective agent, which is particularly notable for its anti-apoptotic effects. 
It is thought to protect cells from stress-induced apoptosis and is upregulated in response to certain metabolic stressors.
Mechanism involves interaction with various pathways, often linked to metabolic regulation and survival signaling.
Evidence is predominantly in vitro and in animal models (mouse/rat). 
No large-scale human clinical trials for exogenous administration are widely documented as approved medical use.
