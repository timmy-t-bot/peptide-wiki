# VIP (Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide) — Raw Research Sources

## Source 1: Wikipedia — Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasoactive_intestinal_peptide
- Retrieved: 2026-06-09

### Key Facts
- 28-amino acid peptide hormone, member of glucagon/secretin superfamily
- Ligand of class II G protein-coupled receptors (VPAC1, VPAC2)
- Produced in gut, pancreas, neocortex, suprachiasmatic nuclei (SCN) of hypothalamus
- Half-life in blood: ~2 minutes
- Encoded by VIP gene on chromosome 6 (human)

### Functions
- Digestive: smooth muscle relaxation, stimulates water/electrolyte secretion, inhibits gastric acid secretion, stimulates pepsinogen secretion
- Cardiovascular: coronary vasodilation, positive inotropic and chronotropic effects
- Brain: SCN circadian pacemaker, prolactin regulation, inhibitory interneuron subtype
- Other: vaginal lubrication (doubles volume)

### Mechanisms
- Binds VPAC1 and VPAC2 receptors → G-alpha signaling → adenyl cyclase → cAMP → PKA → CREB phosphorylation
- SCN: VIP is major synchronizing agent; VIP or VPAC2 knockout mice lose circadian rhythm synchronization
- Co-released with GABA in SCN

### Pathology
- VIPoma: neuroendocrine tumor overproducing VIP → WDHA syndrome (watery diarrhea, hypokalemia, achlorhydria)
- Osteoarthritis: conflicting data; VIP shown to prevent cartilage damage in animals

### Social Behavior
- VIP neurons in hypothalamus affect social behaviors (aggression, parental care) in vertebrates
- VIP knockout birds show decreased aggression

## Source 3: PubMed — Henning & Sawmiller (2001) — Cardiovascular Effects
- PMID: 11121793
- Cardiovasc Res. 2001 Jan;49(1):27-37

### Key Findings
- VIP present in heart: epicardial coronary arteries/veins, sinoatrial node, atrium, AV node, ventricles
- 50-100x more potent than acetylcholine as vasodilator (molar basis)
- Increases coronary artery cross-sectional area, decreases coronary vascular resistance, increases coronary blood flow
- Positive inotropic effect; augments developed isometric force at 10^-8 to 10^-5 mol
- Increases heart rate; more potent chronotropic effect than norepinephrine
- Released during coronary artery occlusion and reperfusion; possible free-radical scavenging
- Reduces mean arterial pressure by 10-15%

## Source 4: PubMed — Vosko et al (2007) — Circadian System
- PMID: 17572414
- Gen Comp Endocrinol. 2007 Jun-Jul;152(2-3):165-75

### Key Findings
- VIP defines a cell population within SCN
- Loss of VIP or VPAC2 receptor → major disruption of SCN circadian oscillations and synchronization
- VIP acts through VPAC2 → intracellular signaling → modulates synaptic transmission and intrinsic membrane currents
- VIP neurons connect to endocrine and other output targets
- Parallels with Pigment Dispersing Factor (PDF) in Drosophila circadian system

## Source 6: PubMed — Levin (1991) — Vaginal Lubrication
- PMID: 1778234
- Exp Clin Endocrinol. 1991;98(2):61-9

### Key Findings
- VIP present in nerves near vaginal blood vessels
- IV or subepithelial VIP injection increases vaginal blood flow and induces vaginal fluid production

## Source 7: PubMed — TESICO Trial (2023) — Aviptadil COVID-19
- PMID: 37348524
- Lancet Respir Med. 2023 Sep;11(9):791-803

### Key Findings
- Randomized, placebo-controlled trial of IV aviptadil + remdesivir in COVID-19 hypoxemic respiratory failure
- 28 US sites, 471 participants randomized to aviptadil vs placebo
- Aviptadil dosing: 12-h daily infusion × 3 days (600→1200→1800 pmol/kg)
- **Primary outcome: NOT significant** — OR 1.11 (95% CI 0.80-1.55; p=0.54) for better category at day 90
- **Mortality: NOT significant** — 38% aviptadil vs 36% placebo (HR 1.04, 95% CI 0.77-1.41; p=0.78)
- Safety: death/SAE/organ failure/serious infection/grade 3-4 AE up to day 5: 63% aviptadil vs 56% placebo
- **DSMB stopped trial for futility on May 25, 2022**
- **Conclusion: Aviptadil did not improve outcomes in COVID-19 respiratory failure**

## Source 8: PubMed — Youssef et al (2022) — 60-Day Aviptadil RCT
- PMID: 36044317
- Crit Care Med. 2022 Nov 1;50(11):1577-1586

### Key Findings
- 196 patients, 10 US hospitals, randomized 2:1 aviptadil:placebo
- **Primary endpoint (alive and free from respiratory failure at day 60): NOT significant** (OR 1.6; 95% CI 0.86-3.11)
- **Secondary: survival at 60 days significantly improved** (OR 2.0; 95% CI 1.1-3.9; p=0.035)
- Significant improvement in respiratory distress ratio and reduced IL-6 by day 3 (p=0.02)
- Subgroup: high-flow nasal oxygen patients more likely to achieve primary endpoint (p=0.039)
- Mechanical ventilation patients: 10-fold increased odds of survival (p=0.031)
- No drug-related serious adverse events; acceptable safety profile
- **Authors concluded benefit vs risk favorable despite primary endpoint miss**

## Source 9: PubMed — Pellesi et al (2021) — VIP Migraine Induction
- PMID: 34357396
- JAMA Netw Open. 2021 Aug 2;4(8):e2118543

### Key Findings
- Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover study
- 21 migraine without aura patients (17 women, 4 men; mean age 25.9)
- **2-hour VIP infusion provoked migraine attacks in 15/21 patients (71%) vs 1/21 (5%) with placebo (P<0.001)**
- VIP-induced attacks mimicked spontaneous attacks
- Superficial temporal artery diameter significantly greater after VIP
- **Conclusion: VIP plays important role in migraine pathophysiology; VIP and receptors could be targets for novel migraine drugs**

## Source 10: PubMed — Pellesi et al (2020) — VIP Headache in Healthy Volunteers
- PMID: 32594760
- Cephalalgia. 2020 Oct;40(11):1212-1223

### Key Findings
- Double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover; 12 healthy volunteers
- 2-hour continuous IV VIP infusion
- Superficial temporal artery diameter significantly larger with VIP (p<0.001); dilation >2h
- Higher headache incidence with VIP (p=0.003)
- Headache intensity more evident in post-infusion period (120-200 min) and post-hospital phase (4-12h)
- Cranial parasympathetic activity (tear production) higher during VIP (p=0.033)
- **Conclusion: VIP induces long-lasting cranial vasodilation, parasympathetic activation, and delayed mild headaches in healthy volunteers**

## Source 11: PubMed — Vollesen et al (2020) — VIP in Cluster Headache
- PMID: 32962406
- Cephalalgia. 2020 Nov;40(13):1474-1488

### Key Findings
- Double-blind crossover; 14 episodic active, 15 episodic remission, 15 chronic cluster headache patients
- PACAP38 (10 pmol/kg/min) or VIP (8 pmol/kg/min) over 20 min
- **Episodic active: PACAP38 induced attacks in 6/14, VIP in 5/14 (p=1.000)**
- **Chronic: PACAP38 and VIP both induced attacks in 7/15 (p=0.765)**
- Remission: neither induced attacks
- **Contradicts PAC1-receptor as solely responsible for attack induction**

## Source 12: PubMed — Simon et al (2021) — VIP and Affective Symptoms
- PMID: 33446759
- Sci Rep. 2021 Jan 14;11(1):1406

### Key Findings
- 37 healthy females
- **Negative correlation: VIP levels vs anxiety (r=-0.44, p=0.002) and depression (r=-0.50, p=0.001)**
- VIP-dependent positive functional connectivity: amygdala with right parahippocampus and right lateral OFC
- VIP positively correlated with brain volume in left amygdala (r=0.28, p=0.007) and left lateral OFC (r=0.29, p=0.004)
- **Conclusion: Potential role for VIP in neurobiology of affective symptoms**

## Source 14: PubMed — Gutzler et al (2025) — VIP COVID-19 Mechanism
- PMID: 40141308
- Int J Mol Sci. 2025 Mar 16;26(6):2666

### Key Findings
- In vitro: CaCo-2 cells stimulated with SARS-CoV-2 spike protein
- **VIP downregulated ACE2 and TMPRSS2 mRNA and surface expression**
- **VIP mediates shedding of ACE2 and TMPRSS2 via ADAM10 upregulation**
- Dual mechanisms reduced SARS-CoV-2 pseudovirus infection rate
- **Implication: VIP hampers viral entry mechanisms**

## Source 15: PubMed — Prasse et al (2010) — Inhaled VIP Sarcoidosis Phase II
- PMID: 20442436
- Am J Respir Crit Care Med. 2010 Aug 15;182(4):540-8

### Key Findings
- Open clinical phase II study; 20 patients with histologically proved sarcoidosis
- Nebulized VIP for 4 weeks
- **VIP inhalation was safe and well-tolerated**
- **Significantly reduced TNF-alpha production by BAL cells**
- **Significantly increased BAL CD4+CD127-CD25+ regulatory T cells**
- In vitro: VIP converted naive CD4+CD25- T cells into CD4+CD25+FoxP3+ Tregs
- **First demonstration of VIP immunoregulatory effect in humans**

## Source 18: PubMed — de Herder & Hofland (2023) — VIPoma

- VIPoma: neuroendocrine neoplasm secreting VIP
- Presents with severe watery secretory diarrhea → hypokalemia, metabolic acidosis, flushes

## Source 19: PubMed — Peng et al (2026) — SCN GABAergic Network
- PMID: 41802008
- PLoS Biol. 2026 Mar 9;24(3):e3003706

### Key Findings (Mouse Model)
- GABAergic regulation from AVP neurons (SCN shell) to VIP neurons (SCN core)
- Blocking GABA release from AVP neurons lengthened activity time
- Eliminating GABAAR in VIP neurons reduced morning locomotor activity
- Optogenetic activation of AVP neurons increased Ca2+ in VIP neurons during night
- **Tonic GABA-mediated inhibition of VIP neurons demonstrated**

## Source 20: PubMed — Wang et al (2026) — SCN Neuronal Feedback Loop
- PMID: 41486279
- Nat Commun. 2026 Jan 5;17(1):1505

### Key Findings (Mouse Model)
- In mice lacking VIP: AVP neurons oscillate intrinsically with short period and reduced amplitude
- **VIP normally amplifies and phase-delays AVP neuronal rhythm each day**
- Behavioral circadian period shortened by AVP neuron-specific VIP receptor dysfunction
- **SCN Neuronal Feedback Loop (SNFL): AVP cellular oscillator + VIPergic signaling essential for robust circadian rhythms**

## Source 21: PubMed — Rasmussen et al (2023) — Lu AG09222 Anti-PACAP mAb
- PMID: 37231350
- J Headache Pain. 2023 May 25;24(1):60

### Key Findings
- Lu AG09222 (anti-PACAP monoclonal antibody) inhibited PACAP38-induced cephalic vasodilation, heart rate increase, and headache
- **VIP was used as a control infusion** — Lu AG09222 did not block VIP effects (antibody is PACAP-specific)
- Demonstrates VIP and PACAP act through distinct pathways despite structural similarity
