## Key Studies (Chronological by Relevance)

### 1. Falutz et al. 2010 - Phase 3 Pivotal Trial
- PMID: 20101189
- Journal: JAIDS (2010)
- Title: "Effects of tesamorelin, a growth hormone-releasing factor, in HIV-infected patients with abdominal fat accumulation: a randomized placebo-controlled trial with a safety extension"
- DOI: 10.1097/QAI.0b013e3181cbdaff
- N=404 HIV patients with excess abdominal fat
- Design: 6-month randomized (2:1 tesamorelin:placebo) + 6-month extension (rerandomized)
- Dose: 2 mg SC daily
- Primary endpoint: VAT change
- Results:
  - VAT decreased -10.9% (-21 cm²) vs. -0.6% (-1 cm²) placebo at 6 months (P<0.0001)
  - Trunk fat, waist circumference, WHR improved
  - No change in limb or abdominal SC fat
  - IGF-1 increased (P<0.001), no glucose parameter changes
  - Belly appearance distress improved (P=0.02)
  - VAT reduced ~18% at 12 months continuous use
  - Rapid VAT reaccumulation on discontinuation
  - Drug well tolerated

### 2. Dhillon 2011 - Drugs Review
- PMID: 21668043
- Journal: Drugs (2011)
- Title: "Tesamorelin: a review of its use in the management of HIV-associated lipodystrophy"
- DOI: 10.2165/11202240-000000000-00000
- Key findings: Effective VAT reduction, no clinically significant SAT effect. Two 26-week trials. Reduction maintained to week 52 on continued therapy. Reaccumulation on discontinuation. Generally well tolerated; SAE <4%. Most AEs were injection site reactions or GH-associated (arthralgia, headache, peripheral edema).

### 3. Ellis et al. 2025 - Neurocognitive Trial
- PMID: 39813152
- Journal: J Infect Dis (2025)
- DOI: 10.1093/infdis/jiaf012
- Phase 2 open-label RCT: tesamorelin vs. standard of care for NCI in HIV+ with abdominal obesity
- N=73, randomized 3:2, 6 months
- Primary outcome: neurocognitive change at 6 months
- Results:
  - Tesamorelin group: trend toward improved NC (P=0.060) but NOT significant
  - SOC group: no improvement (P=0.295)
  - Between-group difference NOT significant (P=0.673)
  - WC reduced more in tesamorelin group (median -2.7 cm; P=0.015)
  - IGF-1 changes did not correlate with cognitive scores
- **CONCLUSION: No clear cognitive benefit from short-term tesamorelin**

### 4. Badran et al. 2026 - Meta-Analysis
- PMID: 41545261
- Journal: Obes Res Clin Pract (2026)
- DOI: 10.1016/j.orcp.2026.01.002
- 5 RCTs, random-effects meta-analysis
- Results:
  - VAT: MD=-27.71 cm² [95% CI -38.37, -17.06]; P<0.001
  - Trunk fat: MD=-1.18 kg [-1.40, -0.96]; P<0.001
  - Limb fat: MD=-0.22 kg [-0.35, -0.08]; P=0.001
  - Hepatic fat: MD=-4.28% [-6.31, -2.24]; P<0.001
  - Waist circumference: MD=-1.61 cm [-2.28, -0.95]; P<0.001
  - Lean body mass: MD=+1.42 kg [1.13, 1.71]; P<0.001
  - No significant SAT or BMI change
  - No CD4+ T-cell count change
  - AEs: arthralgia, myalgia, paresthesia, injection site reactions
  - No glucose perturbation

### 5. Stanley et al. 2021 - Immune Activation in NAFLD
- PMID: 33852720
- Journal: Clin Infect Dis (2021)
- DOI: 10.1093/cid/ciab019
- NCT02196831 trial data
- N=61 PWH with NAFLD, double-blind RCT 12 months tesamorelin vs. placebo
- 92 biomarkers measured
- Results: Tesamorelin decreased 13 interconnected proteins related to cytotoxic T-cell and monocyte activation:
  - Chemokines: CCL3, CCL4, CCL13, IL8
  - Cytokines: IL-10, CSF-1
  - T-cell: CD8A, CRTAM, GZMA, ADGRG1
  - Others: ARG1, Gal-9, HGF
- Liver biopsy confirmed down-regulated immune activation pathways
- **Conclusion: GHRH analog reduces T-cell/monocyte immune activation in HIV+ with metabolic dysregulation**

### 6. Fourman et al. 2021 - Proteomic/Transcriptomic NAFLD
- PMID: 34006921
- Journal: Sci Rep (2021)
- DOI: 10.1038/s41598-021-89966-y
- Tesamorelin reduced: VEGFA (-0.20 ± 0.35 vs +0.05 ± 0.34 placebo, P=0.02), TGFB1 (-0.35 ± 0.56 vs -0.05 ± 0.43, P=0.05), CSF1 (-0.17 ± 0.21 vs +0.02 ± 0.20, P=0.004)
- Reductions in VEGFA and CSF1 correlated with decline in NAFLD activity score
- Reductions in TGFB1 and CSF1 associated with reduced fibrosis score
- **Conclusion: Suppresses angiogenic, fibrogenic, and pro-inflammatory mediators**

### 7. Fourman/Stanley 2024 - INSTI-era Efficacy
- PMID: 38905488
- Journal: AIDS (2024)
- DOI: 10.1097/QAD.0000000000003965
- Tested efficacy/safety of tesamorelin in PWH on integrase inhibitors (INSTIs)
- Phase 3 trials predated INSTI introduction

### 8. Fourman et al. 2021 - Fat Quality
- PMID: 33756511
- Journal: AIDS (2021)
- DOI: 10.1097/QAD.0000000000002897
- Tesamorelin improves fat quality (density on CT) independent of fat quantity changes
- Greater fat density = smaller, higher quality adipocytes
