# CJC-1295 (with DAC) — Research Compilation

## What It Is

CJC-1295 DAC (also DAC:GRF, drug affinity complex:growth hormone-releasing factor) is a synthetic analogue of GHRH developed by ConjuChem Biotechnologies. It is a tetrasubstituted form of GHRH(1-29) with an added Nε-maleimidopropionamide derivative of lysine at the C-terminus (the "DAC" moiety). The maleimide group reacts with the free thiol on Cys34 of serum albumin, covalently binding the peptide to albumin and dramatically extending its half-life from minutes to days.

**CJC-1295 DAC is NOT the same as Modified GRF (1-29) / "CJC-1295 without DAC"** — several scientific papers falsely equate the two.

**Sequence (IUPAC):** Nε30-maleimidopropionyl-[D-Ala2, Gln8, Ala15, Leu27]-Sermorelin-Lys30

**Molecular formula:** C₁₆₅H₂₆₉N₄₇O₄₆
**Molar mass:** 3647.250 g/mol
**CAS:** 446262-90-4
**UNII:** 62RC32V9N7

## Key Papers

### 1. Jetté et al. 2005 — Rat identification study (PMID 15817669)
- *Endocrinology* 2005;146(7):3052-8
- Three maleimido derivatives of hGRF(1-29) synthesized and bioconjugated to human serum albumin ex vivo
- All three conjugates: enhanced in vitro stability against DPP-IV and bioactivity in cultured rat anterior pituitary cells
- CJC-1295 selected as best compound: 4-fold increase in GH AUC over 2h vs hGRF(1-29) in rats
- Present in rat plasma beyond 72h
- Western blot confirmed CJC-1295-albumin conjugate appeared at 15 min and remained >24h

### 2. Teichman et al. 2006 — Human PK/PD Phase I (PMID 16352683)
- *J Clin Endocrinol Metab* 2006;91(3):799-805
- Two randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind, ascending-dose trials
- Single injection → GH 2-10x for ≥6 days; IGF-1 1.5-3x for 9-11 days
- t1/2: 5.8-8.1 days
- Multiple doses: IGF-1 elevated up to 28 days
- No serious adverse reactions at 30 or 60 µg/kg
- Evidence of cumulative effect with multiple doses

### 3. Alba et al. 2006 — GHRH knockout mouse (PMID 16822960)
- *Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab* 2006;291(6):E1290-4
- GHRH knockout mice treated for 5 weeks
- Daily dosing: normalized body weight, length, femur/tibia length
- 48h and 72h dosing: improved but not fully normalized
- Increased pituitary RNA, GH mRNA, somatotroph proliferation (IHC)

### 4. Ionescu & Frohman 2006 — GH pulsatility in humans (PMID 17018654)
- *J Clin Endocrinol Metab* 2006;91(12):4792-7
- GH pulsatility *preserved* during continuous CJC-1295 stimulation
- Basal (trough) GH increased 7.5-fold (P<0.0001)
- Mean GH +46% (P<0.01), IGF-1 +45% (P<0.001)
- Continuous GHRH stimulation increases trough GH without abolishing pulses

### 5. Sackmann-Sala et al. 2009 — Proteomic changes (PMID 19386527)
- *Growth Horm IGF Res* 2009;19(6):471-7
- 11 healthy men, serum proteomics before/after CJC-1295
- Downregulated: ApoA1 isoform, transthyretin isoform
- Upregulated: β-hemoglobin, C-terminal albumin fragments
- Potential biomarkers for GH/IGF-1 action

### 6. Henninge et al. 2010 — Doping identification (PMID 21204297)
- *Drug Test Anal* 2010;2(11-12):647-50
- Norwegian customs seizure: unknown pharmaceutical preparation containing CJC-1295
- Identified by LC-HRMS/MS
- Already available on gray market before completing clinical trials
- WADA S2 prohibited

### 7. Van Hout & Hearne 2016 — Netnography (PMID 26771670)
- *Subst Use Misuse* 2016;51(1):73-84
- 9 bodybuilding forums, 23 female discussion threads
- Uses: weight loss, muscle enhancement, youthful skin, improved sleep, injury healing
- Concerns: gender-specific GH pulsatility, dosing estimation, cycling, long-term consequences

## Phase II Trial Termination (NCT00267527)

**Trial:** Phase II, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
**Sponsor:** ConjuChem Biotechnologies
**Indication:** HIV-associated visceral obesity
**Enrollment:** 192 patients
**Dosing:** Once-weekly CJC-1295 for 12 weeks + 6-week follow-up
**Date:** December 2005 – September 2006

**The Death:**
- Patient received 11th weekly dose on July 13, 2006
- ~2 hours later: chest discomfort, ECG confirmed acute MI
- Death ~3 hours post-injection
- Attending physician: "most likely explanation was the patient had asymptomatic coronary artery disease with plaque rupture and occlusion"
- Physician stated death was unrelated to CJC-1295 treatment
- ConjuChem terminated the Phase II trial as a precaution
- Source: ConjuChem press release Aug 8, 2006 (archived at natap.org)

**Result:** The CJC-1295 clinical development program was permanently discontinued. No further clinical trials have been conducted.

## WADA / Anti-Doping Status

CJC-1295 is prohibited under **WADA Section S2** (Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances, and Mimetics). It is banned both in-competition and out-of-competition.

Detection methods established:
- Immunoaffinity purification + LC-HRMS/MS (Knoop et al. 2016, PMID 26879649)
- Immuno-PCR (Timms et al. 2019, PMID 30489688) — required because DAC albumin conjugation masks the peptide from conventional MS
- LC-MS/MS after immunoaffinity capture + tryptic digestion (Timms et al. 2019, PMID 30938069)
- UF + nanoLC-HRMS/MS (Coppieters et al. 2022, PMID 35298973)
- SPE + UHPLC-MS/MS (Cristea et al. 2023, PMID 37806509)
- nanoLC-Q/Orbitrap MS (Uçaktürk et al. 2026, PMID 41138283)

## 2026 Review Consensus

Multiple 2026 reviews (Mayfield PMID 41476424, Mendias PMID 41966639, Rahman PMID 41490200, Villegas Meza PMID 42160466, Coutinho PMID 41880199, Mavrych PMID 42021992) unanimously:
- Classify CJC-1295 as investigational/experimental
- No rigorous human trials for musculoskeletal/sports medicine
- Uncertain safety profiles
- Gray market sourcing concerns (mislabeling, contamination)
- WADA prohibited
