# Ipamorelin — Supplemental Raw Sources

Papers referenced in the entity page but not captured in the primary Wikipedia or PubMed raw files. Summaries compiled from PubMed/abstracts.

## Ahnfelt-Rønne et al., 2001
- Title: "Growth hormone secretagogues increase gastric ghrelin release"
- Findings: Animal data suggest ipamorelin may stimulate endogenous ghrelin release from gastric tissue.
- Note: Mechanistic hypothesis; not confirmed in humans.

## Aagaard et al., 2009
- Title: Ipamorelin effects on nitrogen balance in prednisolone-treated rats
- Findings: Ipamorelin (0.5 mg/kg/day) reduced prednisolone-induced urea synthesis by ~20%, improved nitrogen balance, normalized organ nitrogen content — similar but weaker than direct GH.
- PMID: 19231263

## Greenwood-Van Meerveld et al., 2012
- Title: Ipamorelin accelerates gastric emptying post-surgery via cholinergic excitatory neurons
- Findings: Ipamorelin (0.014 µmol/kg IV) reduced gastric retention from 78% to 52% in postoperative rat model.
- PMID: 27186127

## Lu et al., 2024
- Title: Growth hormone secretagogue receptor 1a agonists inhibit cisplatin-induced weight loss in ferrets
- Findings: Ipamorelin (1–3 mg/kg IP) inhibited cisplatin-induced weight loss (~24% reduction in delayed phase), but did not affect emesis.
- PMID: 39043357

## Gouda & Ganesh, 2024
- Title: Ipamorelin effects on hypothalamic-pituitary-testicular axis in tilapia
- Findings: Ipamorelin increased spermatogenesis, LH, and 11-ketotestosterone in Oreochromis mossambicus.
- PMID: 38996787

## Krug et al., 2018
- Title: Characterization of seized doping products — ipamorelin contaminants
- Findings: Gray-market ipamorelin products contained unknown contaminants and modified analogs.
- Note: Cited in safety section for product quality concerns.
