# GHRP-2 and GHRP-6 Research Sources
## Compiled: 2026-06-09

### Wikipedia - Pralmorelin (GHRP-2)
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pralmorelin
- Pralmorelin (INN; brand name GHRP Kaken 100; codes KP-102, GPA-748, WAY-GPA-748) is a growth hormone secretagogue used as a diagnostic agent marketed by Kaken Pharmaceutical in Japan for assessment of GH deficiency.
- Orally-active, synthetic hexapeptide, analogue of met-enkephalin
- Sequence: D-Ala-D-(β-naphthyl)-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH2
- CAS: 158861-67-7, Formula: C45H55N9O6, MW: 817.99 g/mol
- GHSR (ghrelin receptor) agonist — first of this class introduced clinically
- Acute administration markedly increases plasma GH and reliably induces hunger/increases food intake
- Was under investigation for GHD and short stature (Phase II) but never marketed for these indications
- May be because GH response is significantly lower in GHD patients vs healthy individuals
- Routes: Oral, intravenous

### Wikipedia - GHRP-6
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GHRP-6
- Growth hormone-releasing peptide 6 (developmental code SKF-110679), also known as growth hormone-releasing hexapeptide
- Synthetic met-enkephalin analogue with unnatural D-amino acids
- Lacks opioid activity but potent GH release stimulator
- Distinct from GHRH — no sequence relation, acts through different receptor (GHSR, now ghrelin receptor)
- Sequence: His-D-Trp-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH2
- CAS: 87616-84-0, Formula: C46H56N12O6, MW: 873.03 g/mol
- Oral bioavailability: <1.0%, Elimination half-life: 2.5 hours
- When GHRP-6 and insulin administered simultaneously, GH response increased
- Carbohydrate/fat consumption around administration blunts GH release
- Mouse study showed differences in body composition, muscle growth, glucose metabolism, memory, cardiac function
- Article flagged for lacking inline citations and disputed factual accuracy (2013)

### PubMed - GHRP-2 Key Papers

**PMID: 15230633 (2004)** - Drugs in R&D
- Pralmorelin overview: Developed by Polygen (Germany) and Tulane University (Dr Cyril Bowers)
- Kaken acquired worldwide rights, sublicensed to Wyeth for US/Canada
- Awaiting approval in Japan as diagnostic agent (2004), planned launch for short stature 2009
- Phase II trials in US for GH deficiency with Wyeth — development discontinued
- Tulane University US Patent 6,468,974 (October 2002)

**PMID: 15646371 (2004)** - Arzneimittel-Forschung
- General pharmacology of KP-102 (GHRP-2) in animal models
- No obvious CNS effects at pharmacological doses
- No significant effect on autonomic nervous system and smooth muscle (except slight transient ileum effects at high doses)
- Negligible effect on respiratory, cardiovascular, digestive, renal, blood systems
- Conclusion: no serious general pharmacological effects at GH-releasing doses

**PMID: 38958228 (2024)** - J Pediatr Endocrinol Metab
- Robust GH responses to GHRP-2 in adolescents
- GHRP-2 used for provocative GH testing; cut-off peak GH level higher than other GHT
- Data on adolescent response previously limited

**PMID: 37295337 (2023)** - Growth Horm IGF Res
- Assessment of anterior pituitary reserve capacity via GHRP-2 test in elderly
- GHRP-2 test relatively safe among endocrine stimulation tests for elderly

**PMID: 35795807 (2022)** - J Endocrine Society
- Clinical usefulness of GHRP-2 test for hypothalamic-pituitary disorder
- GHD develops early in hypothalamic-pituitary disorder, frequently accompanied by other deficiencies including secondary AI
- GHRP-2 test thought to induce release of both GH and ACTH

**PMID: 39672241 (2025)** - Arthroscopy
- GHRP-2 may be associated with decreased M1 macrophage production and increased histologic/biomechanical tendon-bone healing in rat rotator cuff tear model

**PMID: 33197153 (2021)** - Drug Test Anal
- Dried blood spot sampling for anti-doping detection of GHRP-2
- GHRP-2 detected up to 4h after single IV dose (100 μg)
- Method validated per WADA guidelines; LoD 50 pg/ml
- Long-term stability (>2 years) confirmed in DBS

### PubMed - GHRP-6 Key Papers

**PMID: 1592884 (1992)** - J Clin Endocrinol Metab
- Hartman ML et al. Oral administration of GHRP stimulates GH secretion in normal men
- 10 normal men, oral doses 30/100/300 μg/kg, IV 1 μg/kg
- Dose-dependent GH stimulation; 300 μg/kg oral comparable to 1 μg/kg IV
- Peak GH: 18 ± 3.7 μg/L (300 μg/kg oral) vs 4.0 ± 1.5 (placebo)
- GH increase entirely via amplitude increase, not pulse frequency
- Duration of response: 120-150 min

**PMID: 8421084 (1993)** - J Clin Endocrinol Metab
- Peñalva A et al. Effect of GHRH, atropine, pyridostigmine, or hypoglycemia on GHRP-6-induced GH secretion in man
- GHRP-6 + GHRH: synergistic (AUC 4412 vs 973 GHRP-6 alone)
- Atropine (1 mg IM) completely prevented GH response to GHRP-6
- Pyridostigmine (cholinergic agonist) slightly increased GH response
- GHRP-6 + insulin-induced hypoglycemia: much greater GH increase
- Conclusion: GHRP-6 acts through non-GHRH-dependent mechanism; dependent on somatostatinergic tone but not mediated by change in hypothalamic somatostatin

**PMID: 23099431 (2013)** - Eur J Pharm Sci
- Cabrales A et al. Pharmacokinetic study of GHRP-6 in 9 male healthy volunteers
- IV bolus: 100, 200, 400 μg/kg
- Bi-exponential disposition: distribution t1/2 = 7.6 ± 1.9 min, elimination t1/2 = 2.5 ± 1.1 h
- AUC increased proportionally with dose
- Atypical concentration spikes during elimination phase in 4/9 subjects
- LLQ 5 ng/mL reached at 12h post-administration

**PMID: 38638315 (2024)** - Front Neurol
- Hernández-Bernal F et al. Phase I/II non-blinded randomized clinical trial
- Combination EGF + GHRP-6 for acute ischemic stroke
- Safety endpoint: ≤30% SAE with proven causality
- This is the most advanced human clinical trial for GHRP-6

**PMID: 39813130 (2025)** - Endocrinology
- Poelman R et al. Intranasal delivery of ghrelin mimetic engages brain ghrelin signaling in mice
- Only GHRP-6 (5 mg/kg intranasal) increased food intake without adverse effects
- Activated arcuate nucleus neurons: 63.5% Ghsr+, 79% Agrp+, 11.4% Ghrh+
- Intranasal GHRP-6 elevated serum GH levels
- Ghrelin and MK-0677 did not work intranasally

**PMID: 41534456 (2026)** - Int Immunopharmacol
- GHRP-6 ameliorates acute lung injury and interstitial fibrosis in mouse models
- Reduced neutrophilic alveolitis, improved lung compliance, reduced IL-1β
- Chronic scenario: preserved lung parenchymal integrity, reduced collagen accumulation

**PMID: 41327290 (2025)** - J Nanobiotechnology
- GHRP-6 hydrogel for AKI therapy via metabolic regulation in mice
- Activated mTOR-P70 pathway, enhanced TEC survival
- Metabolomic enrichment: spermidine, L-glutamine, acetyl-CoA

**PMID: 32454141 (2020)** - Physiol Behav
- D-Lys-3-GHRP-6 (ghrelin antagonist) impairs memory consolidation in rats
- Downregulates hippocampal serotonin HT1A, HT7 receptors and glutamate GluA1 subunit

**PMID: 32084499 (2020)** - Mol Cell Endocrinol
- miR-709 inhibits GHRP-6 induced GH synthesis by targeting PRKCA in pituitary cells

### Additional Context
- Both GHRP-2 and GHRP-6 are WADA-prohibited substances (Class S2)
- GHRP-2 is approved in Japan as diagnostic agent (GHRP Kaken 100) but not for therapeutic use
- GHRP-6 has no regulatory approval in any jurisdiction
- Both are sold as "research chemicals" in gray markets
- GHRP-2 has oral activity; GHRP-6 oral bioavailability <1%
- Key difference: GHRP-2 stimulates ACTH/cortisol; GHRP-6 has stronger appetite effects
- Both act through GHS-R1a (ghrelin receptor), distinct from GHRH receptor
