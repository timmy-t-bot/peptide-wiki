# Thymosin Beta-4 (TB-500) Research Compilation

## Wikipedia: Thymosin beta-4

Thymosin beta-4 is a 43-amino-acid protein (SDKPDMAEI EKFDKSKLKK TETQEKNPLP SKETIEQEKQ AGES, MW 4921 g/mol) encoded by the TMSB4X gene on the X chromosome. It is the principal actin-sequestering protein in many cell types, forming a 1:1 complex with G-actin to regulate actin polymerization. Intracellular concentration may reach 0.5 mM.

The INN for thymosin beta-4 is "timbetasin" (WHO). It was initially perceived as a thymic hormone but was later found to be widely distributed in mammalian cells.

## Wikipedia: TB-500

TB-500 (Ac-LKKTETQ, CAS 885340-08-9, MW 889 g/mol, C38H68N10O14) is a synthetic heptapeptide corresponding to the N-acetylated active fragment (amino acids 17-23) of thymosin beta-4. The sequence LKKTET is the conserved actin-binding motif. TB-500 is claimed to increase muscle growth, accelerate wound healing and tissue repair, reduce inflammation, and improve flexibility. It has been encountered as a designer drug mainly in racehorses and is prohibited by WADA. Classified as prescription medicine in Australia and New Zealand.

## Wikipedia: Ac-SDKP (Goralatide)

N-acetyl-Ser-Asp-Lys-Pro, tetrapeptide from Tβ4 amino acids 1-4, cleaved by prolyl oligopeptidase. Inhibits hematopoietic stem cell proliferation, anti-inflammatory, anti-fibrotic, pro-angiogenic. Applications in kidney and cardiac fibrosis prevention.

## PubMed: Clinical Trial Results Summary

Search "thymosin beta-4" AND (clinical trial OR human): ~600+ publications. Key clinical results:

1. **Phase 2 Dry Eye Trial**: "Thymosin beta4 significantly improves signs and symptoms of severe dry eye in a phase 2 randomized trial" (PMID ~25826322). Effects lasted long after treatment ended.

2. **Phase 2 Dermal Healing**: "The regenerative peptide thymosin beta4 accelerates the rate of dermal healing in preclinical animal models and in patients." Studied in pressure ulcers, venous stasis ulcers, and epidermolysis bullosa. Safe and well-tolerated.

3. **Phase I Healthy Volunteers**: "A first-in-human, randomized, double-blind, single- and multiple-dose, phase I study of recombinant human thymosin beta4 in healthy Chinese volunteers" (2021). Safety and pharmacokinetics established in healthy volunteers.

4. **STEMI Pilot**: "Safety and efficacy of autologous thymosin beta4 pre-treated endothelial progenitor cell transplantation in patients with acute ST segment elevation myocardial infarction" — pilot study.

5. **Heart Failure**: "Increases in plasma Tβ4 after intracardiac cell therapy in chronic ischemic heart failure is associated with symptomatic improvement."

6. **Corneal Wound Healing**: "Engineered Tandem Thymosin Peptide Promotes Corneal Wound Healing" (2025).

7. **Cardioprotection Trial Proposal**: "Use of the cardioprotectants thymosin beta4 and dexrazoxane during congenital heart surgery: proposal for a randomized, double-blind, clinical trial."

8. **TB-500 Analysis Paper**: "Synthesis and characterization of the N-terminal acetylated 17-23 fragment of thymosin beta 4 identified in TB-500, a product suspected to possess doping potential" (Drug Test Anal. 2012 Sep;4(9):733-8).

9. **Sepsis**: "Thymosin beta 4 regulation of actin in sepsis" — role in cytoskeletal regulation during sepsis.

## Mechanism of Action (from Wikipedia)

### Actin Binding
Forms 1:1 complex with G-actin, preventing polymerization to F-actin. Functions as a buffer for monomeric actin. Release of G-actin drives cytoskeletal dynamics in cell morphology and motility.

### Tissue Regeneration
Administered Tβ4 promotes: cell migration, blood vessel formation (angiogenesis), stem cell maturation, cell survival, and lowering of pro-inflammatory cytokines. In mice, stimulates new heart muscle cell formation from epicardial precursor cells.

### Anti-inflammatory via Sulfoxide
Oxidized Tβ4 (sulfoxide at methionine) exerts anti-inflammatory effects on neutrophils: promotes dispersion from foci, inhibits chemotactic response to f-Met-Leu-Phe, and lowers adhesion to endothelial cells.

### Terminal Deoxynucleotidyl Transferase
Induces TdT activity in thymocyte populations, suggesting a role in lymphocyte maturation.

### Moonlighting
Diverse extracellular effects unlikely to all be mediated by intracellular actin sequestration. Candidate extracellular receptor: β subunit of cell surface ATP synthase, allowing signaling via purinergic receptors.

## Doping in Sports

WADA banned. Central to two Australian doping scandals: Cronulla-Sutherland Sharks (NRL) and Essendon Football Club (AFL). Sports scientist Stephen Dank administered thymosin beta-4 to players. Both clubs had players found guilty of doping and suspended.

## Key Distinction: Tβ4 vs TB-500

- **Tβ4 (Thymosin Beta-4)**: Full 43-amino-acid endogenous protein, MW ~5 kDa. The actual biological molecule studied in clinical trials.
- **TB-500**: Synthetic 7-amino-acid fragment (17-23), MW ~0.9 kDa. Not the compound studied in clinical trials. A truncated synthetic analog sold in research chemical / gray markets.
- **Ac-SDKP** (Goralatide): N-terminal 1-4 fragment, endogenous cleavage product with distinct pharmacology.

The clinical evidence for Tβ4 does NOT directly apply to TB-500. They are different molecules with different sizes, potentially different receptor interactions, and different pharmacokinetics.
