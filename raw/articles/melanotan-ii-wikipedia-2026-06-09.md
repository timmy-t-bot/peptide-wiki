# Melanotan II — Wikipedia

Melanotan II is a synthetic analogue of the peptide hormone α-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (α-MSH) that stimulates melanogenesis to facilitate tanning. It may also increase sexual arousal.

## Synthesis
- Developed as a successor to afamelanotide ("Melanotan I"), an FDA approved drug operating through a similar pathway.
- Clinuvel Pharmaceuticals intended to offer it as a cosmetic, but abandoned this pursuit in the 2000s due to regulatory restrictions.
- Unlicensed Melanotan II is found on the internet; health agencies advise against its use.

## Mechanism of action
- Non-selective agonist of melanocortin receptors MC1, MC3, MC4, and MC5 (Wikberg 2001).
- Melanogenesis via MC1 receptor activation.
- Clinically documented sexual effects thought to be related to MC4 receptor activation (Norris & Lopez 2010; King 2007).
- Adverse effects: flushing, nausea, vomiting, stretching, yawning, loss of appetite via MC4 activation (Plant & Zeleznik 2014; Wein 2011).

## History and research
- Early 1960s: α-MSH caused sexual arousal in rats.
- University of Arizona scientists developed α-MSH analogs as sunless tanning agents, including melanotan-I and melanotan II.
- One scientist injected himself with twice the intended dose and got an eight-hour erection, with nausea and vomiting.
- Afamelanotide (melanotan I) was licensed to Epitan/Clinuvel and FDA approved in 2019.
- As a sexual dysfunction agent, melanotan II was licensed to Palatin Technologies. Palatin ceased development of melanotan II in 2000 and began developing bremelanotide, a likely metabolite of melanotan II with a carboxy group instead of an amide.
- Competitive Technologies sued Palatin; settled 2008 with Palatin retaining bremelanotide rights.

## Safety
- Reversible darkening of moles and freckles.
- Unclear if it increases or reduces melanoma risk; reports of melanomas associated with use have coincided with heavy UV exposure.
- 2013 review: no conclusive evidence it causes melanoma (Javed 2013).
- 2021 review: increased risk in Melanotan users probably explained by more UV exposure (Wensink 2021).
- 2020 in vivo study: Melanotan II suppressed melanoma progression via PTEN upregulation and COX-II inhibition (Wu 2020).
- Side effects: facial flushing, nausea, erection in males.

## Society and culture
- Products sold online and in gyms/beauty salons as "melanotan" or "melanotan-2".
- Unregulated products are not legal to be sold for human usage in any jurisdiction.
- Health agencies in various countries began issuing warnings in 2007 (Danish Medicines Agency, MHRA, FDA, Irish Medicines Board, Norwegian Medicines Agency).
