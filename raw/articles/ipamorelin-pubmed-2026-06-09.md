## Key Papers Summarized

### Foundational Pharmacology
- Raun et al. (1998) - Ipamorelin, the first selective growth hormone secretagogue (PMID: 9849822)
  - Pentapeptide: Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2
  - Highly selective for GH release; does NOT increase ACTH/cortisol or prolactin
  - Selectivity maintained at doses >200-fold ED50 for GH release
  - Developed by Novo Nordisk

- Gobburu et al. (1999) - PK/PD modeling in human volunteers (PMID: 10496658)
  - Half-life: ~2 hours
  - Clearance: 0.078 L/h/kg
  - Vd(ss): 0.22 L/kg
  - Dose-proportional PK; SC50 for GH stimulation: 214 nmol/L
  - Single GH release episode peaking at 0.67 hrs

### Phase II Clinical Trial (Postoperative Ileus)
- Beck et al. (2014) - Proof-of-concept Phase II RCT (PMID: 25331030)
  - NCT00672074, Helsinn Therapeutics
  - 117 patients, double-blind, placebo-controlled
  - Ipamorelin 0.03 mg/kg IV twice daily for up to 7 days
  - Primary endpoint: time to first tolerated meal (25.3h ipamorelin vs 32.6h placebo, p=0.15 - NOT significant)
  - Well tolerated: AEs 87.5% ipamorelin vs 94.8% placebo
  - CONCLUSION: No significant efficacy; program discontinued

### Animal Studies - Bone & Body Weight
- Svensson et al. (2000) - GHSs increase bone mineral content in rats (PMID: 10828840)
  - Ipamorelin & GHRP-6 increased total BMC (DXA) and cross-sectional bone area
  - Volumetric BMD unchanged; bone dimensions increased
  
- Johansen et al. (1999) - Longitudinal bone growth in rats (PMID: 10373343)
  - Dose-dependent LGR increase: 42→52 µm/day
  - Body weight gain increased dose-dependently
  - No change in IGF-I or bone turnover markers

- Andersen et al. (2001) - Counteracts glucocorticoid bone loss (PMID: 11735244)
  - Ipamorelin + methylprednisolone vs steroid alone
  - 4-fold increase in periosteal bone formation rate
  - Increased maximum tetanic tension of calf muscles

- Jiménez-Reina et al. (2002) - Chronic treatment in young female rats (PMID: 12168778)
  - 21-day treatment: increased body weight, somatotroph GH content
  - Dynamic control effect on somatotroph population

### Animal Studies - Other
- Lall et al. (2001) - GH-independent stimulation of adiposity (PMID: 11162489)
  - Ipamorelin increased body fat by GH-INDEPENDENT mechanisms
  - Increased leptin and food intake
  - Important: GHSs can increase adiposity through non-GH pathways

- Adeghate & Ponery (2004) - Insulin release from pancreas (PMID: 15665799)
  - Ipamorelin stimulates insulin secretion from rat pancreas
  - Via calcium channel & adrenergic receptor pathways

- Malmlöf et al. (1999) - Counteracts methylprednisolone catabolism (PMID: 10629165)
  - MP does not disrupt GH response to ipamorelin
  - Repeated IPA dosing increased IGF-I, body weight gain

- Johansen et al. (2003) - GH hypersecretion in STZ diabetic mice (PMID: 14630569)
  - Diabetic mice showed exaggerated GH response to ipamorelin
  - GH resistance and low IGF-I in diabetic animals

- Johansen et al. (1998) - PK and nasal absorption (PMID: 9879640)
  - Systemic clearance 5x lower than GHRP-6
  - Nasal bioavailability ~20%
  - 60-80% excreted intact in urine/bile

### 2026 Review Papers
- Villegas Meza et al. (2026) - Injectable Peptides in Sports Medicine (PMID: 42160466)
  - Growth hormone axis secretagogues (CJC-1295, ipamorelin, tesamorelin) remain investigational
  - Uncertain safety profiles, product quality concerns, widespread antidoping restrictions
  - Level V evidence; predominantly Grade C recommendation

- Mendias & Awan (2026) - Safety and Efficacy for Musculoskeletal Injuries (PMID: 41966639)
  - Gray market compounds, no rigorous human safety data
  - Animal model results not validated in humans

- Mayfield et al. (2026) - Primer for Orthopaedic Physicians (PMID: 41476424)
  - CJC-1295 + ipamorelin improved tetanic tension in murine models only
  - "Current lack of evidence to support clinical use"

- Rahman et al. (2026) - Therapeutic Peptides in Orthopaedics (PMID: 41490200)
  - Activates IGF-1 signaling and satellite cell repair
  - Lack of clinical trials

- Renke & Chinellato (2026) - Therapeutic Peptides Review (PMID: 42123471)
  - Further studies needed before safe human use

- Mavrych et al. (2026) - Peptides in Gerontology (PMID: 42021992)
  - Non-approved peptides: promising preclinical but lack long-term safety

- Coutinho et al. (2026) - Doping with Peptides (PMID: 41880199)
  - Limited clinical evidence for sport use
  - Unregulated supply chain, contamination risks

### Doping Control / Anti-Doping
- Gajda et al. (2019) - Glycine-modified GHS analogs in seized doping materials (PMID: 30136411)
- Semenistaya et al. (2015) - Metabolites after nasal administration (PMID: 25869809)
  - Ipamorelin metabolized intensively; Ipamorelin (1-4) free acid is key metabolite
- Multiple papers on LC-MS detection methods for anti-doping (several PMIDs)
- WADA prohibited substance; athletes suspended (Jodie Meeks - NBA 2018, Chandler - NBA 2019)

### ClinicalTrials.gov
- NCT00672074: Phase II, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 117 patients, 2008-2009
  - Sponsor: Helsinn Therapeutics
  - Discontinued due to lack of efficacy
