# Cathelicidin / LL-37 — Wikipedia

Cathelicidin antimicrobial peptide (CAMP) is an antimicrobial peptide encoded in humans by the CAMP gene. The active form is LL-37, a 37 amino acid peptide with sequence LLGDFFRKSKEKIGKEFKRIVQRIKDFLRNLVPRTES.

## Structure and processing
- In humans, CAMP encodes the peptide precursor CAP-18 (18 kDa), processed by proteinase 3-mediated extracellular cleavage into active LL-37.
- The cathelicidin family includes 30 types; LL-37 is the only cathelicidin in humans.
- Stored in secretory granules of neutrophils and macrophages; released after activation.
- Amphiphilic molecules: one end hydrophilic, one end lipophilic.

## Mechanism of antimicrobial activity
- Disintegrates, damages, or punctures cell membranes of pathogens.
- Rapidly destroys lipoprotein membranes of microbes in phagosomes after fusion with lysosomes.
- Inhibits formation of bacterial biofilms.

## Other activities (pleiotropic effects)
- Cell proliferation and migration, contributing to wound closure (Shaykhiev 2005).
- Agonistic effect on FPRL-1 (formyl peptide receptor-like 1), purinergic receptor P2X7, EGFR (Chen 2000; von Haussen 2008).
- Induces angiogenesis (Koczulla 2003).
- Regulates apoptosis (Ren 2013).
- Lung cancer cell growth factor (von Haussen 2008).

## Cleavage products
LL-37 is cleaved into smaller fragments with retained antimicrobial/anticancer effects:
- RK-31 (7-37), KS-30 (8-37), KR-20 (18-37), FK-13 (17-29), FK-16 (17-32), GF-17 (16-32), GI-20 (12-32).

## Clinical significance
- Rosacea: patients have elevated cathelicidin and stratum corneum tryptic enzymes (SCTEs). Excessive LL-37 production suspected contributing cause.
- Dialysis: lower plasma hCAP18 significantly increases risk of death from infection.
- Psoriasis: LL-37 forms complexes with self-DNA/RNA, stimulates dendritic cells, releasing interferon α/β, contributing to inflammation. Common auto-antigen in psoriasis.
- Alzheimer's disease: LL-37 binds amyloid-β peptide.

## Research applications
- Drug candidates from AMPs have narrow bioavailability window (rapid protease breakdown).
- SAAP-148: modified LL-37 with enhanced antimicrobial activities (de Breij 2018).
