# BPC-157 Research Update — June 2026

## Key New Findings Since Last Update (January 2025)

### Human Evidence — MODEST but GROWING

**IV Safety Pilot (Lee & Burgess, Sep 2025)**:
- First study of IV BPC-157 in humans
- 2 participants: 58M Asian, 68F Caucasian
- 10mg then 20mg in 250cc NS over 1 hour
- No adverse effects; no measurable impact on heart, liver, kidney, thyroid, glucose biomarkers
- Conclusion: "well-tolerated" but n=2 limits any conclusions

**Interstitial Cystitis Pilot (Lee, Walker, Ayadi, Oct 2024)**:
- 12 women, age 39-76 (mean 58.3)
- 10mg BPC-157 injected around bladder inflammation via cystoscopy
- 10/12 patients: 100% symptom resolution
- 2/12: 80% improvement
- No dropouts, no adverse events
- All scored 5/5 on Global Response Assessment

**Known earlier human pilot (knee OA, intraarticular)** — cited in reviews but not newly published

**Summary of all human data as of mid-2026**:
- <30 total subjects across 3 uncontrolled pilot studies
- No serious adverse events reported
- No RCTs, no Phase II trials
- Pharmacokinetic half-life <30 min confirmed in a preliminary 2-subject human analysis

### Critical Biopharmaceutical Assessment (Mateescu et al., May 2026)
- BPC-157 is "pharmaceutically underdeveloped"
- No pharmaceutical-grade formulation exists or validated
- No BCS classification, no permeability data, no excipient compatibility studies
- The primary barrier is not lack of biological activity but absence of basic pharmaceutical science
- Half-life <30 min contrasts with prolonged effects (hours-days) — a PK/PD disconnect

### First Human Tissue Evidence (Yildirim et al., May 2026)
- BPC-157 induced concentration-dependent vasorelaxation in human internal mammary artery rings
- Effect is endothelium-dependent and NO-mediated
- NOS inhibitor L-NAME attenuated the effect
- First direct functional evidence of BPC-157 activity in human vascular tissue

### Formal PK/ADME Study (He et al., Dec 2022)
- First comprehensive PK study: rats and beagle dogs
- t½ <30 min after IV/IM; linear dose-proportional kinetics
- Absolute IM bioavailability: 14-19% (rat), 45-51% (dog)
- Rapidly metabolized to small peptide fragments → single amino acids
- Excreted via urine and bile

### WADA Status
- BPC-157 was specifically banned by WADA in 2022 under S0 (non-approved substances)
- 2025 Prohibited List: S0 remains a catch-all category for any pharmacological substance not approved by any governmental regulatory authority for human therapeutic use
- BPC-157 is not explicitly named in the 2025 list but falls under S0

### Angiogenesis Controversy Refined
- Sikiric group (Sep 2025, PMID 41155565) argues BPC-157 does NOT simply "promote angiogenesis" in an undifferentiated way
- Claims it targets angiogenesis and NO's cytotoxic/damaging actions while maintaining/promoting/recovering protective functions
- This is a nuanced counterpoint to the "VEGF → cancer risk" simplification
- However, this remains predominantly a theoretical framework from animal data

### One Retraction Noted
- PMID 39865815 (Grubisic et al., Curr Neuropharmacol, Jan 2025): "Stable Gastric Pentadecapeptide BPC 157 as a Therapy of Severe Electrolyte Disturbances in Rats" — withdrawn at authors' request

### Surge in Research Output
- 2025: 16 publications (second-highest year after 2021's 19)
- 2026: 16 publications already by June (projected to exceed all previous years)
- Research groups expanding beyond original Croatian team (Turkish, Romanian, Chinese, US-based researchers now publishing)
- Multiple independent groups confirming effects (I/R injury, vascular, biopharmaceutical)

## Confidence Assessment Update
- **Animal data confidence**: Remains HIGH — robust, replicated across labs
- **Human efficacy confidence**: Upgraded from LOW to LOW-MEDIUM — now 3 small pilot studies with consistent positive signals, but all n<30, uncontrolled
- **Safety confidence**: LOW — no long-term data; short-term appears benign in limited human exposure
- **Mechanism confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH — VEGF, NO, FAK-paxillin pathways confirmed; human tissue validated for NO-mediated vasorelaxation
