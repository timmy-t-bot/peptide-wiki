# MOTS-c Source Compilation

## Discovery Paper
- **Lee C, Zeng J, Drew BG, et al.** "The mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c promotes metabolic homeostasis and reduces obesity and insulin resistance." *Cell Metabolism* 2015; 21(3):443-454. PMID: 25738459. DOI: 10.1016/j.cmet.2015.02.009.
  - Discovery paper. Identified MOTS-c as a 16-AA peptide from mtDNA 12S rRNA. Primary target: skeletal muscle. Mechanism: inhibits folate cycle → AICAR accumulation → AMPK activation. Mouse: prevented age-dependent and HFD-induced insulin resistance and obesity.

## Major Reviews
- **Wan W, Zhang L, Lin Y, et al.** "Mitochondria-derived peptide MOTS-c: effects and mechanisms related to stress, metabolism and aging." *J Transl Med* 2023; 21(1):36. PMID: 36670507. DOI: 10.1186/s12967-023-03885-2.
  - Comprehensive review covering retrograde signaling, energy metabolism, stress homeostasis, aging pathology, and molecular mechanisms.

- **Lee C, Kim KH, Cohen P.** "MOTS-c: A novel mitochondrial-derived peptide regulating muscle and fat metabolism." *Free Radic Biol Med* 2016; 100:182-187. PMID: 27216708. DOI: 10.1016/j.freeradbiomed.2016.05.015.

- **Gao Y, Wei X, Wei P, et al.** "MOTS-c Functionally Prevents Metabolic Disorders." *Metabolites* 2023; 13(1):125. PMID: 36677050. DOI: 10.3390/metabo13010125.
  - Summarizes genes/pathways (GLUT4, STAT3, IL-10; AICAR-AMPK, folate-methionine cycle).

- **Kal S, Mahata S, Jati S, Mahata SK.** "Mitochondrial-derived peptides: Antidiabetic functions and evolutionary perspectives." *Peptides* 2024; 172:171147. PMID: 38160808. DOI: 10.1016/j.peptides.2023.171147.

- **Ran Y, Guo Z, Zhang L, et al.** "Mitochondria‑derived peptides: Promising microproteins in cardiovascular diseases (Review)." *Mol Med Rep* 2025; 31(5):127. PMID: 40084698. DOI: 10.3892/mmr.2025.13492.
  - Notes challenges: low bioavailability, poor stability, high synthesis costs.

- **Behan M, Yen K, Cohen P, Kliment CR.** "Mitochondrial-derived microproteins in lung disease: insights and implications." *Am J Physiol Lung Cell Mol Physiol* 2026; 330(3):L222-L231. PMID: 41569667.

## Aging and Physical Performance
- **Reynolds JC, Lai RW, Woodhead JST, et al.** "MOTS-c is an exercise-induced mitochondrial-encoded regulator of age-dependent physical decline and muscle homeostasis." *Nat Commun* 2021; 12(1):470. PMID: 33473109. DOI: 10.1038/s41467-020-20790-0.
  - MOTS-c enhanced physical performance in young (2 mo), middle-age (12 mo), and old (22 mo) mice. Late-life (23.5 mo) initiated intermittent MOTS-c treatment (3x/week) increased physical capacity and healthspan. In humans, exercise induced endogenous MOTS-c expression in skeletal muscle and circulation.

## Human Studies
- **Dieli-Conwright CM, Sami N, Norris MK, et al.** "Effect of aerobic and resistance exercise on the mitochondrial peptide MOTS-c in Hispanic and Non-Hispanic White breast cancer survivors." *Sci Rep* 2021; 11(1):16916. PMID: 34413391. DOI: 10.1038/s41598-021-96419-z.
  - 16-week exercise intervention. MOTS-c increased in Non-Hispanic White BCS post-exercise but NOT in Hispanic BCS. Associations with reduced fat mass, body weight, HOMA-IR, CRP, increased lean mass. Suggests ethnic-specific mtDNA variations affect response.

- **Yoon SH, Yuan F, Zhu X, et al.** "Systemic MOTS-c levels are increased in adults with obesity in association with metabolic dysregulation and remain unchanged after weight loss." *J Clin Transl Endocrinol* 2025; 43:100429. PMID: 41551324.
  - Circulating MOTS-c HIGHER in obese vs lean (273±56 vs 223±50 pg/mL, P<0.01). BMI and HOMA-IR independently predicted elevated MOTS-c. Biphasic relationship with HOMA-IR. Unchanged 6 months post-bariatric surgery despite weight loss.

- **Musolino M, Roumeliotis A, Roumeliotis S, et al.** "MOTS-c is associated with oxidative stress and arterial stiffness in peritoneal dialysis patients: a pilot study." *Int Urol Nephrol* 2026. PMID: 42126770.
  - 32 PD patients. uMOTS-c inversely correlated with AOPPs (R=-0.592, p=0.012). Dialysate MOTS-c inversely correlated with PWV (R=-0.717, p=0.019). Suggests "Mitochondrial-Vascular Axis."

- **Kutuk IS, Akin S, Demirel H, et al.** "Reduced serum and skeletal muscle MOTS-c levels in women with polycystic ovary syndrome are associated with mitochondrial dysfunction." *Sci Rep* 2026; 16(1):8593. PMID: 41680431.
  - 40 PCOS vs 40 controls. Serum MOTS-c: 220.2 vs 498.3 pg/mL (P<0.001). Muscle MOTS-c also reduced (P=0.005). Inversely associated with total testosterone.

- **Filibeli BE, Dedemoglu F, Garipçin P, et al.** "Are serum MOTS-c levels and MOTS-c m.1382A>C polymorphism related to polycystic ovary syndrome?" *Arch Endocrinol Metab* 2026; 70(3):e260031. PMID: 41945630.
  - 121 PCOS vs 125 controls. No significant difference in MOTS-c levels (p=0.059). All participants had wild-type AA genotype for m.1382A>C.

- **Liu S, Ru K, Shen YJ, et al.** "Mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c targets SLC7A11 to preserve spermatogenesis by suppressing ferroptosis." *Free Radic Biol Med* 2026; 250:284-297. PMID: 41933740.
  - MOTS-c significantly reduced in serum of patients with oligoasthenozoospermia. Correlated with semen quality.

## Animal Studies (Mouse/Rat)
- **Gudiksen A, Hansen CC, van der Stede T, et al.** "MOTS-c improves intrinsic muscle mitochondrial bioenergetic health and efficiency in a PGC-1α/AMPK-dependent manner." *Free Radic Biol Med* 2026; 246:682-696. PMID: 41520850.
  - Mouse; transgenic strains. MOTS-c augments muscle mitochondrial bioenergetics via PGC-1α and AMPK. Reduced mitochondrial ROS emission. Human note: no arterio-venous difference during knee extensor exercise, suggesting skeletal muscle may not be source of exercise-induced circulating MOTS-c.

- **Santhanam SS, Jayaraman S, Kurian GA.** "Exogenous MOTS-c mitigates myocardial ischemia-reperfusion injury: experimental and in silico evidence from rat heart models." *Naunyn Schmiedebergs Arch Pharmacol* 2026; 399(7):9969-9984. PMID: 41593376.
  - Rat Langendorff model. 0.5 mg/kg optimal dose → 73% reduction in infarct size. Docking: interacts with MAPK, mTOR, AMPK, NRF2, PI3K, caspase 3.

- **Feng Z, Xing Y, Lou J, et al.** "MOTS-c attenuates cardiac dysfunction following high altitude exposure by promoting mitophagy." *Free Radic Biol Med* 2026; 247:157-172. PMID: 41654147.
  - Mouse decompression chamber (6000m simulation). Exogenous MOTS-c activated Pink1/Parkin pathway → mitophagy. Reduced circulating MOTS-c in patients with high altitude heart disease.

- **Li Z, Wang T, Fu Y, et al.** "Aerobic exercise and MOTS-c attenuate diabetic myocardial fibrosis via inhibition of the THBS1/TGF-β signaling pathway." *Front Endocrinol* 2026; 17:1732329. PMID: 41710402.
  - Mouse T2D model. MOTS-c mimicked exercise benefits.

- **Bai Y, Wu H, Wang X, et al.** "A mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c contributes to the protective effect against brain injury associated with LPS-induced sepsis by strengthening the blood-brain barrier's ultrastructure." *Int J Neurosci* 2026; 136(3):329-342. PMID: 40753494.
  - Mouse sepsis model. MOTS-c (20 mg/kg) 4h before LPS. Reduced mortality, BBB permeability, neuroinflammation.

- **Güvenir Seven S, Sahin H, et al.** "Therapeutic Effects of MOTS-c in the Valproic Acid-Induced Autism Model in Rats." *Mol Neurobiol* 2026; 63(1):453. PMID: 41706383.
  - Rat VPA autism model. 0.5 mg/kg/day MOTS-c IP from PND 21-46. Reversed sociability deficits, repetitive behaviors, cerebellar Purkinje cell loss. Did NOT cross BBB (mechanism independent of BH4/BDNF).

## Safety and Regulatory
- **Mendias CL, Awan TM.** "Safety and Efficacy of Approved and Unapproved Peptide Therapies for Musculoskeletal Injuries and Athletic Performance." *Sports Med* 2026. PMID: 41966639.
  - Includes MOTS-c among unapproved peptides. "Rigorous human safety data are scarce, and there is potential for serious harm to patients." Discusses gray market, social media amplification of placebo effect.

- **WADA 2024 Prohibited List**: MOTS-c explicitly banned beginning 2024 as an unapproved substance (S0 category).
