### Key Facts
- Tesamorelin (INN; trade name Egrifta SV) is a synthetic form of growth-hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH)
- Developed by Theratechnologies, Inc. of Canada
- 44 amino acids of human GHRH + trans-3-hexenoic acid group
- Approved in US (2010) for HIV-associated lipodystrophy
- ATC code: H01AC06
- Bioavailability: ≤4%
- Half-life: 26–38 min
- Metabolism: Proteolysis
- Excretion: Renal/proteolysis
- Route: Subcutaneous injection
- Legal: US: ℞-only; Generally: Prescription only
- CAS: 218949-48-5; salt: 901758-09-6
- Formula: C221H366N72O67S; MW: 5135.86 g/mol

Mechanism: N-terminally modified 44-aa GHRH analog, more potent and stable than natural peptide, more resistant to dipeptidyl aminopeptidase cleavage. Stimulates GH synthesis/release, increases IGF-1. Primary mechanism for fat reduction: lipolysis + triglyceride reduction.

Contraindications: Pregnancy (Category X), hypothalamic-pituitary axis disruption, pituitary tumor, head irradiation, hypopituitarism.

Adverse effects: Injection site erythema, peripheral edema, pruritus, diarrhea, joint/arm/leg pains, lipoatrophy risk, glucose intolerance, increased type 2 diabetes risk.
