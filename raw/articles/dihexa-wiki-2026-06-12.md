Dihexa (PNB-0408) is an oligopeptide derived from Angiotensin IV. 
It is a high-affinity ligand for the Hepatocyte Growth Factor (HGF) receptor, c-Met. 
By potentiating HGF/c-Met signaling, it aims to stimulate synaptogenesis and cognitive recovery.
Animal models of Alzheimer's and other dementias showed significant cognitive improvement.
However, the HGF/c-Met pathway is heavily implicated in oncogenesis (cancer progression and metastasis), raising severe theoretical safety concerns regarding long-term use.
Recent discussions include retractions or failures to replicate some of the more extreme cognitive claims in non-human primates or specific rodent strains.
