# Thymosin Alpha-1 PubMed Research Dump

## Wikipedia Summary
- 28-amino acid fragment: Ac-SDAAVDTSSEITTKDLKEKKEVEEEAEN
- Cleaved from 113-aa precursor prothymosin alpha (PTMA gene)
- TLR2 and TLR9 agonist on myeloid and dendritic antigen-presenting cells
- Approved in 35+ countries for hepatitis B and C (brand: Zadaxin / thymalfasin by SciClone)
- NOT FDA-approved; restricted by FDA in 2023 alongside 21 other peptides in compounding
- Subcutaneous injection route
- Clinical studies: cystic fibrosis, septic shock, ARDS, peritonitis, pancreatitis, CMV, TB, SARS, lung infections, COPD, cancer (melanoma, NSCLC)

## Key Papers

### Dominari 2020 (PMID: 33362999) — Comprehensive Review
- Tα1 modifies, enhances, and restores immune function
- Used for: immunocompromised states, malignancies, vaccine response enhancer, sepsis/infection morbidity reduction
- Postulated benefit in COVID-19 via preventing excessive T-cell activation
- Key properties: antibacterial and antiviral

### Dinetz & Lee 2024 (PMID: 38308608) — Safety Review of 11,000+ Subjects
- >30 clinical trials, >11,000 human subjects
- Consistent evidence of safety and efficacy
- Effective in COVID-19, autoimmune disorders, cancer
- Argues FDA restriction (2023) is unfounded
- Recommends FDA permit 503A compounding pharmacies to compound Tα1

### Goldstein 2009 (PMID: 19392576) — Lab to Bedside
- Approved in >35 countries for hepatitis B/C and as immune stimulant
- Late-stage clinical testing in US/Europe for hepatitis C and stage IV melanoma
- Emerging applications: septic shock, ARDS, peritonitis, acute CMV, TB, SARS, lung infections in critically ill
- Promising chemoprotection agent during chemotherapy

### Garaci 2007 Historical Overview (PMID: 17567941)
- Isolated from thymus, characterized by Allan Goldstein in 1977
- Increased MHC class-I and TLR expression + cytokine production
- Pilot trials in hepatitis C and cancer

### Garaci 2007 Bench to Bedside (PMID: 17600290)
- Lewis lung carcinoma mouse model: Tα1 + IFN + cyclophosphamide effective
- Friend erythroleukemia and B16 melanoma mouse models confirmed efficacy
- Rat DHD/K12 colorectal cancer liver metastasis model
- Pivotal NSCLC and melanoma trials with Tα1 + IFN-α + cisplatin/dacarbazine
- Mechanism: activates infected dendritic cells via TLR signaling, influences inflammation balance, increases tumor/viral/MHC-I antigen expression

### Shang 2023 COVID-19 Meta-Analysis (PMID: 36527881)
- 9 studies, 5,352 patients (Tα1=1,152, control=4,200)
- No significant effect on mortality overall [RR 1.03, p=0.92]
- BUT: significant mortality benefit in subgroup >60 years, female ≤40%, severe/critical COVID-19 [RR 0.66-0.68]
- Conclusion: does NOT support use in hospitalized adult COVID-19 patients overall

### Soeroto 2023 COVID-19 Meta-Analysis (PMID: 37845598)
- 8 studies — CONTRADICTED Shang: significantly lower mortality [RR 0.59, p=0.02]
- No difference in mechanical ventilation or hospital stay
- Benefits affected by sample size and sex

### Gu 2025 Sepsis Meta-Analysis (PMID: 40969554)
- 11 RCTs, 967 Tα1 vs 960 control
- Significant reduction in 28-day mortality [OR 0.73, p=0.003]
- BUT high-quality subgroup [OR 0.82, p=0.09] and multicenter subgroup [OR 0.86, p=0.20] did NOT reach significance
- Potential benefits in cancer, diabetes, CHD subgroups
- TSA suggests sample size inadequate

### Tian 2025 Pancreatitis Meta-Analysis (PMID: 40599771)
- 5 RCTs, 706 SAP patients
- Increased CD4+ cells and CD4+/CD8+ ratio
- Reduced CRP (lower-dose), reduced extrapancreatic infections [RR 0.56]
- No significant reduction in hospital stay

### Cao 2024 COPD Meta-Analysis (PMID: 39648386)
- 39 RCTs, 3,329 AECOPD patients
- Significant improvements in FEV1, FEV1/FVC, PaO2, CD4+ count, CD4+/CD8+ ratio
- Decreased PaCO2, CD8+ count, hospital stay length

### Wu 2015 Hepatitis B Review (PMID: 25640173)
- Tα1 monotherapy effective vs untreated/IFN control in CHB
- Combination with lamivudine or IFN-α better for HBV DNA suppression and HBeAg seroconversion
- Ongoing studies: Tα1 + entecavir for HBV-cirrhosis

### Garaci 2000 Cancer Review (PMID: 11137613)
- Tα1 + low-dose IFN or IL-2 restores immune responses depressed by tumors/chemotherapy
- Tα1 + chemotherapy increases anti-tumor effect while reducing chemotherapy toxicity

### Quagliata 2023 Patent Landscape (PMID: 38131310)
- States: "Thymalfasin (Zadaxin) is the only FDA-approved thymosine-based drug used to treat chronic hepatitis B and C" — NOTE: This appears to be a factual error; the peptide is approved in 35+ countries but NOT by FDA. The patent landscape paper may be referring to a foreign approval or may be inaccurate. The Goldstein 2009 paper explicitly states it was in late-stage US testing.

### Simonova 2025 Aging Review (PMID: 41373628)
- Tα1 restores immune function by stimulating T-cell differentiation, enhancing thymic output, modulating dendritic cell and macrophage activity
- Improves vaccine response in elderly, mitigates immunosenescence
- Refnot: TNFα-Tα1 fusion drug with reduced toxicity for anti-aging/anti-cancer

## ClinicalTrials.gov
- 61 total studies (search: thymosin alpha-1)
- 13 active/recruiting (10 recruiting, 3 active not recruiting)
- Indications across trials: hepatitis B/C, liver cancer, NSCLC, melanoma, sepsis, COVID-19, pancreatitis, COPD, cystic fibrosis

## FDA Status
- NOT FDA-approved as of 2026
- 2023: FDA placed Tα1 on a list of peptides restricted from compounding by 503A pharmacies
- Dinetz 2024 paper argues this restriction is unfounded given >11,000 subject safety data
- Trade name: Zadaxin (SciClone Pharmaceuticals) — approved in 35+ countries, primarily China and other Asian/developing countries

## WADA Status
- Thymosin beta-4 (TB-500) is explicitly prohibited under S2
- Thymosin alpha-1 is NOT a thymosin beta. It is chemically and genetically unrelated to beta thymosins.
- Tα1 is not explicitly listed. However, under S0 (non-approved substances) and S2 (peptide hormones, growth factors, related substances, and mimetics), it could potentially fall under WADA scrutiny as an unapproved immunomodulatory peptide
- Athletes should consult WADA directly for clarification

## Safety Notes
- Dinetz 2024: >11,000 subjects in >30 trials; well-tolerated
- Goldstein 2009: generally well-tolerated
- Common side effects (from reviews): injection site reactions, mild flu-like symptoms, fatigue
- Rare: allergic reactions, transient fever
- No long-term carcinogenicity data published
- No reproductive safety data
- As an immunostimulant, theoretical concern about autoimmune disease exacerbation
