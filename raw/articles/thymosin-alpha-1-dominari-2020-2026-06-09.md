# Thymosin alpha 1: A comprehensive review of the literature
**Dominari A, et al. World J Virol. 2020 Dec 15;9(5):67-78. PMC7747025**

## Key findings:

### Biochemistry
- 28 amino acid peptide originally isolated from thymus
- Sequence: Ac-SDAAVDTSSEITTKDLKEKKEVEEEAEN
- Derived from 113-amino acid precursor prothymosin α
- NMR structure: distorted helical configuration with alpha-helix site from 14-26 residues, two double turns in β region in N-terminal site (12 residues)
- Synthetic form: Thymalfasin (brand name: Zadaxin)

### Approved Status
- Approved in more than 35 countries for Hepatitis B and C
- FDA orphan drug status for malignant melanoma, chronic active hepatitis B, DiGeorge anomaly, hepatocellular carcinoma

### Mechanism
- TLR-9 and TLR-2 agonist in myeloid and dendritic cells
- Increases IL-2, IL-10, IL-12, IFN-α, IFN-γ
- Activates NK cells and CD8+ T cells
- Decreases IL-1β and TNF-α (anti-inflammatory)
- Increases MHC class I/viral antigen expression
- Protects against oxidative damage (superoxide dismutase, glutathione peroxidase)

### Clinical Applications
- **Hepatitis B**: 40.6% complete virological response rate at 1.6 mg SC twice weekly for 52 weeks; now obsolete in era of direct antivirals
- **Hepatitis C**: Monotherapy not useful; combination with IFN-α showed benefit; now discontinued for direct antivirals
- **Sepsis**: Significant mortality reduction; multicenter single-blind RCT showed 9% lower mortality (1.6 mg SC twice daily x 5 days, then once daily)
- **HIV**: Enhances CD4+ T cells, decreases viral load; well-tolerated with HAART
- **Cancer**: Inhibits proliferation, induces apoptosis; PTEN-mediated inhibition of PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway. Effective in breast cancer cell lines at 100-160 IM. NSCLC, melanoma, HCC
- **Vaccine adjuvant**: Enhanced influenza vaccine response in elderly/immunocompromised
- **COVID-19**: Reduces mortality in severe cases; restores T cell counts in lymphocytopenic patients; National Health Commission of China included Tα1 for patients with lymphocytopenia
- **Pseudomonas/mold infections**: Antifungal Th1 response via dendritic cell priming
- **Psoriatic arthritis**: Lower serum Tα1 levels found in patients

### Safety
- Usually well-tolerated
- Common: local irritation, redness, discomfort at injection site
- Rare with IFN-α2b: fever, fatigue, muscle aches, nausea, vomiting, neutropenia
- Contraindicated: hypersensitivity; immunosuppressed patients (organ transplant) unless benefits exceed risks
- SC administration; standard single dose 0.8-6.4 mg; multiple doses 1.6-16 mg for 5-7 days

### Storage
- Store at -20°C; lyophilized stable up to 3 weeks at room temp; long-term below -180°C
- Reconstituted: 4°C for 2-7 days; longer with carrier protein (0.1% HSA or BSA)
- Avoid repeated freeze-thaw

### Comparison with Thymosin Beta 4
- Chemically and functionally distinct
- Tα1: immune restoration, cell-mediated immunity
- TB4: actin-sequestering, wound healing, tested for HIV/influenza
