# Selank — Wikipedia

Selank (Russian: Селанк) is a nootropic anxiolytic peptide-based drug developed by the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences. Heptapeptide with sequence Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro (TKPRPGP). Synthetic analogue of the human tuftsin.

## Pharmacology
- Synthetic analogue of immunomodulatory peptide tuftsin; mimics many of its effects.
- Modulates expression of Interleukin-6 (IL-6) and affects balance of T helper cell cytokines (Uchakina 2008).
- Influences concentration of monoamine neurotransmitters and induces metabolism of serotonin (Narkevich 2008; Semenova 2009, 2010).
- Rapidly elevates expression of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) in rat hippocampus (Inozemtseva 2008).
- Inhibits enzymes involved in degradation of enkephalins and other endogenous regulatory peptides, including carboxypeptidase H (Kost 2001; Solov'ev 2012).
- Produces antidepressant-like effects in animal models of depression and anhedonia (Sarkisova 2008).

## Clinical trials
- Investigated for effects on cognitive function and anxiety (Uchakina 2008).
- Compared with traditional anxiolytic drugs such as benzodiazepines and barbiturates (Zozulia 2008).
- Available in Russian and Ukrainian pharmacies.

## Legal status
- In general: unscheduled.
- Not FDA/EMA approved.
- Approved in Russia and Ukraine.
