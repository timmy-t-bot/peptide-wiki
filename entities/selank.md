# Selank

## Summary

Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro, TKPRPGP) developed by the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences. It is a synthetic analogue of the endogenous immunomodulatory peptide tuftsin. Marketed as a nootropic and anxiolytic agent in Russia and Ukraine, it has not undergone FDA or EMA review. The evidence base consists predominantly of Russian-language preclinical studies and small clinical trials, with very limited independent Western replication.

---

## Structure & Pharmacology

- **Sequence:** Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro (TKPRPGP)
- **Molecular formula:** C₃₃H₅₇N₁₁O₉
- **Class:** Synthetic tuftsin analogue; nootropic / anxiolytic peptide
- **Routes:** Intranasal and intravenous (IV) administration reported

---

## Mechanism of Action

- **Immunomodulation:** Modulates expression of Interleukin-6 (IL-6) and affects T helper cell cytokine balance (Uchakina 2008).
- **Monoaminergic effects:** Influences brain monoamine neurotransmitter concentrations and induces serotonin metabolism in rodent models (Narkevich 2008; Semenova 2009, 2010).
- **Neurotrophic factor:** Rapidly elevates brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) expression in rat hippocampus following intranasal administration (Inozemtseva 2008).
- **Enkephalinase inhibition:** Inhibits enzymes involved in degradation of enkephalins and other endogenous regulatory peptides, including carboxypeptidase H (Kost 2001; Solov'ev 2012). This mechanism may contribute to its anxiolytic and antidepressant-like effects.
- **Antidepressant-like activity:** Shown in animal models of depression and anhedonia in WAG/Rij rats, Wistar rats, and BALB/c mice (Sarkisova 2008).

**Critical note:** Most mechanistic data come from a single Russian research group. Independent replication in Western laboratories is sparse.

---

## Evidence Assessment

### Human Studies

- **Small clinical trials in Russia** investigated Selank for generalized anxiety disorders and neurasthenia. These studies compared Selank to traditional anxiolytics such as benzodiazepines, but they are small, published in Russian-language journals, and lack the rigor of Western regulatory trials (Zozulia 2008; Uchakina 2008).
- **No FDA or EMA clinical trials** have been conducted.
- **fMRI study:** One small neuroimaging study (Panikratova et al. 2020, n=52 healthy volunteers) compared Selank and Semax. Both peptides modulated functional connectivity between right amygdala and right temporal cortex, with Selank showing a distinct connectivity pattern from Semax.
- **Pharmacy availability:** Sold in Russian and Ukrainian pharmacies as a prescription drug, but this does not constitute approval by major Western regulators.

### Animal Models

- **Rat models:** Intranasal Selank elevated hippocampal BDNF and regulated serotonin metabolism after PCPA pretreatment (Inozemtseva 2008; Semenova 2009).
- **Mouse models:** Modulated monoamine content in BALB/c and C57Bl/6 mouse brains (Narkevich 2008).
- **Depression models:** Reduced genetically based and situation-provoked depressive behaviors in WAG/Rij and Wistar rats and BALB/c mice (Sarkisova 2008).

### Anecdotal / Community Evidence

- Gray-market users report reduced anxiety, improved focus, and mild mood elevation. These reports are entirely anecdotal and uncontrolled.
- Selank is frequently discussed alongside [[semax]] in nootropic communities as a "calming" peptide.

## Safety Profile

### ⚠️ Critical Safety Flags

1. **Monoaminergic activity / serotonergic interaction risk:** Selank modulates serotonin metabolism and monoamine neurotransmitters in animal models. Theoretical interaction risk with SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, and other serotonergic agents. No clinical interaction studies exist.

2. **Enkephalinase inhibition / opioid system interaction:** By inhibiting enkephalin-degrading enzymes, Selank may potentiate endogenous opioid peptides. Theoretical concern for interactions with opioid medications or substances affecting the opioid system.

3. **No FDA/EMA safety review:** The peptide has not undergone formal Western regulatory safety assessment. Long-term safety data (>12 weeks) are unpublished.

4. **Gray-market sourcing risks:** Non-pharmaceutical sources (online peptide vendors) are unregulated. Purity, sterility, and identity are not guaranteed.

5. **Limited pharmacokinetic data:** Human half-life, bioavailability (especially intranasal), and metabolic fate are poorly characterized in peer-reviewed literature.

6. **Pregnancy / lactation:** No data. Contraindicated due to complete absence of reproductive safety information.

---

## Legal Status

- **Russia / Ukraine:** Approved prescription drug (nootropic/anxiolytic).
- **United States / EU:** Not FDA or EMA approved. Unscheduled, but not legal to market for human consumption. Sold as a "research chemical" in the gray market.
- **WADA:** Not explicitly listed on the Prohibited List as of 2026, but general peptide analogues may fall under S0 (unapproved substances) at the discretion of anti-doping authorities.

## Dosing (Research Context)

**For educational purposes only. Not medical advice. Selank lacks FDA/EMA approval and rigorous human safety data. No established clinical dosing protocol exists outside Russia/Ukraine.**

- **Typical intranasal dose (reported in community sources):** 200–300 µg per nostril, 2–3× daily
- **IV route:** Occasionally reported in Russian clinical literature; not practical for self-administration
- **Cycle duration:** Commonly 2–4 weeks on, followed by a break
- **Storage:** Lyophilized powder refrigerated; reconstituted with bacteriostatic water

**No Western clinical trial has validated these protocols for safety or efficacy.**

## Confidence Assessment: LOW

**Rationale:**
- Evidence is dominated by a single Russian research group with limited independent replication
- No FDA or EMA clinical trials; Western regulatory review absent
- Small Russian trials exist but are methodologically limited and language-isolated
- Mechanistic data are robust in rodents but translation to humans is uncertain
- Gray-market sourcing introduces unknown purity risks
- Long-term safety data are essentially absent

---

## Gaps in Knowledge

1. No large-scale, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials in Western populations
2. Human pharmacokinetics (intranasal bioavailability, half-life) are poorly characterized
3. No formal drug-drug interaction studies (especially with serotonergic and opioid agents)
4. Long-term safety (>3 months) is unpublished
5. Mechanism of anxiolysis is not fully elucidated beyond enkephalinase inhibition and BDNF elevation
6. Comparative efficacy against established anxiolytics (SSRIs, benzodiazepines) is unknown
