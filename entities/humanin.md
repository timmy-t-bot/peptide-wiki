# Humanin

## Overview
Humanin is a 21-amino acid peptide encoded within the mitochondrial 16S rRNA gene (MT-RNR2). It is a mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP) that serves as a critical cytoprotective and anti-apoptotic signal.

## Mechanism of Action
Humanin protects cells against apoptosis by modulating various survival pathways. It is believed to interact with the mitochondrial permeability transition pore (mPTP) and may influence insulin signaling and metabolic homeostasis.

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies
- Strong evidence for inhibiting apoptosis induced by various stressors, including amyloid-beta and oxidative stress.
- Demonstrated capacity to preserve mitochondrial function and prevent the release of cytochrome c.

### Animal Models (Mouse/Rat)
- Shown to provide neuroprotection in Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease mouse models.
- Evidence suggests a role in improving insulin sensitivity and reducing glucose levels in diabetic rat models.

### Human Studies
- No clinical trials for exogenous administration have reached Phase III or FDA approval.
- Most "human data" consists of endogenous expression levels in various disease states (e.g., decreased humanin in heart failure or diabetic retinopathy).

## Health Optimization Applications
- **Neuroprotection:** Potential for mitigating cognitive decline.
- **Metabolic Health:** Possible application in improving insulin sensitivity.
- **Cardiovascular Support:** Potential for protecting cardiac tissue during ischemic events.

## Safety Profile
- **Known Side Effects:** Very little is known about the safety of exogenous administration in humans.
- **Contraindications:** Unknown.
- **Long-term Safety:** Completely unvalidated in humans.
- *Theoretical concern:* Over-expression of anti-apoptotic signals can theoretically interfere with the clearance of damaged or pre-cancerous cells.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)
*For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.*
- Dosing is primarily observed in laboratory settings (subcutaneous or intravenous in rodents). Human research doses are not standardized.

## Comparison with Related Peptides
Compared to other MDPs like [[mots-c]], Humanin is more focused on acute cytoprotection and anti-apoptosis, whereas MOTS-c acts more as an exercise mimetic and metabolic regulator.
