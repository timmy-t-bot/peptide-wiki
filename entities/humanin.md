# Humanin

Humanin is a mitochondria-derived peptide (MDP) encoded within the 16S rRNA gene (MT-RNR2) of the mitochondrial genome. It was the first MDP to be discovered and is the most evolutionarily conserved, with homologs identified in species ranging from humans to nematodes and naked mole rats.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]

## Overview

- **Sequence:** 24-AA cytosolic form (MAPRGFSCLLLLTSEIDLPVKRRA) or 20-AA mitochondrial form (MAPRGFSCLLLLTSEIDLPV)
- **Discovery:** 2001, independently by three labs (Nishimoto, Reed, and Pinchas Cohen groups)
- **Gene:** MT-RNR2; nuclear paralogs (MTRNR2L1–L8) also exist but their expression status is unclear
- **Synthetic analogs:** HNG (S14G substitution, up to 1000× more potent); HNGF6A (F6A + S14G); shorter fragments retain partial activity^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Legal status:** Research compound; inferred from absence of any published clinical trial registrations or regulatory filings (no FDA/EMA approval; no approved indication anywhere)

## Mechanism of Action

Humanin exerts cytoprotective effects through multiple pathways:

1. **Extracellular signaling:** Binds a tripartite receptor composed of gp130, WSX1, and CNTFR, and interacts with formyl peptide receptor 2 (FPR2/FPRL-1).^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
2. **Intracellular anti-apoptosis:** Interacts with BAX, tBID, IGFBP3, and TRIM11 to inhibit apoptosis.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
3. **Metabolic regulation:** Activates chaperone-mediated autophagy in a dose-dependent manner; decreases inflammatory cytokine production; increases insulin sensitivity and pancreatic beta-cell survival.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
4. **Antioxidant response:** Activates SIRT3 → Nrf2/HO-1 pathway to reduce ROS.^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies
- PC12 cells: HN pretreatment increased survival and reduced ROS under rotenone-induced neurotoxicity via SIRT3/Nrf2/HO-1 pathway activation (2026).^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- Primary human skeletal muscle myotubes: HNG co-treatment with dexamethasone preserved myotube area and blunted STAT3 activation (2026).^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- Pre-osteoblastic MC3T3-E1 cells: SHLP2 (humanin-family peptide) improved viability, reduced ROS, and restored osteogenic differentiation under oxidative stress (2025).^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]

### Animal Models
- **Alzheimer's disease models:** Multiple rodent studies show increased survival and improved physiological parameters after humanin administration.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Huntington's disease, prion disease, stroke models:** Protective effects demonstrated in rodents.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Cardiovascular:** Protects against atherosclerotic plaque formation and heart attack in animal models.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Retinal degeneration (RCS rats):** High-dose HNG (4 mg/kg, twice weekly IP) improved visual acuity after 4 weeks and altered RPE/retinal gene expression (2026 preprint). ERG showed no difference from sham.^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Atrial fibrillation (AngII mouse model):** HNG reduced AF inducibility, attenuated atrial fibrosis and hypertrophy, improved mitochondrial ultrastructure, and lowered IL-1β/IL-6 (2026).^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Duchenne muscular dystrophy (mdx mice):** HNG co-administered with glucocorticoids prevented bone growth impairment and mitigated cortical bone adverse effects (2026).^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Longevity (*C. elegans*):** Overexpression extended lifespan by increasing autophagy.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Metabolic (rats):** Humanin analog normalized glucose levels and reduced diabetes symptoms.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]

### Human Studies
- **Zero interventional human trials** of exogenous humanin or HNG have been published. Confirmed by absence of any human interventional data in both Wikipedia and PubMed raw sources compiled for this review.
- Observational data: Humanin levels are downregulated in human atrial fibrillation tissue and inversely correlate with fibrosis extent.^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- All human data is correlational (endogenous levels in disease states); no dosing, safety, or efficacy data in humans.

## Health Optimization Applications

- **Neuroprotection:** Animal evidence suggests cytoprotective effects in AD, HD, prion, and stroke models. Human relevance is unknown.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Metabolic support:** Preclinical data suggest insulin-sensitizing and beta-cell protective effects in rodents.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Cardiovascular:** Animal evidence for atherosclerosis and heart attack protection; no human data.^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]
- **Muscle/bone:** Emerging mouse data on glucocorticoid-induced atrophy and bone loss mitigation.^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Retinal health:** RCS rat data suggest possible relevance for retinal degenerative diseases; no human data.^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]

## Safety Profile

- **No human safety data:** Zero clinical trials; safety in humans is completely unknown.
- **Theoretical concerns (not sourced in raw materials):**
  - *Mitochondrial signaling disruption risk:* Chronic exogenous administration of a mitochondrial-derived peptide could theoretically interfere with mitochondrial-nuclear signaling homeostasis.
  - *Autophagy modulation:* Long-term effects of sustained chaperone-mediated autophagy activation are unknown.
  - *Immunomodulation:* Anti-inflammatory effects are demonstrated preclinically; theoretical risk of immunosuppression at high or chronic doses.
- **Pregnancy/bone development:** One mouse study showed HNG did not exacerbate skeletal muscle pathology and had mild muscle fiber enlarging effects; however, long-term bone and muscle effects in developing organisms are unknown.^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Gray-market sourcing:** No regulated manufacturing identified in compiled sources; purity and stability concerns are inferred from the absence of clinical-grade production.
- **This is for educational purposes only. Not medical advice.**

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)

No human dosing data exists. Animal studies use a wide range:
- RCS rats: 0.4–4 mg/kg IP, twice weekly^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- AngII mouse model: HNG dose not specified in abstract^[raw/articles/humanin-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- *C. elegans*: Overexpression (transgenic), not exogenous dosing^[raw/articles/humanin-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]

Any dosing in humans would be purely speculative and unsupported by clinical data.
