## 1. Overview

Epitalon (also spelled Epithalon or Epithalone) is a synthetic tetrapeptide with the amino acid sequence **Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly (AEDG)**. It was designed in the late 1990s by **Vladimir Khavinson** at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology, based on the amino acid composition of **Epithalamin** — a crude polypeptide extract derived from bovine pineal glands that had been studied since 1973.[¹](#sources)

- **Sequence:** H-Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly-OH
- **Molecular Formula:** C₁₄H₂₂N₄O₉
- **Molecular Weight:** 390.35 g/mol
- **CAS Number:** 307297-39-8
- **PubChem CID:** 219042

Epitalon was patented in Russia in 2001 (Patent RU 2161501). For many years its presence in the human body was unconfirmed, but in 2017 it was detected for the first time in physiological pineal gland polypeptide complex, suggesting it may be an endogenous peptide.[²](#sources)

### Legal Status

Epitalon is **not FDA-approved** for any medical indication in the United States, the European Union, or most other jurisdictions. It is not classified as a controlled substance in most countries. In Russia, Khavinson commercialized it as a pharmaceutical and food supplement. In Western countries, it is sold as a "research chemical" — not for human consumption. **Do not interpret this section as legal advice; laws vary by jurisdiction.**

### Key Caveats

- The **overwhelming majority** of Epitalon research was conducted by **one research group** (Vladimir Khavinson and colleagues at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology). Independent replication has been limited until very recently.
- The Epitalon Wikipedia article carries a warning flag for "fringe theories" due to over-reliance on primary sources from the Khavinson group (as of October 2022).
- The 2025 comprehensive review by Araj et al. (published after Khavinson's death in January 2024) notes that "the precise mechanism of action remains unverified" and that "information regarding critical issues about this peptide's safety is missing."[¹](#sources)

## 2. Mechanism of Action

The mechanisms of Epitalon remain incompletely characterized. Multiple potential pathways have been proposed based on the body of research:

### 2.1 Telomerase Activation
Epitalon has been shown to **activate telomerase** (the enzyme that extends telomeres) in **human somatic cells in vitro**, leading to telomere elongation.[³](#sources) The proposed mechanism involves the peptide binding to specific DNA sequences (e.g., ATTTC/CAG) in the promoter region of the telomerase gene, potentially influencing transcription through epigenetic mechanisms. This was sufficient to let human fetal fibroblasts **surpass the Hayflick limit** — from the 34th passage (control) to beyond the 44th passage (treated).[⁴](#sources)

Recent **independent** work (2025) has confirmed telomerase activation by Epitalon in human cell lines, showing it may work through **both telomerase upregulation and ALT (Alternative Lengthening of Telomeres) activity**.[⁵](#sources)

### 2.2 Antioxidant Activity
Shown in rat models: Epitalon increased the activity of antioxidant enzymes **superoxide dismutase (SOD), glutathione peroxidase, and glutathione-S-transferase** in aging rats.[⁶](#sources) In mouse brain and liver, it suppressed free radical generation and lipid peroxidation.[⁷](#sources)

### 2.3 Melatonin Synthesis Enhancement
Epitalon appears to **enhance melatonin synthesis** in pinealocytes by increasing expression of the transcription factor pCREB and the enzyme arylalkylamine N-acetyltransferase (AANAT). This was shown in rat pinealocyte culture and in aged rhesus monkeys.[⁸](#sources)[⁹](#sources)

### 2.4 Immune Modulation
Epitalon modulates immune function, including:
- Increased IL-2 mRNA expression in murine splenocytes[¹⁰](#sources)
- Enhanced proliferation of lymphocytes in the thymus[¹¹](#sources)
- Modulation of CD4+ and CD8+ cell populations in aged mice bone marrow and spleen[¹²](#sources)
- Increased interferon gamma production by T-cells

### 2.5 Chromatin / Epigenetic Effects
Epitalon induces decondensation of heterochromatin near the centromeres in cultured lymphocytes from elderly human donors (ages 76–80). It has been shown to penetrate the cell membrane and enter the nucleus, binding to specific DNA sequences (CAG-rich regions).[¹³](#sources) Molecular docking studies suggest interaction with histone proteins H1/6 and H1/3, pointing to an epigenetic mechanism.[¹⁴](#sources)

### 2.6 Enzyme Modulation
Epitalon modestly increases the activity of **acetylcholinesterase (AChE) and butyrylcholinesterase (BuChE)** in human neuroblastoma SH-SY5Y cells, and increases secretion of the soluble form of **amyloid precursor protein (APP)** by ~20%, suggesting a potential cholinergic/cognitive mechanism.[¹⁵](#sources)

### 2.7 Other Pathways
- Inhibition of MMP-9 (matrix metalloproteinase) in aging skin fibroblasts *in vitro*
- Dose-dependent inhibition of human enkephalinase (IC₅₀ = 500 µM)
- Modulation of STAT1 phosphorylation in THP-1 monocytic cells, without affecting IFN-α production
- Possible interaction with amino acid transporters LAT1, LAT2, and PEPT1

> **Important Mechanistic Uncertainty:** The 2025 comprehensive review explicitly states: *"it remains uncertain whether these are the sole mechanisms of action of this compound."* The complete pharmacological profile of Epitalon, including receptor targets, signaling cascades, and pharmacokinetics, has **not been established**.[¹](#sources)

### 3.2 Animal Models

#### Shown in Mouse Models

- **SHR mice:** 17.1% reduction in bone marrow chromosomal aberrations (p < 0.05). No effect on food intake, body weight, or mean lifespan in this strain.[¹⁹](#sources)
- **SAMP-1, SAMR-1, SHR mice:** Significant reduction in chromosomal aberrations in all strains, consistent with telomere protection.[²⁰](#sources)
- **CBA mice:** ~4-fold increase in number of mice reaching 23 months of age. Oldest control mouse: 24 months; oldest treated: 34 months (p < 0.01). Reduced tumor incidence (p < 0.05).[⁷](#sources)
- **C3H/He mice (female):** Reduced spontaneous tumors and reduced metastases.[²¹](#sources)
- **HER-2/neu transgenic mice:** 3.7-fold lower HER-2/neu mRNA expression in mammary tissue. Reduced breast adenocarcinoma incidence (p < 0.05). Extended lifespan.[²²](#sources)
- **FVB/N HER-2/neu mice:** Extended lifespan of last 10% of survivors by 13.3%.[¹⁹](#sources)

#### Shown in Rat Models

- **LIO rats (female):** Prolongation of maximum lifespan in rats under natural northwestern Russia light or constant illumination. Reduced spontaneous tumor incidence in natural light conditions.[²³](#sources)
- **Wistar rats:** Reduced lipid peroxidation in brain and serum. Reduced splenic lymphocyte apoptosis after γ-irradiation by 2.12-fold (p < 0.05).[⁶](#sources)
- **Campbell rats (retinitis pigmentosa model):** Preservation of all retinal layers; prolonged functional activity of retina by 43.9% (parabulbar injection) and up to ~2-fold (intraperitoneal to pregnant females).[²⁴](#sources)
- **Aged rats:** Increased SOD, glutathione peroxidase, and glutathione-S-transferase activities.[⁶](#sources)
- **Wistar rats (digestion):** 6–8-fold increase in active glucose transport in small intestine when administered orally.[²⁵](#sources)

#### Shown in Drosophila melanogaster

- Up to **16% increase in lifespan** of imago flies. Effective at concentrations ~16,000-fold lower than required for a comparable effect with melatonin.[²⁶](#sources)

#### Shown in Rhesus Monkeys (Macaca mulatta)

- Normalization of circadian melatonin and cortisol rhythms in aged monkeys (20–26 years old).[⁹](#sources)
- Improved glucose tolerance in aged monkeys (reduced glucose response area from 479.6 to 388.9 mM/min). Effect reversed after washout.[²⁷](#sources)

### 3.3 Human Studies

> **⚠️ Critical note:** All human data on Epitalon comes from studies conducted by the Khavinson group at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. These studies have **not been independently replicated** at other institutions. The following findings should be interpreted with appropriate caution.

#### Telomere Length (Human)
- Two clinical studies reported that Epitalon and epithalamin significantly increased telomere lengths in blood cells of patients aged 60–65 and 75–80. Efficacy was reported as comparable between the synthetic tetrapeptide and the crude extract. **Source:** Khavinson's 2015 presentation; limited peer-reviewed detail available.[²⁸](#sources)

#### Retinitis Pigmentosa Trial (Human)
- **162 patients**, aged 18–72, treated with 5.0 µg Epitalon per eye, parabulbarly, for 10 consecutive days.
- **Positive clinical effect in 90% of cases.** Visual acuity increased by 0.15–0.20 on average. Peripheral visual field borders broadened by 90–120 degrees in 64.8% of patients. Absolute scotomas reduced or disappeared.
- No side effects reported. No blinding described. No independent replication.
- Published: *Neuro Endocrinol Lett.* 2002.[²⁹](#sources)

#### Circadian Rhythm Study (Epitalon)
- **75 women** given 0.5 mg/day Epitalon sublingually for 20 days. Reported:
  - 1.6× increase in urinary 6-sulfatoxymelatonin (melatonin metabolite)
  - 1.8× decrease in Clock gene expression; 2× increase in Cry2 expression; 2.1× decrease in Csnk1e expression.
  - Published in Khavinson's collected works; limited independent peer-reviewed publication.[³²](#sources)

#### Mortality Studies Using Epithalamin (not Epitalon directly)
- A prospective cohort study of **266 people over age 60** treated with epithalamin (the bovine pineal extract) reported:
  - 1.6–1.8-fold reduction in mortality over 6 years (epithalamin alone)
  - 2.5-fold reduction when combined with thymalin
  - 4.1-fold reduction when combined with thymalin and administered annually
  - Published: *Neuro Endocrinol Lett.* 2003.[³⁰](#sources)

#### Coronary Artery Disease Study (Epithalamin)
- **79 coronary patients** followed for **>12 years**. Three years of biannual epithalamin treatments. Reported:
  - 50% lower cardiovascular mortality
  - 50% lower cardiovascular failure and serious respiratory disease
  - 28% lower overall mortality
  - Improved physical endurance, circadian rhythm, and carbohydrate/lipid metabolism
  - Published: *Bull Exp Biol Med.* 2011 (Korkushko et al.).[³³](#sources)

#### Pulmonary Tuberculosis Study
- Epitalon did not correct pre-existing chromosomal aberrations but exerted a protective effect against future development of additional aberrations.[³⁴](#sources)

## 4. Health Optimization Applications

> **⚠️ Disclaimer:** The following applications are described in the context of existing research literature only. Epitalon is a **research compound, not approved for human consumption** in most jurisdictions. No therapeutic claims are made.

### Longevity / Anti-Aging
The primary research focus of Epitalon is geroprotection. Evidence for longevity effects comes from:
- Extended lifespan in multiple rodent strains (mice, rats)
- Telomerase activation in human cells in vitro
- Reduced age-related biomarkers in animal models
- Mortality reduction in human observational cohorts (using epithalamin, not Epitalon directly)

### [[sleep|Sleep]] / Circadian Regulation
Epitalon has been shown to enhance melatonin synthesis in pinealocytes and normalize cortisol/melatonin circadian rhythms in aged primates. Human data from the 75-woman study suggests it may upregulate natural melatonin production through modulation of clock gene expression.

### [[immunity|Immune]] Function
Epitalon may support thymic function and immune cell populations, particularly in aging models. Effects on CD4+/CD8+ populations, IL-2 expression, and thymic serum factor have been observed in animal models.

### [[skin|Skin]] Aging
In vitro data suggests Epitalon inhibits MMP-9 (which degrades collagen) in aging skin fibroblasts. Reduced senescence markers (p16, p21) have been observed in cultured stem cells.

### [[eye-health|Eye]] Health
The retinitis pigmentosa clinical trial is the most substantial human data for Epitalon. Animal models (Campbell rats) showed preservation of retinal structure and function. However, this requires rigorous independent replication.

### Neuroprotection / [[cognition|Cognition]]
Evidence is preliminary: AChE/BuChE modulation, amyloid precursor protein processing, and neuroprotective effects in neuronal cultures have been observed in vitro. Animal studies suggest neuroprotective properties, but human data on cognitive outcomes is absent.

## 5. Safety Profile

> **⚠️ Critical Warning:** Long-term safety data for Epitalon is **essentially nonexistent**. The comprehensive 2025 review explicitly notes: *"Before Epitalon's approval as a new API, additional studies on its potential short- and long-term toxicity are essential. These future studies should also include aspects such as genotoxic activity, carcinogenic potential, and food–drug and drug–drug interactions."*[¹](#sources)

### Known / Reported Side Effects
- In the retinitis pigmentosa trial (162 patients, 10-day treatment), no side effects were reported. However, this was a short-duration study with limited safety reporting.
- In rodent studies, no overt toxicity, weight changes, or food consumption changes were reported over multi-month treatment periods.

### Contraindications
- **No formal contraindications have been established** due to lack of regulatory safety studies.

### Cancer Risk — Prominent Flag ⚠️
**This is a complex and unresolved issue.** Epitalon activates telomerase — an enzyme that is **also upregulated in ~85–90% of human cancers** and is considered a hallmark of cancer. While Epitalon's mechanism is proposed to restore telomerase to youthful levels (rather than over-activate it), the long-term consequences of chronic telomerase activation in humans are **completely unknown**.

The Khavinson group has argued that Epitalon has anti-cancer effects:
- Reduced spontaneous tumors in multiple mouse strains
- Reduced metastases in C3H/He mice
- 3.7-fold reduction in HER-2/neu oncogene expression in transgenic mice
- Reduced colon carcinogenesis in rat models

However, these findings come predominantly from **one research group** and have not been independently replicated. No long-term human cancer surveillance data exists. **The theoretical risk that chronic telomerase activation could promote pre-existing undetected malignancies cannot be ruled out.**

### Long-Term Safety
- **No chronic toxicity studies** (≥2 years in rodents, per standard regulatory requirements) have been published.
- **No carcinogenicity studies** per ICH guidelines have been published.
- **No reproductive toxicity studies** have been published.
- **No drug-drug interaction studies** have been published.
- The peptide's pharmacokinetics (absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion) are **not well-characterized** in humans.

### Metabolic Risks
No significant metabolic adverse effects have been reported in animal studies. In aged rhesus monkeys, Epitalon improved glucose tolerance (a beneficial effect). In rats, it enhanced intestinal glucose transport. The clinical significance and safety of these effects in humans is unknown.

## 6. Dosing Protocols

> **For educational and research context only. Not medical advice. Epitalon is not approved for human use by the FDA or EMA. The following information summarizes protocols used in published research studies.**

### Research Dosing From Published Studies

| Study Type | Route | Dose | Duration |
|------------|-------|------|----------|
| Mouse lifespan studies | Subcutaneous | 0.1–1 µg/mouse, 5×/week | Until natural death (months) |
| Rat antioxidant studies | Subcutaneous | 0.1–5 µg/rat, 5×/week | 5–14 days to months |
| Human retinitis pigmentosa | Parabulbar (eye) | 5 µg/eye/day | 10 consecutive days |
| Human circadian study | Sublingual | 0.5 mg/day | 20 days |
| Rodent geroprotection | Subcutaneous | ~0.1–1 µg/animal | Intermittent (5 days on, then off) or continuous |

### Community-Reported Protocols (Anecdotal, Not Validated)

The peptide enthusiast community has adopted protocols typically involving **short courses** (10–20 days) of subcutaneous injection, often at doses of **5–10 mg per course** (significantly higher than research doses). These protocols are:
- **Not derived from clinical trials**
- **Not safety-tested**
- **Inconsistent** with published research models
- **Not endorsed** by any regulatory body or medical authority

> **Important:** Animal research uses doses in the **microgram** range (0.1–5 µg). Community-reported protocols often use doses **1000× higher**. Extrapolating animal dosing to humans without pharmacokinetic data carries significant risk.

Routes of administration studied in research: subcutaneous injection (most common), intramuscular, intraperitoneal, intranasal, oral, sublingual, and parabulbar (eye). Bioavailability via oral route is not well-characterized; rodent studies show some oral activity.[²⁵](#sources)

## 7. Comparison with Related Peptides

| Peptide | Class | Mechanism | Key Evidence | Telomerase? |
|---------|-------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Epitalon (AEDG)** | Pineal tetrapeptide | Telomerase, antioxidant, pineal regulation | Mouse/rat lifespan, human telomere pilot, retinitis pigmentosa trial | **Yes** |
| **Epithalamin** (bovine pineal extract) | Pineal polypeptide complex | Same as Epitalon, plus broader peptide effects | Human mortality cohort studies (266 patients) | Yes (via Epitalon content) |
| **Pinealon (EDR)** | Pineal tripeptide | Neuroprotection, gene expression | Rat neuroprotection studies | **No** |
| **[[ghk-cu|GHK-Cu]]** | Copper peptide | Copper-dependent wound healing, SOD activation | Extensive wound/skin studies, some human | **No** |
| **TA-65** (Cycloastragenol) | Plant-derived small molecule | Telomerase activator (different mechanism) | Small human studies, commercialized | **Yes** |
| **Thymalin** (thymus extract) | Thymic polypeptide complex | Immune system regulation | Combined with epithalamin in mortality studies | No |

### Epitalon vs. Epithalamin
- Epitalon is a single, well-defined synthetic tetrapeptide (AEDG).
- Epithalamin is a complex polypeptide extract from bovine pineal glands containing multiple peptides, including Epitalon.
- Research suggests comparable efficacy between the two in some endpoints (telomere lengthening, melatonin restoration).
- Epitalon has the advantage of being a single molecular entity with defined structure — enabling precise dosing and mechanistic study.
- The human mortality data (1.6–4.1-fold reduction) comes from **epithalamin**, not Epitalon directly.

### Epitalon vs. Other Telomerase Activators
- Unlike TA-65 (a small-molecule plant derivative), Epitalon is a peptide and may have different tissue specificity and epigenetic effects.
- Unlike gene therapy approaches to telomerase, Epitalon is reversible and intermittent.
- The relative potency, safety, and efficacy of different telomerase activators have **never been compared in a head-to-head study**.

## 9. Confidence Assessment

### Overall Confidence: **LOW**

**Reasoning:**

1. **Researcher Concentration Risk (Major Concern):** The overwhelming majority of Epitalon research was produced by a single research group (Vladimir Khavinson and colleagues). While the body of work is extensive (spanning 25+ years and hundreds of publications), the lack of **independent replication** until very recently (2025) severely limits confidence. The Epitalon Wikipedia article's "fringe theories" flag reflects broader scientific community skepticism.

2. **Publication Quality Concerns:** Many key studies were published in *Bulletin of Experimental Biology and Medicine* — a journal that, while legitimate, has a lower impact factor than top-tier gerontology or molecular biology journals. Several critical human clinical findings appear primarily in presentations and book chapters rather than widely accessible peer-reviewed journals with detailed methods.

3. **Recent Independent Replication (Encouraging):** The 2025 papers by Al-Dulaimi et al., Gatta et al., and Ullah et al. provide the first **independent confirmation** of Epitalon's telomerase-activating effects from laboratories unaffiliated with Khavinson. This is a positive development but covers only *in vitro* work — no independent *in vivo* or human replication yet exists.

4. **Mechanism Uncertainty:** Even the comprehensive 2025 review states the precise mechanism "remains unverified." Whether telomerase activation is the primary mechanism, and what the full pharmacological profile looks like, is unknown.

5. **Safety Data Vacuum:** The absence of formal toxicology, carcinogenicity, and pharmacokinetic studies is a **critical gap**. The cancer risk (telomerase activation) cannot be dismissed without long-term data.

6. **Human Data Limitations:** The most compelling human data (mortality reduction) comes from epithalamin, not synthetic Epitalon. The retinitis pigmentosa study (the strongest Epitalon-specific human data) is a single unreplicated trial with limited methodological detail. Cohort studies with historical controls are susceptible to confounding.

**Areas of Higher Confidence:**
- Epitalon activates telomerase in human cells in vitro (replicated independently)
- Epitalon has antioxidant effects in animal models
- Epitalon extends lifespan in some rodent strains
- Epitalon's chemical identity and structure are well-defined

**Areas of Low Confidence:**
- Whether Epitalon extends human lifespan
- Whether telomerase activation is safe long-term
- Whether Epitalon has meaningful cognitive benefits
- Whether Epitalon has anti-cancer or pro-cancer effects in humans
- Pharmacokinetics and optimal dosing in humans
