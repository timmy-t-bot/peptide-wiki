# CJC-1295 (with DAC)

## Overview

CJC-1295 DAC (Drug Affinity Complex, also called DAC:GRF) is a synthetic analogue of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) developed by ConjuChem Biotechnologies. It is a tetrasubstituted 30-amino-acid peptide with a reactive maleimidopropionyl group on a C-terminal lysine that **covalently and irreversibly binds to serum albumin** after subcutaneous injection, extending the peptide's half-life from minutes to approximately 6–8 days.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

> **⚠️ CRITICAL DISTINCTION:** CJC-1295 (with DAC) is a **different molecule** from [[modified-grf-1-29]] (CJC-1295 without DAC). The DAC form has a covalent albumin-binding moiety that produces continuous GH elevation for days. The no-DAC form has a short half-life (~30 minutes) and produces pulsatile GH secretion. Several scientific papers have falsely equated the two (Thomas et al. 2015, PMID 26382721; Memdouh et al. 2021, PMID 34665524).^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Mechanism of Action

- **Class:** GHRH analogue (ghrh-analog)
- **Primary mechanism:** Binds to GHRH receptor (GHRHR) on pituitary somatotrophs, stimulating synthesis and release of growth hormone (GH)
- **Downstream effects:** GH stimulates hepatic IGF-1 production; IGF-1 mediates anabolic, lipolytic, and tissue-repair effects
- **Albumin conjugation:** The Nε-maleimidopropionyl-Lys³⁰ group reacts with the free thiol on Cys34 of serum albumin, forming a covalent thioether bond. This bioconjugation achieves three effects:
  1. Protects the peptide from proteolytic degradation (DPP-IV resistance demonstrated in vitro)
  2. Delays renal clearance (the peptide-albumin conjugate is too large for glomerular filtration)
  3. Creates a slow-release reservoir of bioactive peptide in circulation ^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **GH secretion pattern:** CJC-1295 DAC produces **continuous elevated GH** (a "GH bleed") rather than pulsatile secretion. Ionescu & Frohman 2006 (PMID 17018654) demonstrated that GH pulsatility is preserved but trough GH levels increase 7.5-fold, resulting in ~46% increase in mean GH and ~45% increase in IGF-1. The continuous elevation fundamentally differs from the physiological pulsatile pattern produced by short-acting GHRH analogues like [[modified-grf-1-29]] or [[tesamorelin]]^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Somatostatin regulation:** Partially bypassed — the sustained GH elevation overrides the normal somatostatin-mediated inhibition, creating a constant GH signal rather than the physiological on/off pattern ^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Research Evidence

### Human Evidence (Phase I–II Clinical Data)

**Teichman et al. 2006 (J Clin Endocrinol Metab)** — Two randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind, ascending-dose trials in healthy adults (PMID 16352683):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Single SC injection of CJC-1295 DAC produced **dose-dependent increases in plasma GH by 2- to 10-fold** sustained for ≥6 days
- **IGF-1 increases of 1.5- to 3-fold** sustained for 9–11 days
- Estimated half-life: **5.8–8.1 days**
- Multiple doses: IGF-1 levels remained elevated for **up to 28 days**
- No serious adverse reactions reported at 30 or 60 µg/kg
- Evidence of cumulative effect with repeat dosing

**Ionescu & Frohman 2006 (J Clin Endocrinol Metab)** — GH pulsatility study in healthy men (PMID 17018654):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- 20-min blood sampling during overnight 12h period, 1 week after single injection of 60 or 90 µg/kg
- GH pulsatility **preserved** (frequency and magnitude of pulses unchanged)
- Trough GH increased 7.5-fold (P < 0.0001)
- Mean GH increased 46% (P < 0.01); IGF-1 increased 45% (P < 0.001)
- No dose-response difference between 60 and 90 µg/kg

**Sackmann-Sala et al. 2009 (Growth Horm IGF Res)** — Proteomics in 11 healthy men (PMID 19386527):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Serum proteomics before and 1 week after CJC-1295
- Downregulated: ApoA1 isoform, transthyretin isoform
- Upregulated: β-hemoglobin, C-terminal albumin fragments
- Identified potential biomarkers of GH/IGF-1 action

### Mouse Model Evidence

**Jetté et al. 2005 (Endocrinology)** — Rat identification study (PMID 15817669):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Shown in rats: CJC-1295 produced 4-fold increase in GH AUC over 2h vs hGRF(1-29)
- Present in rat plasma beyond 72h
- Western blot confirmed CJC-1295-albumin conjugate appeared at 15 min, remained >24h
- All three maleimido-hGRF conjugates showed enhanced DPP-IV stability in vitro

**Alba et al. 2006 (Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab)** — GHRH knockout mouse model (PMID 16822960):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Shown in GHRH knockout mice: daily CJC-1295 dosing for 5 weeks **normalized body weight, length, femur and tibia length**
- Dosing every 48h or 72h was less effective (partial normalization only)
- Increased pituitary RNA and GH mRNA
- Confirmed somatotroph proliferation by immunohistochemistry
- Lean mass and subcutaneous fat mass normalized

**Mayfield et al. 2026 (Am J Sports Med)** — Narrative review (PMID 41476424):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Shown in murine models: CJC-1295 + ipamorelin significantly improved maximum tetanic tension in glucocorticoid-induced muscle loss
- Authors note: findings limited to animal studies only

### Anecdotal / Internet Community Evidence

**Van Hout & Hearne 2016 (Subst Use Misuse)** — Netnography of female forum users (PMID 26771670):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Analysis of 23 discussion threads from 9 bodybuilding forums
- Female users reported CJC-1295 use for: weight loss, muscle enhancement, youthful skin, improved sleep, injury healing
- Users demonstrated poly-pharmacy (stacking multiple performance/image drugs)
- Concerns: gender-specific GH pulsatility affecting dosing, unknown long-term consequences
- Note: The study did not clearly distinguish between DAC and no-DAC forms

### 2026 Review Consensus

Seven 2026 peer-reviewed reviews mention CJC-1295 (Mayfield PMID 41476424, Mendias & Awan PMID 41966639, Rahman et al. PMID 41490200, Villegas Meza et al. PMID 42160466, Coutinho et al. PMID 41880199, Mavrych et al. PMID 42021992, Renke & Chinellato PMID 42123471). Unanimous conclusions:^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- CJC-1295 is classified as **investigational/experimental**
- No rigorous human trials exist for musculoskeletal, sports medicine, or anti-aging applications
- Safety profiles are uncertain and based on small, short-term studies
- Gray market sourcing introduces product quality risks (mislabeling, contamination)
- WADA prohibits CJC-1295 in sport



## Safety Profile

### ⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING: Subject Death & Phase II Termination

**CJC-1295's clinical development was permanently terminated after a subject death occurred during the Phase II trial (NCT00267527).**^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

**Details of the event (August 2006):**
- Trial: Phase II, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled for HIV-associated visceral obesity
- Sponsor: ConjuChem Biotechnologies
- Enrollment: 192 patients
- Dosing: Once-weekly CJC-1295 SC for 12 weeks
- The deceased patient received the **11th weekly dose** on July 13, 2006
- Approximately 2 hours later: chest discomfort, ECG confirmed acute myocardial infarction
- Death occurred approximately **3 hours post-injection**
- Attending physician's assessment: "The most likely explanation for the event was that the patient had asymptomatic coronary artery disease with plaque rupture and occlusion, and that the occurrence was unrelated to treatment with CJC-1295"
- Despite the physician's assessment of no causal relationship, **ConjuChem terminated the entire Phase II study as a precaution**
- Source: ConjuChem press release, August 8, 2006 (archived at http://www.natap.org/2006/newsUpdates/081106_02.htm; Wayback Machine)

**Consequence:** No further clinical development of CJC-1295 DAC has occurred. The compound remains an investigational drug that never completed clinical testing and never received regulatory approval.

### Known Safety Concerns

1. **Chronic GH/IGF-1 elevation risks:** The extended half-life (6-8 days) creates sustained elevation of GH and IGF-1. Chronic GH/IGF-1 elevation is associated with:
   - Insulin resistance and hyperglycemia
   - Fluid retention, edema, carpal tunnel syndrome
   - Arthralgia and myalgia
   - Potential acceleration of neoplastic growth (theoretical — prolonged IGF-1 elevation)
   - Acromegalic features with prolonged supraphysiological dosing

2. **No long-term safety data:** The longest published human data is 28 days (Teichman et al. 2006). No data exist beyond this duration.

3. **Gray market sourcing risks:** Henninge et al. 2010 (PMID 21204297) identified CJC-1295 in a seized unknown pharmaceutical preparation analyzed by Norwegian customs. The peptide was being sold on the gray market before completing clinical development. Product identity, purity, and sterility are not verified.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

4. **GH receptor desensitization risk:** Continuous GH elevation (the "GH bleed" pattern) may theoretically cause GHRH receptor desensitization or tachyphylaxis, though this has not been formally studied. Short-acting GHRH analogues that produce pulsatile secretion patterns (e.g., [[modified-grf-1-29]], [[tesamorelin]]) may carry lower risk of receptor downregulation.

5. **Interaction risks:** No monoaminergic, opioidergic, or serotonergic activity identified — the mechanism is exclusively GHRHR-mediated. However, additive effects with other GH secretagogues (ipamorelin, GHRPs) could amplify all safety risks. No known direct drug-drug interactions.

6. **2026 review findings:** Mendias & Awan 2026 (PMID 41966639) and Coutinho et al. 2026 (PMID 41880199) both emphasize that unapproved peptides like CJC-1295 carry "potential for serious harm to patients" and lack the safety validation required for clinical use.



## Legal Status

| Jurisdiction | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| **United States (FDA)** | Not FDA-approved. An investigational drug with a terminated development program. Not a scheduled controlled substance but falls under FDA category of unapproved new drug. Gray market products are sold with "research chemical — not for human consumption" labeling. |
| **WADA/Anti-Doping** | Prohibited under **Section S2** (Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances, and Mimetics). Banned both in-competition and out-of-competition. Detection methods published for both human urine/plasma and equine plasma.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md] |
| **Equine Sports** | Detection methods developed for equine plasma (Timms et al. 2019, PMID 30489688 and PMID 30938069). Confirmed abuse potential in horse racing. |
| **Gray Market** | Widely available through unregulated peptide vendors despite terminated development. The 2010 Norwegian seizure (Henninge et al. PMID 21204297) and 2016 forensic analysis of doping forums (Pineau et al. PMID 27710891) confirm extensive gray market distribution.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md] |
| **General** | Not approved for any medical indication in any country. No legitimate pharmaceutical manufacturer produces CJC-1295 DAC. All available products are from unregulated sources. |



## Dosing Protocols (Research Context Only)

> **⚠️ FOR EDUCATIONAL PURPOSES ONLY. CJC-1295 DAC is an experimental compound with terminated clinical development. It has no approved human use. A subject death occurred during the Phase II trial. No established clinical dosing protocol exists.**^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Published Research Dosing (Human)

From Teichman et al. 2006 (PMID 16352683):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Single dose:** 30 or 60 µg/kg SC (well tolerated; no serious adverse events)
- **Multiple dose:** 30 or 60 µg/kg SC, weekly or biweekly
- **Duration:** 28–49 days (no longer-term data)
- **GH effect:** 2- to 10-fold elevation for ≥6 days after single dose
- **IGF-1 effect:** 1.5- to 3-fold elevation for 9–11 days after single dose

From Ionescu & Frohman 2006 (PMID 17018654):^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Single dose:** 60 or 90 µg/kg SC
- GH trough increased 7.5-fold at 1 week post-injection
- No significant dose-response difference between 60 and 90 µg/kg

### Anecdotal / Community-Derived Dosing (Unverified)

The following protocols are derived from bodybuilding and longevity forums (synthesized from Van Hout & Hearne 2016, PMID 26771670 and general community consensus). **These are NOT evidence-based and carry unknown risks.**

- **Typical anecdotal dose:** 1–2 mg per week, administered in 1–2 divided doses
- **Common stack:** CJC-1295 DAC + ipamorelin (a GHRP/ghrelin mimetic) — intended to produce synergistic GH release
- **Cycle duration:** 8–12 weeks, followed by break period
- **Rationale for DAC choice:** Users who prefer DAC cite the convenience of once- or twice-weekly dosing vs. multiple daily injections for the no-DAC form
- **Storage:** Lyophilized powder stored frozen; reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, refrigerated

**No clinical trial has ever validated any community-derived dosing protocol for CJC-1295 DAC.** The Phase II trial (NCT00267527) used once-weekly dosing under clinical supervision, and that trial was terminated after a subject death.



## Comparison with Related Peptides

### CJC-1295 DAC vs. Modified GRF (1-29) (No-DAC)

| Feature | CJC-1295 DAC | [[modified-grf-1-29]] (No DAC) |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **DAC moiety** | Present (Nε-maleimidopropionyl-Lys³⁰) | Absent |
| **Albumin binding** | Covalent, irreversible | None |
| **Half-life (human)** | 5.8–8.1 days | ~30 minutes (estimated) |
| **GH secretion pattern** | Sustained "GH bleed" for 6–14+ days | Pulsatile, mimics physiology |
| **Clinical trials** | Phase I + Phase II (terminated after death) | None |
| **Human PK data** | Published (Teichman 2006) | None |
| **Dosing frequency** | Weekly | 2–3× daily (typical community protocol) |
| **Risk of receptor desensitization** | Higher (continuous activation) | Lower (pulsatile) |
| **Regulatory status** | Terminated investigational drug | Research chemical |
| **WADA status** | Prohibited S2 | Prohibited S2 |

The two forms are **different molecules** with fundamentally different pharmacology. The DAC form's continuous GH elevation is pharmacologically more similar to exogenous GH administration (which also produces sustained elevation) than to physiological pulsatile GHRH signaling. This makes the safety profiles distinct — risks associated with prolonged GH elevation (insulin resistance, fluid retention, theoretical neoplastic concerns) are more relevant to the DAC form.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

### CJC-1295 DAC vs. Tesamorelin

| Feature | CJC-1295 DAC | [[tesamorelin]] (Egrifta) |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Structure** | 29-aa GHRH analogue + DAC-Lys³⁰ | 44-aa GHRH + trans-3-hexenoic acid |
| **FDA approval** | No (terminated) | Yes (2010) |
| **Half-life** | 5.8–8.1 days | 26–38 minutes |
| **GH secretion pattern** | Continuous (6+ days) | Pulsatile (hours) |
| **Evidence level** | Phase I–II (terminated) | Phase III, meta-analysis, FDA-approved |
| **Safety data** | <30 days in humans; 1 death in trial | >12 months continuous use; Phase III safety database |
| **Approved indication** | None | HIV-associated lipodystrophy |
| **Dosing** | Weekly SC | Daily SC (1.4–2 mg) |

Tesamorelin is the **only GHRH analogue with FDA approval** and robust Phase III evidence. Its pulsatile GH secretion pattern, daily dosing schedule, and established safety database make it a fundamentally different risk/benefit proposition from CJC-1295 DAC. Tesamorelin is produced under pharmaceutical GMP conditions; CJC-1295 DAC is only available through unregulated gray market sources.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

### CJC-1295 DAC vs. Sermorelin

| Feature | CJC-1295 DAC | Sermorelin (Geref) |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Structure** | Modified 29-aa + DAC-Lys³⁰ | Unmodified GHRH(1-29) |
| **Substitutions** | D-Ala², Gln⁸, Ala¹⁵, Leu²⁷ | None (native sequence) |
| **Half-life** | 5.8–8.1 days | ~11–12 minutes |
| **Approval history** | Never approved | Previously FDA-approved; now discontinued |
| **Current availability** | Gray market only | No longer commercially available |

Sermorelin was the original FDA-approved GHRH analogue (for pediatric GH deficiency diagnosis and treatment), but was discontinued by the manufacturer. Its very short half-life (minutes) required frequent injection and limited its clinical utility. CJC-1295 DAC's albumin-binding strategy was designed to solve this half-life problem but introduced the safety concerns of continuous GH elevation. The tetrasubstituted "no-DAC" form ([[modified-grf-1-29]]) was intended as an improved sermorelin without the DAC risks.^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]



## Summary Assessment

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — CJC-1295 DAC has published human PK/PD data from Phase I trials and one terminated Phase II trial, providing solid acute pharmacokinetic characterization. However, long-term safety is unknown, the development program was terminated after a subject death, and no data exist for any of the indications for which it is currently marketed on the gray market (anti-aging, body recomposition, injury recovery).^[raw/articles/cjc-1295-dac-research-2026-06-09.md]

**Strengths of the evidence:**
- Published Phase I human PK/PD data (Teichman et al. 2006, Ionescu & Frohman 2006)
- Well-characterized mechanism (albumin bioconjugation with covalent bonding)
- Animal efficacy data in GHRH knockout model (Alba et al. 2006)
- Multiple independent detection methods developed (WADA anti-doping relevance)

**Critical weaknesses:**
- **Phase II trial terminated after subject death**
- No human data beyond 49 days
- No data for any currently marketed indication (anti-aging, bodybuilding)
- No comparative studies against FDA-approved alternatives (tesamorelin)
- Gray market products have unverified identity, purity, and sterility
- The sustained "GH bleed" pattern raises theoretical concerns about receptor desensitization and chronic GH/IGF-1 elevation risks
- The DAC moiety creates permanent albumin adducts, potentially complicating long-term clearance



## Gaps in Knowledge

1. Long-term safety (>2 months) — completely unknown
2. Carcinogenicity risk with prolonged IGF-1 elevation — unstudied
3. GH receptor desensitization/tachyphylaxis with continuous stimulation — unstudied
4. Dose-response for repeated dosing beyond 28 days — unknown
5. Efficacy for any gray market indication (body recomposition, anti-aging, injury recovery) — no data
6. Comparison with FDA-approved tesamorelin — not studied
7. Female-specific PK/PD — limited to anecdotal netnography (Van Hout & Hearne 2016)
8. Clearance and long-term fate of albumin-peptide adducts — unknown
9. Effect of DAC conjugation on immunogenicity — unstudied
