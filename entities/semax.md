# Semax

**Semax** (Russian: СЕМАКС, an abbreviation of "seven amino acids") is a synthetic heptapeptide with the sequence **Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro** (MEHFPGP). It is a synthetic analogue of the adrenocorticotropic hormone fragment ACTH(4-10), modified with a Pro-Gly-Pro C-terminal tripeptide that enhances stability and biological activity.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

Developed in Russia in the late 1980s/early 1990s by the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences, Semax is approved as a prescription drug in Russia and is included on the Russian List of Vital & Essential Drugs (2011). It has not been evaluated or approved by the FDA, EMA, or any other Western regulatory body.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

## Overview

- **Molecular formula:** C₃₇H₅₁N₉O₁₀S (MW: 813.93 g/mol)
- **CAS:** 80714-61-0
- **Sequence:** Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro
- **Class:** Synthetic ACTH(4-10) analogue; nootropic / neuroprotective peptide
- **Origin:** Developed at the Institute of Molecular Genetics, Russian Academy of Sciences (first described 1991)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Route:** Intranasal (1% solution) or subcutaneous injection; **poor oral bioavailability** due to peptide structure
- **ATC Code:** N06BX (Other psychostimulants and nootropics)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Status (Russia):** Approved prescription drug; on the Russian List of Vital & Essential Drugs since 2011^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Status (US/International):** Not FDA approved; unscheduled in the US; widely sold online by nootropic vendors as a "research chemical." Not on WADA Prohibited List explicitly, but qualifies as a non-approved substance under S0 catch-all language.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Related compounds:** Selank (anxiolytic tuftsin analogue with same Pro-Gly-Pro tail); Adamax (N-acetyl Semax variant with claimed enhanced stability)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

> **⚠️ For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.** Semax is an unapproved pharmaceutical outside Russia. Self-administration carries unknown risks.

## Mechanism of Action

The exact mechanism of Semax is **not fully understood**. It operates through multiple intersecting pathways with no single identified primary receptor.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Melanocortin Receptor Modulation
Semax is structurally derived from ACTH(4-10), which acts on melanocortin receptors. Evidence indicates Semax **competitively antagonizes α-MSH at MC₄ and MC₅ receptors** in both in vitro and in vivo conditions, suggesting antagonist or partial agonist activity at these receptors. It did not antagonize α-MSH at MC₃, though MC₃ remains a possible target. MC₁ and MC₂ were not assayed.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md] Melanocortin receptor modulation links to Semax's neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory, and cognitive effects.

### BDNF/TrkB System
Semax rapidly elevates levels and expression of **brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)** and its signaling receptor **tropomyosin receptor kinase B (TrkB)** in the hippocampus. A single intranasal application in rats (50 μg/kg) increased BDNF protein 1.4-fold, TrkB phosphorylation 1.6-fold, and BDNF exon III/TrkB mRNA 2-3 fold.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md] This was confirmed in humans: the 2018 post-stroke trial demonstrated Semax increased plasma BDNF levels, correlating with improved functional recovery (Barthel Index).^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Enkephalinase Inhibition
Semax inhibits enzymes that degrade enkephalins and other endogenous regulatory peptides in human serum (**IC₅₀ = 10 μM**). The related peptide Selank has a similar effect (IC₅₀ = 20 μM).^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md] The clinical significance of this property is uncertain but may contribute to prolonged activity of endogenous opioid peptides and mood effects.

### Dopaminergic & Serotonergic Activation
In rodent models, Semax rapidly activates both **dopaminergic and serotonergic** brain systems. It potentiates the locomotor activity produced by D-amphetamine, consistent with dopaminergic facilitation.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### GABA & Glycine Current Modulation
In isolated rat cerebral neurons, Semax (1 μM) **augmented GABA-activated ionic currents** in cerebellar Purkinje cells by 147% and **diminished glycine-activated chloride currents** in hippocampal pyramidal neurons to 43-68% of control levels.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md] These effects developed slowly and were poorly reversible, suggesting involvement of second messenger systems.

### μ-Opioid Receptor Interaction
A 2025 study in female mice demonstrated that Semax targets the **μ-opioid receptor gene Oprm1**, regulating deubiquitination via USP18 and promoting functional recovery after spinal cord injury. Network pharmacology and molecular docking confirmed μ-opioid receptor as a Semax target.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Copper Chelation & Aβ Redox Silencing
Semax contains a His residue that gives it high affinity for Cu(II) ions. It can **extract Cu(II) from Cu(II)-Aβ complexes**, influence redox cycling, and decrease associated ROS production, showing cytoprotective effects on SH-SY5Y cells against oxidative stress from copper-catalyzed Aβ oxidation.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Transcriptome-Level Neuroprotection
RNA-Seq studies in the rat tMCAO stroke model demonstrate that Semax **compensates for ischemia-disrupted gene expression profiles**: it suppresses inflammatory gene expression and restores neurotransmission-related gene expression. The neuroprotective transcriptome effects involve nearly 1,171-2,000 genes across brain regions, including neurogenesis, angiogenesis, protein kinase, and growth factor-related pathways.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Intracellular Calcium Dynamics
Semax (1 μM) significantly increases the frequency of spontaneous intracellular calcium fluctuations in hippocampal CA1 pyramidal neurons in rat brain slices, providing insight into its rapid effects on neuronal network activity.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Animal Evidence (Rat & Mouse Models)

Animal evidence for Semax is **extensive** with over 200 publications spanning three decades. Key findings include:

**Cognitive Enhancement:**
- Improved learning and conditioned avoidance reactions in rats^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- Attenuation of behavioral effects of neonatal maternal deprivation^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Mood & Stress:**
- Antidepressant-like and anxiolytic-like effects in chronic unpredictable stress models^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- Attenuation of chronic stress behavioral effects^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- Reduced corticosterone and stress-induced gut pathology^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Stroke & Ischemia (tMCAO rat model):**
- Transcriptome-level neuroprotection: suppression of inflammatory genes, restoration of neurotransmission genes (1,171-2,000 DEGs)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- Neurogenesis, angiogenesis, protein kinase, and growth factor-related gene activation^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- Differential spatial regulation in striatum vs. frontal cortex^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Spinal Cord Injury (mouse model):**
- Functional recovery via μ-opioid receptor targeting, USP18 deubiquitination, FTO pathway (2025)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Alzheimer's Disease (transgenic mouse):**
- Reduced amyloid plaques in cortex and hippocampus in APPswe/PS1dE9/Blg mice (2025)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- Improved cognitive function: open field, novel object recognition, Barnes maze^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Gut Protection:**
- Alleviated stress-induced colon pathology; normalized microbiota composition in restraint stress^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Human Evidence

Human evidence for Semax is **limited but includes some clinical trial data**, predominantly from Russian research groups.

**Clinical Trial (n=110 post-stroke patients — Gusev et al., 2018):**
- 110 patients after ischemic stroke (43 men, 67 women, mean age 58.0 ± 9.7)
- Semax regimen: 6,000 mcg/day intranasal, 2 × 10-day courses with 20-day interval
- **Results:** Semax increased BDNF plasma levels which remained elevated throughout the study; accelerated improvement and improved final Barthel Index scores; positive correlation between BDNF levels and functional recovery^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Conclusion:** Early rehabilitation + Semax increased BDNF, speeded functional recovery, improved motor performance

**fMRI Studies (Healthy Volunteers):**
- **Lebedeva et al. (2018)** — n=24 healthy volunteers (11 men, 13 women, age 43.9 ± 9.5): Single intranasal Semax 1% increased volume of the default mode network (DMN) rostral subcomponent (medial frontal cortex) vs. placebo at 5 and 20 min post-administration^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Panikratova et al. (2020)** — n=52 healthy participants: Semax and Selank compared; both modulated functional connectivity between right amygdala and right temporal cortex, with distinct patterns. Semax effects on DLPFC connectivity also noted^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Russian Clinical Experience:**
Semax has been used clinically in Russia since the 1990s for: stroke, transient ischemic attack, cognitive disorders, memory impairment, optic nerve disease (glaucomatous optic neuropathy), peptic ulcers, and immune system support. It is on the Russian Vital & Essential Drugs list. However, much of this clinical evidence is published in Russian-language journals with limited accessibility and methodological detail in English.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

## Health Optimization Applications

> **All applications below are described in a research context. Semax is not approved for any of these uses outside Russia.**

### Cognitive Enhancement (Research Context)
The strongest signal from animal and human evidence. Semax enhances BDNF/TrkB signaling, modulates the default mode network, and improves learning in animal models. The fMRI data in healthy humans showing DMN modulation within 5-20 minutes of intranasal administration is notable, though the functional significance of these connectivity changes for real-world cognitive performance is unestablished.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Neuroprotection & Post-Stroke Recovery
The 2018 n=110 human stroke trial provides the best human evidence for Semax, demonstrating enhanced BDNF levels and functional recovery. The extensive rat tMCAO transcriptome data (2020-2025) provides a molecular basis for this effect.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Mood & Stress Resilience
Rodent data consistently shows antidepressant-like and anxiolytic-like effects via dopaminergic/serotonergic activation and stress hormone modulation. A 2008 commentary in *CNS Spectrums* hypothesized antidepressant potential.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md] However, no human depression trials have been conducted.

### Alzheimer's Disease (Preclinical)
The 2025 transgenic mouse study showing reduced amyloid plaques and cognitive improvement is promising but extremely early-stage. The copper-chelating mechanism (2025 in vitro study) provides a complementary rationale. No human AD data.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

## Safety Profile

### Known Safety Data
- **Low acute toxicity** demonstrated in mouse embryonic stem cell models (Kobylyanskii et al., 2017)^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Clinical use in Russia since the 1990s** without major safety signals — however, Russian pharmacovigilance data is not readily accessible to Western researchers
- The 2018 post-stroke trial (n=110) reported no serious adverse events attributed to Semax, though detailed safety reporting standards differ from Western RCTs^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Safety Concerns & Unknowns

⚠️ **Major Unknowns:**
1. **μ-opioid receptor activity:** The 2025 discovery of μ-opioid receptor targeting raises theoretical concerns about dependence, tolerance, or withdrawal — completely unstudied in humans. This is the most significant new safety finding.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
2. **No Western regulatory evaluation:** Semax has never undergone FDA or EMA review. Manufacturing standards, purity, and quality control for online-sourced Semax are unknown.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
3. **No long-term human safety data in English-language literature:** Decades of Russian clinical use exist but are not systematically documented in accessible databases.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
4. **Melanocortin receptor pharmacology:** MC₄/MC₅ receptor effects are incompletely characterized. MC₄ antagonism could theoretically affect appetite, energy homeostasis, and sexual function.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
5. **Dopaminergic/serotonergic interactions:** Given documented dopaminergic and serotonergic activation in animal models (including D-amphetamine potentiation), concurrent use with SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, or stimulants is theoretically contraindicated — though no interaction studies exist.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
6. **Drug interactions:** No systematic interaction studies exist. Interaction with antidepressants, stimulants, or other nootropics is unknown.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
7. **Online vendor sourcing:** Semax sold online as a "research chemical" is unregulated. Vanhee et al. (2020) confirmed Semax and Selank in seized pharmaceutical preparations from online vendors. Purity, sterility, and authenticity are buyer-beware.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Contraindications
No formally established contraindications. Theoretical caution in: pregnancy/breastfeeding, seizure disorders, psychiatric conditions with dopaminergic sensitivity, concurrent opioid use, concurrent use with SSRIs/SNRIs/MAOIs or stimulants (given documented dopaminergic and serotonergic activation in animal models).

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)

> **For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.**

**Russian Clinical Protocol (Gusev et al., 2018, post-stroke):**
- 6,000 mcg/day intranasal (1% Semax solution)
- 2 courses of 10 days each, with a 20-day interval between courses^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Animal Studies:**
- 50 μg/kg intranasal (single dose) — BDNF/TrkB upregulation in rats^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- 5-450 μg/kg intraperitoneal — stress protection in rats^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- 1 μM in perfusion solution — electrophysiology studies in rat neurons^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**fMRI Human Studies:**
- Single intranasal dose of 1% Semax solution (exact mcg not specified in abstract); effects measured at 5 and 20 min post-administration^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Community / Online Patterns (Anecdotal):**
- Typical nootropic community doses range from 200-1,000 mcg intranasally, often as a 0.1% or 1% nasal spray
- Some users report twice-daily administration; others use as-needed for cognitive tasks
- **Note:** Community dosing is anecdotally reported and not validated by clinical research

### Administration
Semax has **poor oral bioavailability** and is administered intranasally (via nasal spray) or by subcutaneous injection. The intranasal route is preferred for CNS targeting due to direct access via the olfactory epithelium.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

## Comparison with Related Peptides

### Semax vs. [[Selank]]
These are often discussed together as a pair of Russian nootropic/anxiolytic peptides sharing the Pro-Gly-Pro C-terminal tail:

| Feature | Semax | Selank (see [[comparison page]]) |
|---------|-------|------|
| **Parent molecule** | ACTH(4-10) | Tuftsin (immunoglobulin G fragment) |
| **Sequence** | Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro | Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro |
| **Primary effect** | Nootropic / neuroprotective / stimulant-like | Anxiolytic / anti-anxiety |
| **Melanocortin** | MC₄/MC₅ antagonist/partial agonist | Not melanocortin-mediated |
| **Enkephalinase** | IC₅₀ = 10 μM | IC₅₀ = 20 μM |
| **BDNF** | Strong upregulation | Less studied for BDNF |
| **fMRI (human)** | DMN modulation, DLPFC connectivity | Amygdala-temporal FC modulation |
| **Russian approval** | Yes (Vital & Essential Drugs) | Yes (prescription anxiolytic) |

Both peptides are available from the same online vendors and are frequently stacked together by nootropic users.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Semax vs. ACTH(6-9)PGP
Filippenkov et al. (2024-2025) compared Semax (ACTH(4-7)PGP) with a shorter ACTH(6-9)PGP peptide. Both showed neuroprotective transcriptome effects, but Semax affected more genes and showed more consistent compensation of ischemia-disrupted expression profiles. ACTH(6-9)PGP caused additional inflammatory gene expression in the striatum that was not seen with Semax.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

### Semax vs. [[BPC-157]]
Both are synthetic peptides with neuroprotective claims, but with fundamentally different origins and mechanisms:
- **BPC-157** derives from gastric juice proteins; operates via VEGF/angiogenesis, NO modulation, FAK-paxillin
- **Semax** derives from ACTH; operates via melanocortin receptors, BDNF, enkephalinase inhibition
- Both have extensive rat data, limited human data, and are sold by research chemical vendors
- [[BPC-157]] has broader tissue repair claims (gut, tendon, bone); Semax is CNS-focused
- Neither is FDA approved

## Confidence Assessment

**Confidence: LOW**

**Justification:**

1. **Single-research-group dominance:** The vast majority of Semax research originates from the Institute of Molecular Genetics (Moscow) and affiliated groups (Myasoedov, Andreeva, Grivennikov, Levitskaya). While recent independent work from Chinese (Liu et al., 2025 — SCI/mu-opioid), Italian (Tomasello et al., 2025 — Cu chelation), and Belgian (Vanhee et al., 2020) groups is encouraging, the core clinical evidence remains Russian-group-led.

2. **Limited human evidence:** Despite ~30 years of clinical use in Russia, the English-language human evidence base consists of one post-stroke trial (n=110), two small fMRI studies (total n=76), and a commentary letter. No randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Western-style RCT exists.

3. **Mechanism incompletely understood:** Semax's mechanism is described as "unknown" even in authoritative sources. It appears to hit multiple targets (melanocortin receptors, enkephalinase, BDNF, Cu chelation, μ-opioid, GABA/glycine currents), but no single, definitive primary mechanism has been established.

4. **No Western regulatory evaluation:** Unlike drugs that have undergone FDA/EMA scrutiny, Semax's safety and efficacy have never been independently assessed by a major Western regulator.

5. **Emerging independent replication (positive signal):** The 2025 publications from non-Russian labs (Italy, China) represent the beginning of independent mechanistic validation — a positive development, but still early.

**Contrast with [[epitalon]]:** Both are Russian-developed peptides with decades of Russian clinical use, limited Western data, and single-group research dominance. Both rate LOW confidence for similar reasons. ^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]

**Contrast with [[bpc-157]]:** BPC-157 has comparable limitations (single Croatian group dominance, limited human data) but has marginally more independent replication from non-originator labs (e.g., Yildirim 2026, various Asian groups) and 3 small human pilot studies. BPC-157 also rates LOW confidence.^[raw/articles/semax-sources-2026-06-09.md]
