# GHRP-6

## Overview
GHRP-6 (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide-6) is a synthetic hexapeptide analogue of met-enkephalin. It acts as a potent secretagogue of growth hormone by targeting the ghrelin receptor.

**Sequence:** His-D-Trp-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH₂

**Legal Status:** Research chemical; no regulatory approval for human therapeutic use.

## Mechanism of Action
- **Primary Target:** GHS-R1a (Ghrelin receptor).
- **Signaling:** Potently stimulates GH release. Unlike GHRH, it does not require GHRH to be effective, but they act synergistically.
- **Appetite:** Strong activation of NPY/AgRP neurons in the arcuate nucleus, leading to intense hunger.

## Research Evidence

### Animal Models
- **Tissue Protection:** Emerging 2025/2026 data suggests protective effects against acute kidney injury (AKI) via mTOR-P70 and lung protection in ARDS/fibrosis models.
- **Metabolism:** Robust data on body composition and appetite stimulation in rodent models.

### Human Studies
- **PK/PD:** Early human trials (1990s) and a small PK study (2013) establish its ability to elevate GH.
- **Clinical Trials:** A Phase I/II trial (2024) investigated its use in stroke recovery, though results are not yet widely disseminated.

## Health Optimization Applications
- **GH Elevation:** Research use for increasing GH and IGF-1.
- **Appetite Stimulation:** Used in research contexts for cachexia or severe appetite loss.

## Safety Profile
- **Hyperphagia:** Extreme appetite stimulation can lead to overeating and metabolic stress.
- **Cortisol/ACTH:** Like GHRP-2, it can elevate cortisol, which may oppose some of the benefits of GH.
- **Sourcing Risks:** Unregulated gray-market purity and thermal degradation.
- **Long-term Data:** None.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)
- **Common Research Dose:** 100-300 mcg.
- **Administration:** Subcutaneous.
- **Disclaimer:** For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.

## Comparison with Related Peptides
- **vs GHRP-2:** GHRP-6 is a significantly stronger appetite stimulant. GHRP-2 is generally a more potent GH secretagogue.
- **vs Ipamorelin:** Ipamorelin is far more selective and does not cause the intense hunger associated with GHRP-6.
