# Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide (VIP)

## Overview
- Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide (VIP) is a 28-amino acid neuropeptide found in the nervous system and endocrine cells throughout the body.
- Sequence: HPMAPHSN (simplified)
- Discovered in the late 1960s; known for potent vasodilatory and immunomodulatory effects.
- Status: Naturally occurring peptide; synthetic versions are used primarily in research contexts.

## Mechanism of Action
- **Primary Target:** Acts via three G-protein coupled receptors: VPAC1, VPAC2, and VPAC1/2.
- **Signaling:** Increases intracellular cAMP levels via adenylate cyclase activation.
- **Effects:** 
  - In the cardiovascular system, it induces potent vasodilation.
  - In the immune system, it inhibits T-cell activation and reduces pro-inflammatory cytokine production.
  - In the brain, it modulates circadian rhythms in the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN).

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies
- Demonstrated inhibition of TNF-alpha and IL-2 production in T-cells.

### Animal Models (Mouse/Rat)
- Strong evidence for systemic vasodilation and protection against ischemia-reperfusion injury.
- Shown to reduce inflammation in colitis models.

### Human Studies
- Limited clinical data; some research into COPD treatment and autoimmune modulation, but not widely adopted as a systemic therapy.
- Human evidence for longevity/optimization is primarily anecdotal.

## Health Optimization Applications
- **Immune Modulation:** Potential for reducing systemic inflammation.
- **Cardiovascular Health:** Theoretical use for improving blood flow (though risky).
- **Cognitive/Sleep:** Potential modulator of circadian rhythms.

## Safety Profile
- **Known Side Effects:** Exogenous administration can cause severe hypotension (low blood pressure) and reflex tachycardia.
- **Contraindications:** Should not be used in individuals with cardiovascular instability.
- **Long-term Safety:** Unknown long-term effects of systemic synthetic VIP administration.
- **Disclaimer:** For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)
- Research doses vary widely between systemic and localized (nasal) administration.
- Route: Typically subcutaneous or intravenous in research settings.

## Comparison with Related Peptides
- Compared to other vasodilators, VIP has a more complex immunomodulatory profile.
- Differs from GHRH-analogs as it focuses on vascular and immune regulation rather than GH axis.
