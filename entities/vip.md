# Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide (VIP)

## Overview

Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) is a 28-amino acid neuropeptide belonging to the glucagon/secretin superfamily of class II G protein-coupled receptor ligands.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] It is produced in many vertebrate tissues including the gut, pancreas, neocortex, and suprachiasmatic nuclei (SCN) of the hypothalamus. VIP is encoded by the _VIP_ gene on human chromosome 6.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

VIP has a remarkably short half-life in blood of approximately **two minutes**, which presents a significant challenge for therapeutic development.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] The synthetic pharmaceutical form is called **aviptadil** (INN), developed primarily for respiratory indications.

VIP is structurally and functionally related to pituitary adenylate cyclase-activating peptide (PACAP), sharing receptors (VPAC1, VPAC2) but differing in migraine-inducing properties and receptor affinity profiles.

## Mechanism of Action

### Receptor Signaling

VIP binds to two high-affinity G protein-coupled receptors: **VPAC1** and **VPAC2**. Both receptors have comparable affinity for VIP and PACAP. VIP also binds PAC1 with lower affinity than PACAP.

Upon receptor binding, VIP activates a G-alpha-mediated signaling cascade:
1. **Adenylyl cyclase activation** → increased intracellular cAMP
2. **PKA activation** → phosphorylation of CREB and other transcriptional factors
3. **Gene expression modulation** — including circadian rhythm regulation

In some vascular tissues, VIP's vasodilatory effects may also involve secondary signaling pathways.

### Immune Modulation

VIP has pleiotropic immunoregulatory functions. It is released by both neurons and immune cells, and immune cells express VIP receptors.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] Key immunomodulatory actions include:

- **Suppression of pro-inflammatory cytokines** (TNF-α, IL-6)
- **Induction of regulatory T cells (Tregs)** — VIP converts naive CD4⁺CD25⁻ T cells into CD4⁺CD25⁺FoxP3⁺ Tregs^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Inhibition of humoral immune responses**
- **Attenuation of vascular and bronchial remodeling**^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Circadian Rhythm Regulation

In the SCN, VIP is the **major synchronizing agent** among individual neuronal oscillators. VIP-expressing neurons in the ventrolateral SCN (the "core") receive retinal input via the retinohypothalamic tract and relay environmental light information to the SCN network.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

Shown in mouse models: VIP or VPAC2 receptor deficiency causes loss of synchronized circadian oscillations. VIP normally amplifies and phase-delays arginine vasopressin (AVP) neuronal rhythms each day, lengthening their period to approximately 24 hours.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] A recently characterized **SCN Neuronal Feedback Loop (SNFL)** — composed of the AVP cellular oscillator and VIPergic signaling — is essential for generating robust circadian rhythms.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies

- **SARS-CoV-2 entry inhibition**: In CaCo-2 epithelial cells, VIP downregulated ACE2 and TMPRSS2 mRNA and surface expression, and mediated shedding of both proteins via ADAM10 sheddase upregulation. This dual mechanism reduced SARS-CoV-2 pseudovirus infection rate.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Gutzler et al., 2025)
- **Treg induction**: VIP converted naive CD4⁺CD25⁻ T cells into CD4⁺CD25⁺FoxP3⁺ regulatory T cells in vitro.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Prasse et al., 2010)
- **Mast cell interaction**: Mast cells express VPAC receptors and internalize VIP. (Casado-Bedmar et al., 2019)
- **Alveolar cell protection**: VIP attenuated cigarette smoke extract-induced apoptotic death of alveolar L2 cells.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Mathioudakis et al., 2013)

### Animal Models (Mouse/Rat)

- **Circadian disruption**: Shown in mouse models: VIP-knockout or VPAC2-knockout mice lose synchronized SCN circadian oscillations and exhibit disrupted behavioral rhythms.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Vosko et al., 2007; Wang et al., 2026)
- **SCN network**: Shown in mouse models: GABAergic regulation from AVP neurons to VIP neurons sets the timing of circadian behavior. Blocking GABA release from AVP neurons lengthened activity time; eliminating GABA-A receptors in VIP neurons reduced morning locomotor activity.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Peng et al., 2026)
- **Cardioprotection**: Shown in animal models: VIP and PACAP application had beneficial effects in hypertension, heart failure, and myocardial fibrosis. PACAP inhibited proliferation of cardiac fibroblasts.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Dvoráková, 2005)
- **Osteoarthritis**: Shown in animal models: VIP prevented cartilage damage. However, conflicting data exist on whether VIP down-regulation or up-regulation contributes to OA.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Wikipedia)
- **Colitis**: Shown in mouse models (DSS-induced colitis): increased mast cells and mast cells in close proximity to VIP compared to control mice.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Casado-Bedmar et al., 2019)
- **Social behavior**: Shown in bird models: VIP-knockout birds exhibited decreased aggression over nesting territory. VIP circuits in hypothalamus regulate social behaviors including parental care and aggression across vertebrate species.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] (Wikipedia)

### Human Studies

#### COVID-19 Respiratory Failure — Negative Phase III Trial

The **TESICO trial** (2023) was a randomized, placebo-controlled Phase III trial of IV aviptadil in 471 participants with COVID-19 acute hypoxemic respiratory failure across 28 US sites.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Primary outcome (day 90 ordinal scale): NOT significant** — OR 1.11 (95% CI 0.80–1.55; p=0.54)
- **Mortality up to day 90: NOT significant** — 38% aviptadil vs 36% placebo (HR 1.04; 95% CI 0.77–1.41; p=0.78)
- **Safety composite (day 5):** 63% aviptadil vs 56% placebo had death, SAE, organ failure, serious infection, or grade 3–4 AE
- **DSMB stopped the trial for futility on May 25, 2022**
- **Conclusion: Aviptadil did not improve outcomes**

#### COVID-19 Respiratory Failure — Mixed Phase II Trial

A smaller 196-patient multicenter trial (Youssef et al., 2022) randomized 2:1 to IV aviptadil vs placebo:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Primary endpoint (alive and free from respiratory failure at day 60): NOT significant** (OR 1.6; 95% CI 0.86–3.11)
- **Secondary: survival at 60 days significantly improved** (OR 2.0; 95% CI 1.1–3.9; p=0.035)
- Significant improvement in respiratory distress ratio and reduced IL-6 by day 3 (p=0.02)
- Subgroup: high-flow nasal oxygen patients more likely to achieve primary endpoint (p=0.039)
- Mechanical ventilation patients: 10-fold increased odds of survival (p=0.031)
- No drug-related serious adverse events

**Contradiction note:** The larger, more rigorous TESICO trial found no survival benefit (HR 1.04), directly contradicting the survival signal in this smaller trial. The TESICO result carries more weight due to larger sample size, factorial design, and DSMB oversight.

#### Sarcoidosis — Positive Phase II Immunomodulation

An open-label Phase II study treated 20 patients with histologically proved sarcoidosis with nebulized VIP for 4 weeks:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- VIP inhalation was **safe and well-tolerated**
- **Significantly reduced TNF-α production** by bronchoalveolar lavage (BAL) cells
- **Significantly increased BAL CD4⁺CD127⁻CD25⁺ regulatory T cells**
- First demonstration of VIP immunoregulatory effect in humans (Prasse et al., 2010)

#### Migraine Induction — Clear Provocation Effect

A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover study in 21 migraine without aura patients:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **2-hour VIP infusion provoked migraine attacks in 71% (15/21) vs 5% (1/21) with placebo (P<0.001)**
- VIP-induced attacks mimicked patients' spontaneous attacks
- Superficial temporal artery diameter significantly greater after VIP
- **Conclusion: VIP plays an important role in migraine pathophysiology** (Pellesi et al., 2021)

#### Headache in Healthy Volunteers

In 12 healthy volunteers, 2-hour continuous IV VIP infusion induced:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Long-lasting cranial vasodilation (>2 hours)
- Delayed mild headaches (particularly 4–12 hours post-infusion)
- Activation of cranial parasympathetic system (increased tear production)
- (Pellesi et al., 2020)

#### Cluster Headache Provocation

In a double-blind crossover study of cluster headache patients:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Episodic active phase:** VIP induced cluster-like attacks in 5/14 patients (36%); PACAP38 in 6/14 (43%)
- **Chronic:** VIP and PACAP38 both induced attacks in 7/15 patients (47%)
- **Remission phase:** Neither peptide induced attacks
- Provoked attacks were **not** accompanied by changes in plasma CGRP, tryptase, or histamine
- (Vollesen et al., 2020; Pellesi et al., 2022)

#### Affective Symptoms — Correlational

In 37 healthy females, plasma VIP levels showed:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Negative correlation with anxiety** (r=−0.44, p=0.002)
- **Negative correlation with depression** (r=−0.50, p=0.001)
- Positive correlation with brain volume in left amygdala and left lateral orbitofrontal cortex
- VIP-dependent functional connectivity between amygdala and parahippocampus/OFC
- (Simon et al., 2021)

**Important:** This is correlational data only. No causal relationship has been established.

#### Inflammatory Bowel Disease — Biomarker Association

In Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis patients:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Elevated plasma VIP levels compared to controls
- Increased mast cells expressing VPAC1 in intestinal tissue
- Increased mast cells in close proximity to VIP-positive nerve fibers
- (Casado-Bedmar et al., 2019)

## Health Optimization Applications

### Areas with Some Human Evidence

- **Immune modulation**: Shown in human Phase II trial: inhaled VIP increased regulatory T cells and reduced TNF-α in sarcoidosis patients.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] This is the strongest human efficacy signal for VIP.
- **Respiratory conditions**: Preclinical rationale exists (bronchodilation, anti-inflammatory), but the largest human trial (TESICO, n=471) was negative for COVID-19 respiratory failure.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Areas with Only Preclinical/Correlational Evidence

- **Circadian rhythm/sleep**: Strong mechanistic rationale from mouse SCN studies, but zero human interventional data for sleep or circadian optimization.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Cardiovascular health**: Shown in animal models: cardioprotective effects in heart failure, hypertension, myocardial fibrosis. No human trials for cardiovascular indications.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Mood/anxiety**: Correlational only — lower VIP associated with higher anxiety/depression scores in one small study (n=37). No interventional data.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Gut health**: VIP is a normal regulator of intestinal secretion and motility. Elevated in IBD (may be compensatory). No therapeutic trials for GI conditions. Overlapping interest with [[bpc-157]] for gut mucosal integrity, though mechanisms differ entirely.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Sexual function**: VIP mediates vaginal lubrication. Evidence from 1990s studies. No modern trials.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Areas of Concern

- **Migraine/headache**: VIP is a **provoking agent** for migraine and cluster headache attacks. This is a demonstrated adverse effect, not a therapeutic application.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Safety Profile

### Known Adverse Effects from Human Studies

- **Headache/migraine induction**: 2-hour VIP infusion provoked migraine in 71% of migraine patients and delayed mild headache in healthy volunteers.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] This is the most consistently observed adverse effect.
- **Vasodilation/hypotension**: VIP is 50–100× more potent than acetylcholine as a vasodilator. Reduces mean arterial pressure by 10–15%.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md] Clinically significant hypotension is a theoretical risk.
- **Tachycardia**: VIP has positive chronotropic effects — more potent than norepinephrine for increasing heart rate.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Flushing**: Observed with VIP infusion, consistent with its vasodilatory properties.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Safety Data from Clinical Trials

- **Inhaled VIP (sarcoidosis Phase II):** Safe and well-tolerated over 4 weeks.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **IV aviptadil (COVID-19 trials):** No drug-related serious adverse events reported in the smaller trial. In TESICO, the safety composite at day 5 was 63% aviptadil vs 56% placebo — a numerical imbalance favoring placebo.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Pathological Overproduction — VIPoma

VIPoma is a rare neuroendocrine tumor (75% pancreatic) that overproduces VIP, causing **WDHA syndrome** (Watery Diarrhea, Hypokalemia, Achlorhydria). This demonstrates what chronic VIP excess does in humans:^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

- Severe secretory diarrhea → life-threatening electrolyte depletion
- Metabolic acidosis
- Flushing
- Hypercalcemia
- Stimulation of glycogenolysis

### Unknowns and Concerns

- **No long-term safety data**: All human VIP administration studies have been short-term (days to weeks).
- **Extremely short half-life (~2 min)**: Any therapeutic use requires continuous infusion or stabilized analogs, which introduces additional unknowns.
- **Cancer/tumor risk**: VIP receptors are expressed on various tumor cells. The relationship between VIP signaling and tumor biology is complex and not fully characterized. VIP has both pro- and anti-inflammatory effects that could theoretically influence tumor surveillance.
- **No regulatory approval**: VIP/aviptadil is not approved by FDA or EMA for any indication.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)

**All dosing information below is from published clinical trials. VIP/aviptadil is not approved for any clinical use. This is educational only.**

### IV Aviptadil (COVID-19 Trials)

Reported in literature at:
- **TESICO trial**: 12-hour daily IV infusion × 3 days, escalating: 600 pmol/kg (day 1) → 1,200 pmol/kg (day 2) → 1,800 pmol/kg (day 3)^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Youssef trial**: Same escalating 3-day IV protocol^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Inhaled VIP (Sarcoidosis Trial)

Reported in literature at:
- Nebulized VIP, 4 weeks duration (specific dosing details not publicly available from abstract)^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### IV VIP (Headache Provocation Studies)

Reported in literature at:
- **Migraine induction**: 2-hour continuous IV infusion (specific pmol/kg rate not in abstract)^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Cluster headache**: 8 pmol/kg/min over 20 minutes^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]
- **Healthy volunteer headache**: 2-hour continuous IV infusion^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Route Considerations

- **IV**: Requires continuous infusion due to ~2-minute half-life. Impractical outside hospital setting.
- **Inhaled/nebulized**: Investigated for pulmonary conditions. More practical but still experimental.
- **Subcutaneous**: Not well-studied for VIP. Half-life limitation applies.
- **Stabilized analogs**: Research ongoing for degradation-resistant VIP agonists.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Comparison with Related Peptides

### VIP vs PACAP

| Dimension | VIP | PACAP |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| **Structure** | 28 AA | PACAP38 (38 AA) / PACAP27 (27 AA) |
| **Receptors** | VPAC1, VPAC2 (high affinity); PAC1 (low affinity) | VPAC1, VPAC2, PAC1 (all high affinity) |
| **Migraine induction** | Yes — 71% of migraine patients (2-h infusion) | Yes — PACAP38 is a known migraine trigger |
| **Cluster headache** | Induces attacks in active/chronic phase | Induces attacks similarly |
| **Vasodilation** | Potent, long-lasting (>2h with continuous infusion) | Potent, long-lasting |
| **Therapeutic development** | Aviptadil (COVID-19, sarcoidosis) | Anti-PACAP mAbs (Lu AG09222) for migraine prevention |

VIP and PACAP are structurally and functionally related but act through distinct pathways. Lu AG09222 (anti-PACAP monoclonal antibody) blocked PACAP38-induced vasodilation and headache but did **not** block VIP effects, confirming separate receptor mechanisms.^[raw/articles/vip-research-2026-06-09.md]

### VIP vs Other Immune-Modulating Peptides

VIP shares immunomodulatory properties with [[thymosin-alpha-1]] but through entirely different mechanisms:
- **VIP**: VPAC1/VPAC2 → cAMP → PKA → Treg induction, cytokine suppression
- **Thymosin Alpha-1**: TLR2/TLR9 agonism → dendritic cell maturation → adaptive immune stimulation

VIP is predominantly **anti-inflammatory/immunosuppressive** (Treg induction, cytokine suppression), while Tα1 is **immunostimulatory** (TLR agonism, enhanced antigen presentation).

## Confidence Assessment

**Confidence: Medium**

### Justification

- **Mechanistic understanding**: High. VIP receptor signaling, SCN circadian role, and immune modulation are well-characterized across decades of research.
- **Animal model evidence**: Strong. Mouse knockout/optogenetic studies provide compelling causal evidence for circadian and cardiovascular roles.
- **Human evidence**: Mixed and limited. The largest human trial (TESICO, Phase III, n=471) was **negative** for its primary endpoint. A smaller trial showed a survival signal that the larger trial contradicted. The clearest human efficacy signal is the sarcoidosis Phase II immunomodulation study (n=20, open-label). Headache/migraine provocation is consistently demonstrated but represents an adverse effect, not a benefit.
- **Safety**: Incomplete. Short-term administration appears tolerable, but VIP causes headache in healthy people and migraine in susceptible patients. No long-term safety data exist. The VIPoma syndrome demonstrates the dangers of chronic VIP excess.
- **Therapeutic translation**: Poor. The 2-minute half-life makes VIP impractical as a therapeutic without continuous infusion or stabilized analogs. The negative Phase III trial is a significant setback.

### Key Uncertainties

1. Whether the survival signal in the smaller COVID-19 trial represents a real subgroup effect or a false positive (TESICO suggests the latter)
2. Whether inhaled VIP for chronic inflammatory lung diseases would show efficacy in blinded, controlled trials
3. Whether VIP-based interventions could improve circadian/sleep disorders (no human data)
4. Long-term safety of repeated or chronic VIP administration
5. Cancer risk implications of VIP receptor modulation
