# Modified GRF (1-29) / CJC-1295 without DAC

## Summary

Modified GRF (1-29), also referred to as "CJC-1295 without DAC," is a synthetic analogue of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) comprising the first 29 amino acids of GHRH with four amino acid substitutions (D-Ala2, Gln8, Ala15, Leu27) that improve stability relative to endogenous GHRH and to sermorelin. It is **distinct from CJC-1295 DAC** — the version with a Drug Affinity Complex — and does **not** contain the C-terminus Nɛ-maleimidopropionyl-Lysine extension. This distinction is important because CJC-1295 DAC binds covalently to serum albumin, yielding a half-life of 5.8–8.1 days, while Modified GRF (1-29) has a much shorter half-life (estimated ~30 minutes) and preserves the pulsatile nature of GH secretion.

**Critical note:** CJC-1295 and Modified GRF (1-29) are falsely equated in several scientific papers and in the consumer peptide market. The Wikipedia entry for CJC-1295 explicitly distinguishes the two forms. Much of the published human data pertains to the DAC version only.

---

## Structure & Pharmacology

The peptide sequence for Modified GRF (1-29) is:

`H-Tyr-D-Ala-Asp-Ala-Ile-Phe-Thr-Gln-Ser-Tyr-Arg-Lys-Val-Leu-Ala-Gln-Leu-Ser-Ala-Arg-Lys-Leu-Leu-Gln-Asp-Ile-Leu-Ser-Arg-NH₂`

**Substitutions relative to endogenous GHRH(1-29):**
- **D-Ala²**: Replaces L-Ala at position 2 to resist dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) degradation
- **Gln⁸**: Replaces Asn⁸ to reduce deamidation and isomerization
- **Ala¹⁵**: Replaces Gly¹⁵ to enhance bioactivity
- **Leu²⁷**: Replaces Met²⁷ to prevent methionine oxidation

**Molecular formula:** C₁₅₂H₂₅₂N₄₄O₄₂ (without the DAC Lys30 extension)
**Comparison to sermorelin (GHRH 1-29):** Sermorelin is the unmodified 1-29 fragment of GHRH. Modified GRF (1-29) contains four substitutions that make it more stable and longer-acting than sermorelin, but much shorter-acting than CJC-1295 DAC.

## Mechanism of Action

- **Class:** GHRH analogue (ghrh-analog)
- **Primary mechanism:** Binds to the growth hormone-releasing hormone receptor (GHRHR) on pituitary somatotrophs, stimulating synthesis and pulsatile release of growth hormone (GH)
- **Downstream effects:** GH stimulates hepatic IGF-1 production; IGF-1 mediates many of the anabolic, lipolytic, and tissue-repair effects
- **Pulsatility preservation:** Unlike CJC-1295 DAC (which produces a sustained "GH bleed" for days), Modified GRF (1-29) has a short half-life (~30 minutes), allowing it to be used in controlled pulses that better mimic physiological GH secretory patterns. This may reduce risk of GH receptor desensitization and tachyphylaxis.
- **Somatostatin regulation:** Like endogenous GHRH, Modified GRF (1-29) remains subject to negative feedback via somatostatin, which acts as a physiological "brake" on excessive GH release.

Source: Wikipedia CJC-1295; Sermorelin mechanistic data from Wikipedia/extrapolation of GHRHR pharmacology.

## Evidence Assessment

### Human Evidence (CJC-1295 DAC — Phase II clinical data)

**Teichman et al. 2006 (J Clin Endocrinol Metab)** — RCT in healthy adults (n not specified in abstract):
- Single subcutaneous injection of CJC-1295 DAC produced dose-dependent increases in plasma GH (2- to 10-fold) sustained for ≥6 days
- IGF-1 increases of 1.5- to 3-fold sustained for 9–11 days
- Estimated half-life: 5.8–8.1 days
- Multiple doses maintained IGF-1 elevation for up to 28 days
- No serious adverse reactions reported at 30 or 60 µg/kg
- **Caveat:** This is CJC-1295 WITH DAC, not Modified GRF (1-29). Efficacy and PK data do not extrapolate to the no-DAC form.
- Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16352683/

**ClinicalTrials.gov NCT00267527** — Phase II trial for HIV-associated visceral obesity:
- Sponsor: ConjuChem Biotechnologies
- Status: **TERMINATED**
- Reason: One subject death during the trial. The trial physician attributed death to asymptomatic coronary artery plaque rupture (unrelated to drug), but the study was discontinued as a precaution.
- Conditions: Obesity, HIV Infections
- This termination effectively halted pharmaceutical development of CJC-1295 DAC.
- Source: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT00267527

**Human evidence for Modified GRF (1-29) specifically:** There are **no published clinical trials** of Modified GRF (1-29) (without DAC) in humans. The human evidence base for this specific peptide is essentially nil.

### Mouse Model Evidence (CJC-1295 DAC)

**Alba et al. 2006 (Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab)** — GHRH knockout mouse model:
- Daily dosing of CJC-1295 DAC normalized body weight and length in GHRHKO mice
- Dosing every 48 or 72 hours was insufficient for full growth normalization
- Femur and tibia length normal with daily or every-48h dosing
- Lean mass and subcutaneous fat mass normalized in all treated groups
- CJC-1295 increased pituitary RNA and GH mRNA, indicating somatotroph proliferation (confirmed by immunohistochemistry)
- Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16822960/

**Mayfield et al. 2026 (Am J Sports Med)** — Narrative review of injectable peptides:
- "CJC-1295 combined with ipamorelin showed significantly improved maximum tetanic tension in murine models with glucocorticoid-induced muscle loss, but these findings are limited to animal studies."
- Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41476424/

### Anecdotal / Internet Community Evidence

**Van Hout & Hearne 2016 (Subst Use Misuse)** — Netnography of 23 female discussion threads on bodybuilding forums:
- Female users reported using CJC-1295 for: weight loss, muscle enhancement, youthful skin, improved sleep, and injury healing
- Users demonstrated poly-pharmacy sophistication with other performance/image drugs
- Concerns identified: gender variations in GH pulsatility affecting dosing estimation, cycling protocols, and unknown long-term consequences
- Authors called for public health interventions addressing self-medicating use
- **Caveat:** This paper does not clearly distinguish between DAC and no-DAC forms; forum users may conflate both.
- Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26771670/

### Orthopaedic and Sports Medicine Reviews (2026)

Multiple 2026 review papers mention CJC-1295 in the context of sports medicine/orthopaedics (Rahman et al. PMID 41490200, Mayfield et al. PMID 41476424, Mendias & Awan PMID 41966639, Coutinho et al. PMID 41880199, Villegas Meza et al. PMID 42160466, Mavrych et al. PMID 42021992). Consensus across all reviews:
- CJC-1295 and related growth hormone secretagogues (ipamorelin, sermorelin, tesamorelin) are mentioned as being marketed for muscle growth, fat loss, recovery, and anti-aging
- **All reviews emphasize the lack of rigorous human trials for musculoskeletal or sports medicine applications**
- Product quality concerns (mislabeling, contamination) are frequently noted
- WADA prohibits these compounds in sport
- Clinical use should be confined to approved indications or research protocols only

## Safety Profile

### ⚠️ Critical Safety Flags

1. **Phase II termination due to death:** The CJC-1295 DAC clinical program was terminated after one subject died during the Phase II HIV-associated obesity trial. The trial physician attributed the death to asymptomatic coronary artery plaque rupture and considered it unrelated to the drug, but research was discontinued as a precaution. This event creates a permanent cloud over the entire CJC-1295 compound family.

2. **No human safety data for Modified GRF (1-29) specifically:** Zero published human trials exist for the no-DAC form. All safety data in humans pertains to the DAC version. The pharmacokinetic profiles are fundamentally different, so safety data from DAC trials cannot be assumed to apply to the shorter-acting form.

3. **Gray market sourcing risks:** Modified GRF (1-29) is sold as a "research chemical" by unregulated vendors. The 2019 study by Gajda et al. (PMID 30136411) found that seized doping materials labeled as growth hormone secretagogues (including modified GRF 1-29) contained glycine-modified variants; Henninge et al. 2010 (PMID 21204297) identified CJC-1295 in an unknown pharmaceutical preparation. Product identity, purity, and sterility are not verified.

4. **Growth hormone axis risks:** Chronic elevation of GH/IGF-1 is associated with:
   - Insulin resistance and hyperglycemia
   - Fluid retention / carpal tunnel syndrome
   - Arthralgia
   - Potential acceleration of neoplastic growth (theoretical)
   - Acromegalic features with prolonged supraphysiological dosing

5. **Interaction risks:** No monoaminergic, opioidergic, or serotonergic activity identified — the mechanism is exclusively GHRHR-mediated. No known direct drug-drug interaction risks beyond additive effects with other GH secretagogues.

6. **Contamination risk in combination products:** Modified GRF (1-29) is commonly sold in combination vials with ipamorelin or other GHRPs. Mislabeling and cross-contamination are documented in the gray market.

## CJC-1295 DAC vs. Modified GRF (1-29): Comparison

| Feature | CJC-1295 DAC | Modified GRF (1-29) |
|---|---|---|
| **DAC additive** | Yes (Nɛ-maleimidopropionyl-Lys30) | No |
| **Albumin binding** | Covalent, irreversible | None |
| **Half-life** | 5.8–8.1 days | ~30 minutes (estimated) |
| **GH release pattern** | Sustained elevation for 6–14+ days ("GH bleed") | Pulsatile, mimics physiology |
| **Clinical trials** | Phase I + Phase II (terminated) | None |
| **Human PK data** | Published (Teichman 2006) | None |
| **Somatostatin regulation** | Largely bypassed (constant GH elevation) | Preserved negative feedback |
| **Dosing frequency** | Weekly to biweekly | Multiple times daily (typical protocol: 2–3×/day) |
| **Risk of tachyphylaxis** | Higher (sustained receptor activation) | Lower (pulsatile, physiological pattern) |
| **Regulatory status** | Discontinued investigational drug | Research chemical; not FDA-approved |
| **WADA status** | Prohibited (S2 — Peptide Hormones) | Prohibited (S2 — Peptide Hormones) |

Source: Wikipedia, Teichman et al. 2006, GHRH pharmacology.

## Legal Status

- **United States:** Not FDA-approved. Not a scheduled controlled substance but may fall under the FDA's category of unapproved new drug. Sold as a "research chemical — not for human consumption."
- **WADA/anti-doping:** Prohibited under Section S2 (Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, and Related Substances). Both CJC-1295 DAC and Modified GRF (1-29) are banned in-competition and out-of-competition.
- **Equine sports:** Detection methods have been developed for CJC-1295 in equine plasma (Timms et al. 2019, PMID 30938069; Timms et al. 2019, PMID 30489688), confirming concern about abuse in animal sports as well.
- **General:** Available on the gray market through peptide vendors and compounding pharmacies (where sermorelin may be substituted).

## Dosing (Anecdotal / Community-Derived)

**For educational purposes only. Not medical advice. Modified GRF (1-29) lacks human safety and efficacy data. No established clinical dosing protocol exists.**

Anecdotal protocols (sourced from bodybuilding and longevity forums analyzed by Van Hout & Hearne 2016 and general community consensus) describe:

- **Typical dose:** 100 µg (1 µg/kg) of Modified GRF (1-29) combined with 100–200 µg of a GHRP (e.g., ipamorelin, GHRP-2, or GHRP-6)
- **Frequency:** 2–3× daily, timed to coincide with natural GH pulses (upon waking, post-workout, pre-bed, and ideally on an empty stomach ~2 hours after last meal)
- **Rationale:** The combination of a GHRH analogue (Modified GRF 1-29) with a GHRP (ghrelin mimetic) is intended to produce a synergistic GH pulse; the GHRP stimulates GH release and suppresses somatostatin, while the GHRH analogue provides the releasing factor
- **Cycle duration:** Commonly 8–12 weeks on, followed by equal time off
- **Storage:** Lyophilized powder stored frozen; reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, refrigerated

**No clinical trial has validated any of these protocols for safety or efficacy.**

## Confidence Assessment: LOW

**Rationale:**
- There are no human clinical trials of Modified GRF (1-29) specifically
- Most published human data concerns CJC-1295 DAC, a fundamentally different molecule
- The Phase II DAC program was terminated after a subject death
- Animal data (GHRH knockout mouse) shows efficacy for DAC form only
- Anecdotal use data from internet forums cannot establish safety or efficacy
- 2026 sports medicine reviews unanimously classify CJC-1295/ipamorelin as investigational
- Gray market sourcing introduces unknown purity/identity risks

---

## Gaps in Knowledge

1. Human pharmacokinetics of Modified GRF (1-29) are unpublished
2. No dose-response data in humans
3. No long-term safety data (>28 days) for any form
4. Combination protocols (CJC-1295 + ipamorelin) are entirely unstudied in controlled trials
5. Optimal dosing frequency, cycling, and duration are unknown
6. No comparative studies against FDA-approved alternatives (sermorelin, tesamorelin)
7. Female-specific data is limited to one netnography study
