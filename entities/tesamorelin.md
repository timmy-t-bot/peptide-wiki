# Tesamorelin

## Overview

Tesamorelin (INN; trade name Egrifta SV) is a synthetic analogue of human growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) comprising all 44 amino acids of human GHRH with an added trans-3-hexenoic acid group at the N-terminus. It was approved by the FDA in 2010 and is the **only FDA-approved treatment for HIV-associated lipodystrophy**. It is developed and produced by Theratechnologies, Inc. of Canada.

Tesamorelin is **not a direct fat-loss drug for the general population** — it is indicated specifically for reduction of excess abdominal fat in HIV-infected adults with lipodystrophy, and its use for general weight loss or body recomposition is off-label.

## Mechanism of Action

Tesamorelin binds to the GHRH receptor in the anterior pituitary, stimulating the synthesis and pulsatile release of endogenous growth hormone (GH). The released GH then binds to receptors on various body organs, elevating insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1) levels and exerting downstream effects.

The N-terminal modification (trans-3-hexenoic acid) makes tesamorelin **more potent and stable** than natural GHRH and **more resistant to dipeptidyl aminopeptidase cleavage** than endogenous GHRH [Ferdinandi et al., 2007, PMID: 17214611].

### Primary Effects
- **Lipolysis**: The primary mechanism by which tesamorelin reduces body fat is lipolysis followed by reduction in triglycerides [Benedini et al., 2008, PMID: 18345707].
- **IGF-1 elevation**: Stimulates hepatic IGF-1 production, contributing to anabolic effects including lean body mass preservation.
- **Visceral fat selectivity**: Reduces visceral adipose tissue (VAT) without clinically significant effects on subcutaneous adipose tissue (SAT).
- **Hepatic fat reduction**: Reduces liver fat content in HIV-associated NAFLD via suppression of angiogenic, fibrogenic, and pro-inflammatory mediators [Fourman et al., 2021, PMID: 34006921].
- **Immune modulation**: Reduces markers of T-cell and monocyte/macrophage activation, including chemokines CCL3, CCL4, CCL13, IL8, and cytokines IL-10 and CSF-1 [Stanley et al., 2021, PMID: 33852720].

## Human Evidence

Tesamorelin has the **strongest evidence base of any GHRH analogue**, having completed multiple Phase 3 trials and obtained FDA approval. The evidence is exclusively from HIV-infected populations unless otherwise noted.

### HIV-Associated Lipodystrophy (FDA-Approved Indication)

**Pivotal Phase 3 Trial** — Falutz et al., 2010 (PMID: 20101189): N=404 HIV-infected patients with excess abdominal fat. Double-blind, 6-month treatment (2 mg SC daily, 2:1 randomization), followed by 6-month extension.

| Outcome | Tesamorelin | Placebo | P-value |
|---------|------------|---------|---------|
| VAT reduction (6 mo) | -10.9% (-21 cm²) | -0.6% (-1 cm²) | P<0.0001 |
| VAT reduction (12 mo continuous) | ~-18% | — | P<0.001 |
| Trunk fat | Significant ↓ | — | P<0.001 |
| Waist circumference | ↓ | — | P=0.02 |
| Waist-to-hip ratio | ↓ | — | P=0.001 |
| Limb/abdominal SC fat | No change | — | — |
| IGF-1 | ↑ | — | P<0.001 |
| Glucose parameters | No change | — | — |
| Belly appearance distress | Improved | — | P=0.02 |

**Critical finding**: Upon discontinuation at 6 months, VAT reaccumulated rapidly — the effect is **not durable without continued therapy**.

**Second Phase 3 Trial**: Dhillon 2011 review (PMID: 21668043) confirmed results across two 26-week trials. VAT reduction maintained to week 52 on continued therapy. SAEs <4%. 

### Meta-Analysis

**Badran et al., 2026** (PMID: 41545261): 5 RCTs, random-effects meta-analysis:

| Outcome | Effect Size | 95% CI |
|---------|------------|--------|
| VAT | -27.71 cm² | [-38.37, -17.06] |
| Trunk fat | -1.18 kg | [-1.40, -0.96] |
| Limb fat | -0.22 kg | [-0.35, -0.08] |
| Hepatic fat | -4.28% | [-6.31, -2.24] |
| Waist circumference | -1.61 cm | [-2.28, -0.95] |
| Lean body mass | **+1.42 kg** | [+1.13, +1.71] |
| SAT | No significant change | — |
| BMI | No significant change | — |

### NAFLD/NASH in HIV

**NCT02196831** (Stanley/Fourman, MGH): Double-blind RCT, 12 months tesamorelin vs. placebo in 61 PWH with NAFLD. Liver fat reduced. Downregulation of hepatic gene sets involved in inflammation, tissue repair, and cell division. Suppression of VEGFA, TGFB1, and CSF1 correlated with reduced NAFLD activity and fibrosis scores [Fourman et al., 2021, PMID: 34006921].

**NCT03375788**: Phase 2 trial in HIV-negative individuals with NAFLD (completed, results available). Investigated liver fat and cardiovascular risk.

### Neurocognitive Effects

**Ellis et al., 2025** (PMID: 39813152): Phase 2 open-label RCT, N=73 virally-suppressed PWH with abdominal obesity, 6 months tesamorelin vs. standard of care.

- **Primary outcome (neurocognitive change): NOT significant** — between-group difference P=0.673
- Tesamorelin group showed a non-significant trend (P=0.060 within-group)
- Waist circumference reduced significantly (-2.7 cm median, P=0.015)
- **Conclusion:** No clear cognitive benefit from short-term tesamorelin. The study was open-label versus standard of care (no placebo arm) and may have been underpowered to detect a modest effect.

**NCT02572323** (UCSD): Phase 2 trial of tesamorelin for cognition in aging HIV-infected persons (completed, results available). Aimed to test whether tesamorelin improves memory and thinking.

### Fat Quality

**Fourman et al., 2021** (PMID: 33756511): Tesamorelin improves fat density on CT (greater density = smaller, higher-quality adipocytes) independent of changes in fat quantity.

### INSTI-Era Efficacy

**Fourman/Stanley, 2024** (PMID: 38905488): Confirmed tesamorelin remains efficacious and safe in PWH on integrase inhibitor-based ART, which was introduced after the original Phase 3 trials.

## Mouse Model Findings

Limited direct mouse model evidence was identified. The preclinical pharmacology of TH9507 was evaluated by Ferdinandi et al. (2007, PMID: 17214611), which confirmed the compound's resistance to dipeptidyl aminopeptidase cleavage and superior stability compared to human GHRH. This was performed in non-clinical models (species not specified in available abstract — likely rats as standard for GHRH analog testing).

Additionally, a 2026 rodent model study examined combined antiretroviral therapy with dietary regimens and noted metabolic syndrome development, but tesamorelin was not the primary intervention.

**No peer-reviewed mouse model data specifically showing tesamorelin effects on longevity, cognitive enhancement, or body composition in healthy animals was identified.**

## Dosing

> **For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **FDA-approved dose (Egrifta SV)** | 1.4 mg SC once daily |
| **Original formulation dose (Egrifta)** | 2 mg SC once daily |
| **Injection site** | Abdomen, rotating sites |
| **Reconstitution** | Use only provided Sterile Water for Injection (0.5 mL) |
| **Injection volume** | 0.35 mL (Egrifta SV) |
| **Timing** | Any time of day; consistent timing recommended |

### Clinical Trial Dosing Protocol
- 2 mg SC daily (used in all Phase 3 and most Phase 2 trials)
- Treatment duration in studies: 6–12 months
- **Effect is not durable** — VAT reaccumulates within weeks of discontinuation
- Long-term safety beyond 12 months not established in controlled trials

### Important Notes
- The Egrifta SV (1.4 mg) and original Egrifta (2 mg) formulations have **different reconstitution instructions, vial counts, and storage requirements** — they are not interchangeable without recalculating.
- No established dose-response data for off-label uses (body recomposition, cognition, NAFLD in non-HIV).
- IGF-1 levels should be monitored; consider discontinuation if persistently elevated.

## Safety

> **⚠️ SAFETY WARNING: Tesamorelin carries significant risks that require medical supervision.**

### Critical Contraindications
1. **Pregnancy Category X**: Contraindicated; may harm fetus.
2. **Active malignancy**: Preexisting malignancy must be inactive with completed treatment before starting. Discontinue if recurrent malignancy detected.
3. **Hypothalamic-pituitary axis disruption**: Contraindicated in pituitary tumor, head irradiation, hypopituitarism.
4. **Glucose intolerance**: May cause or worsen glucose intolerance and **increase type 2 diabetes risk**.

### FDA Warnings and Precautions
| Risk | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Neoplasms** | Prolonged IGF-1 elevation may increase malignancy risk. Preexisting malignancy must be inactive. Discontinue if recurrent. |
| **Elevated IGF-1** | Effects of prolonged elevation unknown. Monitor levels during therapy. |
| **Fluid retention** | May cause edema, arthralgia, carpal tunnel syndrome. |
| **Glucose intolerance / Diabetes** | May develop during use. Evaluate glucose before and during therapy. |
| **Hypersensitivity** | Reactions including rash, urticaria reported. |
| **Injection site reactions** | Erythema, pruritus, pain, irritation, bruising. |

### Adverse Reactions (>5% incidence in clinical trials)
- Arthralgia (joint pain)
- Injection site erythema
- Injection site pruritus
- Pain in extremity
- Peripheral edema
- Myalgia (muscle pain)
- Paresthesia

### Additional Safety Concerns
- **Carpal tunnel syndrome:** Carpal tunnel syndrome has been reported with tesamorelin and other growth hormone axis therapies; monitor for symptoms.
- **Lipoatrophy risk**: Prolonged exposure combined with thymidine NRTIs may increase lipoatrophy risk [Martínez, 2011].
- **Anti-drug antibodies**: Development of anti-tesamorelin antibodies observed in some patients; clinical significance unclear.
- **Long-term cardiovascular safety**: NOT established. FDA label explicitly states this limitation.
- **IGF-1 monitoring**: Persistently elevated IGF-1 is a reason to consider discontinuation.
- **No data on >12-month continuous use** in controlled settings.
- **Interaction Warning**: While tesamorelin acts through the GHRH/GH/IGF-1 axis and not directly through monoaminergic, opioidergic, or serotonergic pathways, its metabolic effects (glucose, IGF-1) create potential indirect interactions with insulin, oral hypoglycemics, corticosteroids, and other hormones. Concomitant use with other GH secretagogues or exogenous GH is untested and potentially hazardous.

### Discontinuation Effects
- VAT reaccumulates rapidly upon stopping (demonstrated in Phase 3 extension phases)
- Return to baseline body composition within weeks to months
- Rebound effects on glucose metabolism not well characterized

- Cognitive function in MCI (Phase 1 completed)
- Cognitive function in aging HIV (Phase 2 completed — no significant benefit)
- Muscle wasting in COPD (terminated)
- Sleep/TBI in service members (withdrawn)

### Knowledge Gaps
- Long-term (>2 year) safety in any population
- Efficacy in non-HIV, non-lipodystrophic populations
- Dose-response for off-label uses
- Durability of metabolic improvements after prolonged use
- Cardiovascular outcomes data
- Combination strategies (e.g., with GLP-1 agonists for fat loss + glycemic control)

## Comparison to Other GHRH Analogues

| Property | Tesamorelin (Egrifta) | Sermorelin (Geref) | CJC-1295 (no DAC) | CJC-1295 with DAC |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Structure** | 44-aa GHRH + trans-3-hexenoic acid | 1-29 GHRH fragment | 1-29 GHRH + 4 substituted AAs | CJC-1295 + Drug Affinity Complex |
| **Approval** | FDA-approved (2010) | Previously FDA-approved; discontinued | Research chemical only | Research chemical only |
| **Half-life** | 26-38 min | ~11-12 min | ~30 min | ~6-8 days |
| **GH Secretion Pattern** | Pulsatile (physiological) | Pulsatile | Pulsatile | Continuous ("GH bleed") |
| **Evidence Level** | Phase 3, FDA-approved | Phase 3, discontinued | Preclinical/anecdotal | Phase I–II (terminated after subject death) |

Tesamorelin is the only GHRH analogue with robust Phase 3 evidence and regulatory approval. Other GHRH analogues (CJC-1295 variants, sermorelin) and related GH secretagogues (ipamorelin) lack comparable clinical trial data and are either discontinued pharmaceuticals or research chemicals.

## Summary Assessment

**Confidence: HIGH** — Tesamorelin's effects on VAT reduction in HIV-associated lipodystrophy are well-established by multiple Phase 3 trials and meta-analysis. The mechanism is well-characterized. Effects on hepatic fat, lean body mass, and immune activation are supported by Phase 2 evidence.

**Strengths of the evidence:**
- FDA-approved with rigorous Phase 3 program
- Consistent results across multiple RCTs
- Meta-analytic confirmation (2026)
- Mechanistically well-understood
- Selective for VAT over SAT

**Limitations of the evidence:**
- All Phase 3 data is in HIV-infected patients — generalizability to immunocompetent populations is unproven
- Long-term safety (>1 year) not established
- No cardiovascular outcomes data
- Cognitive benefit not demonstrated
- Effect not durable — requires continuous therapy
- Low bioavailability (≤4%) necessitates daily injection

**Bottom line:** Tesamorelin is the most evidence-supported GHRH analogue, but its approved indication is narrow (HIV lipodystrophy). Its appeal for off-label body recomposition derives from its unique profile of selectively reducing VAT while preserving or increasing lean mass — a combination few other interventions achieve. However, the safety risks (glucose intolerance, malignancy concerns, fluid retention) and the requirement for daily injection make it unsuitable for unsupervised use.
