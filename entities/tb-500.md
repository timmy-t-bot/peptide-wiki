# Thymosin Beta-4 (TB-500)

## ⚠️ CRITICAL DISTINCTION: Tβ4 vs. TB-500

**Thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4)** and **TB-500** are NOT the same molecule. This is one of the most important facts to understand before reading further:

| Property | Tβ4 (Thymosin Beta-4) | TB-500 |
|----------|----------------------|--------|
| **Type** | Endogenous human protein | Synthetic fragment analog |
| **Size** | 43 amino acids | 7 amino acids (17-23 fragment) |
| **MW** | ~4,921 Da | ~889 Da |
| **Sequence** | Full: SDKPDMAEI EKFDKSKLKK TETQEKNPLP SKETIEQEKQ AGES | Ac-LKKTETQ |
| **Clinical Trials** | Yes — Phase 1 and 2 human trials conducted | **None** — zero human clinical trials |
| **Regulatory Status** | Investigational drug (timbetasin) | Research chemical / designer drug |
| **Availability** | Clinical trial supply only | Gray-market research chemical vendors |
| **WADA Status** | Banned (banned substance itself) | Also banned (as a Tβ4-related compound) |

**The clinical trial evidence discussed below is for full-length Tβ4, NOT for TB-500.** TB-500 is sold as a "research chemical" but there is no peer-reviewed evidence that the 7-amino-acid fragment replicates the full protein's effects in humans. This distinction is frequently ignored in community discussions and marketing materials.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Overview

Thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4) is a 43-amino-acid protein encoded by the **TMSB4X** gene on the X chromosome. It was discovered as a component of Thymosin Fraction 5 and originally classified as a thymic hormone. It is now known to be a **ubiquitous intracellular protein** present at concentrations up to 0.5 mM in many cell types, where it serves as the **principal actin-sequestering protein** — binding G-actin monomers and preventing their polymerization into F-actin filaments.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

Beyond its intracellular actin-regulating role, Tβ4 has attracted significant interest for its **extracellular tissue-regenerative properties**: promoting cell migration, angiogenesis (blood vessel formation), stem cell maturation, cell survival, and suppression of pro-inflammatory cytokines. These properties have led to clinical development for wound healing, cardiac repair, dry eye, and corneal conditions.

The WHO-assigned International Nonproprietary Name (INN) for thymosin beta-4 is **timbetasin**.

## Mechanism of Action

### Actin Sequestration (Primary Intracellular Role)

Tβ4 functions as the **principal actin-sequestering protein** in most mammalian cell types. It forms a tight 1:1 complex with monomeric G-actin, acting as a buffer that maintains the pool of unpolymerized actin:

```
F-actin ↔ G-actin + Tβ4 ↔ G-actin/Tβ4
```

Release of G-actin monomers from the Tβ4 complex enables rapid actin polymerization in response to cellular signals, driving cytoskeletal reorganization essential for **cell migration, morphology changes, and division**. The actin-binding motif LKKTET (residues 17-23) is the conserved sequence responsible for this interaction, though X-ray crystallography shows that essentially the entire Tβ4 sequence interacts with actin in the complex.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Extracellular Tissue Regeneration ("Moonlighting" Functions)

The diverse tissue-regenerative effects of Tβ4 are unlikely to be explained solely by intracellular actin sequestration — when present in extracellular fluid, Tβ4 produces effects even in cells that already have high intracellular Tβ4 concentrations. This has been described as **protein moonlighting** (multi-tasking by a single protein through different interaction partners).^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

Key extracellular effects include:

#### Angiogenesis
Tβ4 promotes new blood vessel formation, a critical mechanism for wound healing and tissue repair. In the heart, it stimulates recruitment of new blood vessels within cardiac muscle.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Cell Migration and Stem Cell Maturation
Tβ4 promotes directed cell migration to injury sites. In the heart, it induces epicardial precursor cells to migrate into the myocardium and differentiate into new cardiomyocytes — demonstrated in mouse models.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Anti-Inflammatory Effects
- **Sulfoxide form**: Oxidized Tβ4 (sulfoxide at the N-terminal methionine) exerts anti-inflammatory effects on neutrophils: promotes their dispersion from inflammatory foci, inhibits chemotactic response to bacterial peptides (f-Met-Leu-Phe), and reduces neutrophil adhesion to endothelial cells. The sulfoxide form is generated by the respiratory burst at sites of inflammation and may be part of the mechanism by which steroids exert anti-inflammatory effects.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]
- Suppression of **pro-inflammatory cytokines** (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β) in multiple models.

#### Terminal Deoxynucleotidyl Transferase (TdT) Induction
Tβ4 induces TdT activity in thymocyte populations, suggesting a role in **immune cell maturation**.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Candidate Extracellular Receptor
A high-affinity candidate receptor is the **β subunit of cell surface ATP synthase**, which would allow extracellular Tβ4 to signal via purinergic receptors. This receptor interaction is distinct from actin binding and may explain the "moonlighting" functions.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Endogenous Fragments with Distinct Pharmacology

Tβ4 is cleaved by prolyl oligopeptidase to produce **Ac-SDKP** (N-acetyl-Ser-Asp-Lys-Pro, also known as Goralatide or Seraspenide), the N-terminal 1-4 fragment. Ac-SDKP has its own distinct pharmacology: it is an inhibitor of hematopoietic stem cell proliferation with anti-inflammatory, anti-fibrotic, and pro-angiogenic properties, particularly studied for kidney and cardiac fibrosis prevention. This is relevant because TB-500 represents a **different** fragment (17-23), and the pharmacology of this specific fragment in humans is largely uncharacterized.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Anecdotal Evidence (TB-500 — Community Reports)

Extensive self-reported use in bodybuilding, biohacking, and injury recovery communities. Commonly reported benefits:
- Accelerated soft tissue injury recovery (tendons, ligaments, muscles)
- Reduced joint pain and inflammation
- Improved flexibility and range of motion
- Faster recovery from intense training
- General "systemic healing" effect (broader than BPC-157's more localized effects)

Commonly reported side effects: injection site reactions, mild fatigue, occasional headaches, and in rare cases, reports of increased anxiety or lethargy.

**Anecdotal reports are not clinical evidence.** TB-500 bought from gray-market vendors has unknown purity, may not contain the stated peptide, and has no established safety profile in humans.

## Safety Profile

### Clinical Trial Safety Data (Tβ4)

In Phase 1 and Phase 2 trials, recombinant human thymosin beta-4 was reported as **safe and well-tolerated**. The Phase 1 healthy volunteer study (2021) established safety and pharmacokinetics without significant adverse events. No serious safety signals were reported in the completed Phase 2 trials for dry eye, wound healing, or epidermolysis bullosa.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

### TB-500 Safety (Unknown)

**No clinical safety data exists for the TB-500 fragment.** Community reports suggest a relatively benign acute side-effect profile (injection site reactions, mild fatigue), but the lack of formal toxicology, pharmacokinetic, or long-term safety data is a critical gap.

### Major Safety Concerns

#### ⚠️ Cancer / Angiogenesis Risk (UNRESOLVED)

Tβ4 is a potent **angiogenic factor** — it stimulates new blood vessel formation. This is therapeutic in wound healing and cardiac repair contexts but raises theoretical concerns about promoting tumor vascularization. **No long-term human cancer surveillance data exists for either Tβ4 or TB-500.** The precautionary principle applies: these compounds should not be used in the presence of any known or suspected malignancy.^[raw/articles/tb-500-research-2026-06-09.md]

The angiogenesis concern is shared with [[bpc-157]] and other pro-angiogenic peptides. The question of whether therapeutic angiogenesis can promote dormant tumors is one of the most important unresolved questions across the peptide field.

#### ⚠️ TB-500 Purity and Sourcing Issues

TB-500 is sold exclusively through unregulated research chemical vendors and gray-market peptide websites:
- **No quality control**: Products may contain incorrect peptides, degradation products, or contaminants
- **No purity verification**: Independent testing of gray-market peptides frequently shows significant purity issues
- **No dosing verification**: Stated concentrations may be inaccurate
- **TB-500 may not actually contain TB-500**: The 2012 analytical chemistry paper was published precisely because the researchers needed to confirm the identity of what was being sold as TB-500

#### ⚠️ Unknown Fragment Pharmacology

TB-500 is the 17-23 fragment. Full Tβ4 is the 1-43 protein. These are fundamentally different molecules. The assumption that TB-500 replicates Tβ4's effects is based on the fact that the 17-23 sequence contains the actin-binding motif (LKKTET). However:
- Tβ4's extracellular receptor interactions may involve other regions of the protein
- The Ac-SDKP fragment (1-4) has distinct, well-characterized pharmacology — demonstrating that different Tβ4 fragments have different effects
- The pharmacokinetics of the 7-amino-acid fragment may differ dramatically from the 43-amino-acid protein
- TB-500 is N-acetylated, which may alter its properties relative to the endogenous fragment

#### ⚠️ Legal and Anti-Doping Risks

- **WADA**: Thymosin beta-4 AND its fragments/analogs (including TB-500) are prohibited under S2 (Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances, and Mimetics)
- **Professional sports**: TB-500 was the substance at the center of two major Australian doping scandals (Essendon AFL, Cronulla-Sutherland NRL). Athletes in both clubs served suspensions.
- **Australia / New Zealand**: TB-500 is classified as a prescription medicine
- **General**: TB-500 is not FDA-approved, not EMA-approved, and not approved by any major regulatory body. It is sold as a "research chemical" not for human consumption.

### Contraindications

- Active or suspected cancer (any type)
- Pregnancy and breastfeeding (zero safety data)
- Bleeding disorders or anticoagulant therapy (theoretical angiogenesis-related risk)
- Athletes subject to WADA testing
- Use of unregulated research chemicals from unknown sources

## Confidence Assessment

### Overall Confidence: **LOW**

**Reasoning:**

1. **Tβ4 vs. TB-500 Confusion (Critical):** The single largest confidence-destroying factor is that the molecule used in clinical trials (full Tβ4, 43 amino acids) is NOT the molecule sold to and used by the community (TB-500, 7-amino-acid fragment). The clinical data should not be extrapolated to TB-500 without direct evidence.

2. **TB-500 Has Near-Zero Direct Evidence:** One 2012 analytical chemistry paper confirmed the structure of what was being sold as TB-500. That is the sum total of peer-reviewed scientific literature on TB-500 specifically.

3. **Tβ4 Clinical Data Are Promising But Limited:** The Phase 2 data for dry eye, neurotrophic keratopathy, and wound healing are positive but come from small-to-medium sized trials. No Phase 3 results are publicly available. Cardiac applications remain in early-stage trials.

4. **Mechanism Complexity:** Tβ4 "moonlights" through multiple mechanisms (actin binding, extracellular receptor signaling, fragment generation). Which of these are preserved in the 7-amino-acid TB-500 fragment is unknown.

5. **Angiogenesis Risk Unresolved:** Both Tβ4 and TB-500 promote blood vessel formation. Long-term cancer risks from chronic pro-angiogenic exposure are unknown.

6. **Independent Replication:** Unlike [[epitalon]] (which has researcher-concentration risk), Tβ4 has been studied by multiple independent groups globally. This is a strength. However, TB-500 itself has not been studied by anyone beyond the doping-control analytical chemistry community.

### Areas of Higher Confidence

- Tβ4 is the principal actin-sequestering protein in mammalian cells (well-established biochemistry)
- Tβ4's actin-binding motif is LKKTET (residues 17-23) — this is structurally validated
- Tβ4 has genuine tissue-regenerative properties demonstrated in multiple animal models and human trials
- TB-500 is a fragment of Tβ4, not a different peptide entirely — there is a plausible mechanistic basis for some effect
- WADA and Australian authorities consider TB-500 as a Tβ4-related doping substance

### Areas of Low Confidence

- Whether TB-500 produces the same effects as full Tβ4 in humans
- Whether TB-500 has the same safety profile as Tβ4
- Whether long-term angiogenic stimulation is safe
- Optimal dosing for any indication (either Tβ4 or TB-500)
- Whether TB-500 from gray-market vendors is what it's claimed to be

### Biggest Knowledge Gaps

1. **Zero clinical data on TB-500** — the community is using an unstudied fragment based on extrapolation from the full protein
2. **No pharmacokinetic data on TB-500** — half-life, bioavailability, metabolism unknown
3. **No TB-500 receptor characterization** — does the fragment bind the same extracellular targets as the full protein?
4. **Long-term cancer risk with pro-angiogenic compounds** — shared with [[bpc-157]]
5. **TB-500 product quality** — gray-market sourcing means identity, purity, and dosing are unreliable
6. **No head-to-head comparison of TB-500 vs. Tβ4** — would need to be done to validate the fragment approach
