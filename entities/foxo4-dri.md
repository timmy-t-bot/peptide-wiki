# FOXO4-DRI

## Overview
FOXO4-DRI is a synthetic D-retro-inverso peptide acting as a senolytic. It specifically targets and eliminates senescent cells by disrupting the FOXO4-p53 complex, thereby triggering p53-mediated apoptosis in those la cells.

## Mechanism of Action
In senescent cells, the transcription factor FOXO4 binds to p53, preventing p53 from inducing apoptosis. FOXO4-DRI mimics the binding site, displacing p53 and allowing the cell to execute its apoptotic program. This is a "targeted" senolysis approach.

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies
- Confirmed displacement of p53 from FOXO4 in human senescent fibroblasts.
- Selective induction of apoptosis in senescent cells vs. non-senescent cells.

### Animal Models (Mouse/Rat)
- Administration in mice resulted in the clearance of p16-positive senescent cells.
- Observed improvements in muscle regeneration and overall healthspan markers.
- **Safety Signal:** Research indicated a potential increase in pulmonary hypertension and vascular dysfunction following systemic senolysis.

### Human Studies
- Zero human exogenous trials. All data is currently preclinical.

## Health Optimization Applications
- **Senolysis:** Clearing "zombie cells" to reduce systemic inflammation (SASP).
- **Tissue Regeneration:** Potentially improving the regenerative capacity of aged tissues.

## Safety Profile
- **Critical Warning:** Potential for inducing pulmonary hypertension.
- **Long-term Safety:** Unknown; systemic removal of senescent cells may disrupt essential tissue niches.
- **Cancer Risk:** Uncertain; while clearing senescent cells can be anti-tumor, disrupting p53-mediated la cells always carries a theoretical risk.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)
*For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.*
- Dosing is limited to experimental animal models (systemic injection). No established human research protocol.

## Comparison with Related Peptides
Unlike Dasatinib + Quercetin (which targets a broader range of senescent cells), FOXO4-DRI is a more targeted approach focusing on the p53/FOXO4 axis.
