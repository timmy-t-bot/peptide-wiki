# Dihexa

Dihexa (developmental code PNB-0408; N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6) aminohexanoic amide) is an oligopeptide drug derived from angiotensin IV. It was *reportedly* claimed to bind with high affinity to hepatocyte growth factor (HGF) and potentiate its activity at the c-Met receptor, but the primary paper supporting this mechanism was retracted in 2025. It was developed at Washington State University and M3 Biotechnology (now Athira Pharma) as a potential treatment for Alzheimer's disease and other cognitive impairments.^[raw/articles/dihexa-wikipedia-2026-06-09.md]

## Overview

- **Structure:** N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6) aminohexanoic amide; formula C₂₇H₄₄N₄O₅; molar mass 504.672 g/mol
- **Class:** Angiotensin IV-derived small molecule / oligopeptide
- **Developer:** Washington State University / M3 Biotechnology (now Athira Pharma)
- **Mechanism:** HGF potentiator / c-Met receptor activator (disputed; see Mechanism section)
- **Legal status:** Research compound; no FDA or EMA approval; no approved clinical indication anywhere
- **Key controversy:** The primary mechanistic paper (Benoist et al. 2014, J Pharmacol Exp Ther) was **retracted in 2025** due to data reliability concerns.

## Mechanism of Action

Dihexa's mechanism is contested and under active scientific debate:

1. **HGF/c-Met hypothesis (disputed):** The 2014 Benoist et al. paper claimed dihexa binds HGF with high affinity and potentiates c-Met phosphorylation, leading to hippocampal spinogenesis, synaptogenesis, and improved spatial learning in the Morris water maze. This paper was retracted in April 2025 because "a review of the data and original images concluded that the data reported in the article are not sufficiently reliable to support the conclusions."^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
2. **AT4 / IRAP hypothesis:** Albiston and colleagues have presented evidence that the angiotensin IV receptor is insulin-regulated aminopeptidase (IRAP). Dihexa may act through this pathway rather than (or in addition to) HGF/c-Met.^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
3. **Stem cell differentiation:** Independent work shows dihexa (combined with vitamin C and forskolin) can substitute for growth factors in hepatic specification from human pluripotent stem cells, suggesting broader biological activity beyond neurocognition.^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]

The mechanism by which dihexa exerts procognitive effects remains unresolved. The retraction of the key HGF/c-MeT paper undermines the primary mechanistic narrative that dominated the literature for a decade.

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies
- The key HGF/c-Met mechanistic paper (Benoist et al. 2014) was retracted in 2025; no published replication of the specific HGF-binding and c-Met phosphorylation claims has appeared in the peer-reviewed literature since.^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- Hepatocyte differentiation: Dihexa + vitamin C + forskolin (VDF cocktail) induced hepatic specification from pluripotent stem cells (Pan et al. 2022; Siller et al. 2015).^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]

### Animal Models
- **Alzheimer's disease / cognitive impairment:** Multiple studies from the Wright/Harding laboratory at Washington State University reported that dihexa improved spatial working memory, passive avoidance, and memory consolidation in rodent models. These studies predate the 2025 retraction and relied on the now-retracted mechanistic framework.^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Huntington's disease (3-NP rat model):** Wells et al. 2024 tested PNB-0408 (dihexa) in 40 male Wistar rats exposed to 3-nitropropionic acid. **Dihexa did NOT protect rats from the deficits induced by 3-NP neurotoxicity.** The authors concluded: "PNB-0408 may not be an efficacious treatment strategy for preventing 3-NP-induced HD-like symptoms in a preclinical model."^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]
- **Parkinson's disease:** Earlier Wright/Harding reviews mentioned procognitive and synaptogenic effects in PD models, but these claims trace to the same lab and the retracted mechanistic paper.

### Human Studies
- **Zero human interventional trials** of dihexa have been published.
- No Phase I safety, PK, or dosing data exists in the peer-reviewed literature.

## Health Optimization Applications

- **Cognitive enhancement:** Animal models from a single research group suggested procognitive effects, but the retraction of the primary mechanistic paper and the negative Huntington's disease trial substantially weaken the evidence base.
- **Neuroprotection:** Theoretical relevance for neurodegenerative disease, but unvalidated in humans and now contested in animal models.
- **Stem cell applications:** Independent evidence for hepatocyte differentiation suggests dihexa has broader biological activity, though this is unrelated to the nootropic claims.

## Safety Profile

- **No human safety data:** Zero clinical trials; safety in humans is completely unknown.
- **Mechanistic uncertainty:** The retraction of the HGF/c-Met paper means the primary target is now unclear. Unknown off-target effects are a concern.
- **c-Met activation risk (if mechanism holds):** *Theoretical concern:* HGF/c-Met signaling promotes cell scattering, angiogenesis, and tumor metastasis. Chronic c-Met potentiation carries a theoretical oncogenic risk, though this extrapolation is not directly supported by the compiled raw sources.
- **Blood-brain barrier penetration:** Dihexa is described as BBB-permeant and orally active; if true, CNS side effects (mood, sleep, vascular) are plausible but unstudied.
- **Gray-market sourcing:** No regulated manufacturing; purity, stability, and authenticity concerns are significant given the lack of clinical-grade production.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)

No human dosing data exists. Animal studies used:
- Oral administration in Morris water maze studies (doses not consistently reported in abstracts)
- Intracerebroventricular delivery in some mechanistic studies
- Huntington's model (Wells et al. 2024): PNB-0408 administered alongside chronic 3-NP exposure; specific dose not detailed in abstract^[raw/articles/dihexa-pubmed-2026-06-09.md]

Any human dosing would be purely speculative.

## Confidence Assessment

- **Confidence: low**
- **Reasoning:** The evidence base for dihexa's procognitive effects is dominated by a single research group (Wright/Harding/WSU) and relied on a mechanistic paper that was retracted in 2025. The only independent interventional animal study (Wells et al. 2024) found **no protective effect** in a Huntington's model. Zero human trials exist. The compound's mechanism is now disputed, and the HGF/c-Met narrative is unreliable.
- **Contested:** true — the mechanism and efficacy claims are actively contested due to the retraction and the negative replication attempt.
- **Biggest gaps:** Validated mechanism of action; independent replication of procognitive effects in animal models; any human safety or efficacy data.
