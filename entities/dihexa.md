# Dihexa

## Overview
Dihexa is a synthetic oligopeptide derived from Angiotensin IV, designed to be a potent agonist of the c-Met (Hepatocyte Growth Factor receptor) pathway. It is researched primarily for its potential to reverse cognitive decline and stimulate synaptogenesis.

## Mechanism of Action
Dihexa binds with high affinity to the c-Met receptor, potentiating the activity of Hepatocyte Growth Factor (HGF). This triggers a cascade that promotes the growth of new synapses and the restoration of dendritic spines in neurons.

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies
- Demonstrates high potency in activating c-Met phosphorylation.
- Promotes neurite outgrowth in cultured neurons.

### Animal Models (Mouse/Rat)
- Significant cognitive recovery observed in rodent models of Alzheimer's disease.
- Evidence of increased synaptic density in the hippocampus.
- *Contested:* Some recent efforts to replicate the magnitude of these effects have yielded inconsistent results, and certain early claims are viewed as overstated.

### Human Studies
- No FDA-approved human trials. 
- Use is entirely anecdotal and limited to the "research chemical" community.

## Health Optimization Applications
- **Cognitive Restoration:** Theoretical use for recovering from brain injury or age-related decline.
- **Synaptogenesis:** Potential to increase neural plasticity.

## Safety Profile
- **CRITICAL CONCERN:** The HGF/c-Met pathway is a known driver of tumor growth and metastasis. Chronic activation of this pathway is strongly associated with various cancers.
- **Long-term Safety:** High risk; no human data exists to mitigate the theoretical oncogenic risk.
- **Contraindications:** Any history of malignancy or predisposition to cancer.

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)
*For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.*
- Anecdotal reports suggest intranasal administration to bypass the blood-brain barrier, though no clinical standard exists.

## Comparison with Related Peptides
Unlike standard nootropics, Dihexa is a structural modifier of the brain (synaptogenic) rather than a simple modulator of neurotransmitters. It is significantly more potent and dangerous than most cognitive enhancers.
