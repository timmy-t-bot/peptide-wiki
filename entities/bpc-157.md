# BPC-157

**Body Protection Compound-157** (also known as bepecin, PL 14736) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide (15 amino acids) with the sequence **Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val**, derived from a protective protein found in human gastric juice. It has attracted significant research and public interest for its apparent tissue-healing properties, but remains an investigational compound with no regulatory approval for human use.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

## Overview

- **Molecular formula:** C₆₂H₉₈N₁₆O₂₂ (MW: 1419.56 g/mol)
- **CAS:** 137525-51-0
- **Half-life:** <30 minutes (rat, dog; preliminary human data confirm short t½) ^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]
- **Class:** Synthetic cytoprotective / tissue repair peptide
- **Origin:** Discovered by Croatian researchers (Sikiric et al.) from gastric juice protective proteins
- **Key property:** Unusual stability in gastric juice (>24 hours at room temperature), enabling potential oral bioavailability ^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2025-01-10.md]
- **Status:** Research chemical. Not approved by FDA, EMA, or any major regulatory body. Banned by WADA (2022) under S0 (non-approved substances). Legal status varies by country; some jurisdictions have classified it as prescription-only despite no legitimate prescription pathway.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]
- **Availability:** Sold via research chemical vendors and compounding pharmacies (503A in US); widespread gray-market availability online.

## Mechanism of Action

BPC-157 operates through multiple intersecting pathways. No single receptor has been definitively identified as its primary target; its effects appear to derive from multi-pathway modulation.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

### Angiogenesis & VEGF Pathway
Upregulates **VEGFR2** and engages the **Akt-eNOS signaling axis**, promoting new blood vessel formation. This is a primary driver of wound-healing and tissue-repair effects. However, BPC-157's effect on angiogenesis is nuanced — recent arguments (Sikiric et al., 2025) propose it targets angiogenesis and NO's *cytotoxic and damaging actions* while maintaining, promoting, or recovering their *essential protective functions*, rather than simply driving undifferentiated vessel growth.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md] Regardless, VEGF activation raises theoretical concerns in cancer contexts (see Safety Profile).

### Nitric Oxide (NO) System Modulation
BPC-157 interacts bidirectionally with the **nitric oxide synthase (NOS)** pathway — it can both increase and decrease NO levels contextually, always combined with counteraction of free radical formation. This was recently validated in human tissue: BPC-157 produced **endothelium-dependent, NO-mediated vasorelaxation in human internal mammary artery rings** (Yildirim et al., 2026).^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md] NOS inhibitor L-NAME attenuated the effect, confirming NO pathway involvement.

### FAK-Paxillin & ERK1/2 Signaling
Activates the **FAK-paxillin** pathway, driving fibroblast migration and collagen organization — critical for tendon, ligament, and myotendinous junction healing. Also engages **ERK1/2 signaling** for endothelial and muscle repair.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

### Gut Mucosal Protection
Stabilizes gut tight junctions and protects gastrointestinal mucosa against damage from NSAIDs, alcohol, and other insults. This aligns with its gastric juice origin — the parent protein evolved to protect the GI tract.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2025-01-10.md]

### Anti-Inflammatory Effects
Modulates pro-inflammatory cytokines including **TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-1β**. In I/R injury models, BPC-157 reduced IL-6 immunoreactivity, downregulated p53/Bax/Casp3, and restored antioxidant defenses (SOD, TAS).^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

### Cytoprotection as a Unifying Framework
The Sikiric group proposes "cytoprotection" as a unifying concept: BPC-157 acts as a mediator that preserves endothelial integrity, normalizes microcirculation, modulates NO homeostasis, stabilizes hemostatic balance, and recruits adaptive collateral pathways — all without directly affecting the coagulation cascade.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

### Pharmacokinetics (He et al., 2022)
- **Half-life:** <30 min after IV/IM in both rats and dogs; linear dose-proportional kinetics
- **IM bioavailability:** 14–19% (rat), 45–51% (dog)
- **Metabolism:** Rapidly cleaved into small peptide fragments → single amino acids → normal amino acid metabolism
- **Excretion:** Urine and bile
- **PK/PD disconnect:** Sub-30-min plasma half-life contrasts with prolonged biological effects lasting hours to days — mechanism unexplained^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

**Critical observations from animal data:**
- Effective dose range spans 5 orders of magnitude (ng to μg/kg)
- Effective via oral, IP, IM, and local routes
- Effects appear within minutes of administration (vessel "recruitment" to injury)
- The Sikiric group accounts for the overwhelming majority of publications; independent replication by Turkish, Chinese, and Romanian groups is growing but still limited
- One paper was retracted in 2025 (electrolyte disturbances, withdrawn at authors' request)

### Dog Studies
- **PK/ADME study** (He et al., 2022): IM bioavailability 45–51%; linear kinetics; t½ <30 min; no adverse effects noted in the PK study

### Human Studies

**⚠️ Human data remain extremely limited as of June 2026.** Only three pilot studies exist, totaling <30 subjects, none randomized or controlled.

| Study | Design | n | Key Findings | Year |
|-------|--------|---|-------------|------|
| **IV Safety Pilot** (Lee & Burgess) | Open-label; 10mg then 20mg IV over 1 hour; 3-day monitoring | 2 | No adverse effects; no biomarker changes (heart, liver, kidney, thyroid, glucose); well-tolerated | 2025 |
| **Interstitial Cystitis** (Lee, Walker, Ayadi) | Open-label; 10mg injected around bladder via cystoscopy | 12 women | 10/12 complete symptom resolution; 2/12 80% improvement; all 5/5 on Global Response Assessment; no adverse events | 2024 |
| **Knee OA** (intraarticular) | Pilot; details limited in literature | small | Cited in reviews as showing benefit; no published RCT | pre-2024 |

**Summary:** Three small, uncontrolled pilot studies show consistent positive signals with no reported serious adverse events. This is promising but falls far short of clinical evidence standards. A formal Phase I/II trial has not been completed. No efficacy can be claimed for any indication.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

### Anecdotal Evidence
Extensive self-reported use in bodybuilding, biohacking, and injury recovery communities (Reddit r/Peptides, various forums). Commonly reported benefits:
- Faster injury recovery (tendons, ligaments, muscle)
- Reduced joint pain
- Gut symptom improvement (bloating, IBS-like symptoms, NSAID-induced GI issues)
- Improved sleep and mood (secondary reports)

Commonly reported side effects: mild nausea, temporary lethargy, injection site reactions, and occasional headaches.

**Anecdotal reports are not clinical evidence** and are subject to placebo effects, expectation bias, and the variability of unregulated products.

## Health Optimization Applications

### Primary Indications (Strongest Preclinical Support)
- **Soft tissue injury recovery:** Tendonitis, ligament sprains, muscle tears — strongest and most replicated animal data
- **GI health:** Gastric ulcers, NSAID-induced GI damage, anastomotic healing, fistula closure — mechanistically plausible given gastric origin
- **Wound healing:** Surgical recovery, chronic wounds, fistula management (animal data only)

### Secondary / Exploratory (Emerging Preclinical Evidence)
- **Ischemia-reperfusion injury:** Multiple organ protection demonstrated in 2025–2026 rat studies
- **Cardiovascular:** Antiarrhythmic effects; thrombosis/hemorrhage bidirectional control; vasorelaxation (human tissue confirmed)

- **Neuroprotection:** Brain-gut axis modulation; counteracted neuroleptic-induced damage; behavioral benefits in rodent models

## Safety Profile

### Known Side Effects
- **Human pilot studies:** No adverse events reported in any of the three small studies (<30 subjects total). IV infusion at 20mg well-tolerated in 2 subjects.
- **Anecdotal:** Mild nausea, lethargy, injection site pain/redness, occasional headache
- **Animal studies:** Generally well-tolerated even at high doses; LD1 not achieved in toxicology studies

### Major Concerns

#### ⚠️ Cancer / Angiogenesis Risk (CRITICAL)
VEGF upregulation is theoretically tumor-promoting. **Absolute contraindication in known or suspected cancers.** The Sikiric group argues BPC-157's angiogenesis effect is context-dependent and protective rather than undifferentiated, but this framework is based entirely on animal data. No long-term human cancer surveillance data exist. Until resolved, the precautionary principle applies: do not use in the presence of any malignancy or elevated cancer risk.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

#### ⚠️ Unknown Long-Term Effects
No multi-year human safety data. Peptide compounds with pleiotropic effects can have unpredictable long-term metabolic, vascular, and immunological consequences.

#### ⚠️ Purity / Sourcing / Regulation
- Research chemical market is unregulated. Contaminants, mislabeled products, and incorrect dosing are common risks.
- BPC-157 from compounding pharmacies (503A) is not FDA-approved; quality standards vary.
- BPC-157 is banned by WADA under S0. Athletes subject to doping control should not use it.

#### ⚠️ PK/PD Disconnect
The sub-30-minute half-life with prolonged biological effects is unexplained. This raises questions about active metabolites (not yet characterized), depot effects, or irreversible target binding — all of which have safety implications.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

#### Other Theoretical Risks
- **Bleeding risk:** Theoretical interaction with anticoagulants via endothelial modulation. However, Sikiric et al. (2026) claim BPC-157 counteracts both hemorrhage AND thrombosis without affecting coagulation cascade directly.
- **Drug interactions:** NSAID interaction paradox — BPC-157 protects against NSAID GI damage but full interaction profile is unknown.
- **Neurotransmitter modulation:** BPC-157 may modulate dopamine, serotonin, glutamate, GABA, and acetylcholine systems in rats (preliminary observations); implications for psychiatric medications unknown.

### Contraindications
- Active or suspected cancer (any type)
- Pregnancy and breastfeeding (zero safety data)
- Bleeding disorders or anticoagulant therapy (theoretical risk; evidence debated)
- Athletes subject to WADA testing (banned substance)
- Concurrent use with other unregulated research compounds

BPC-157 is a research chemical that has not been approved for human use by any regulatory agency. Its human safety and efficacy profiles are unestablished. Use outside of supervised research settings is not recommended.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2026-06-08.md]

| Route | Typical Community Range | Preclinical Reference | Duration |
|-------|------------------------|----------------------|----------|
| **Subcutaneous** | 250–500 mcg/day (often split BID) | 10 μg/kg/day in rats | 2–8 weeks |
| **Intramuscular (local)** | 250–500 mcg near injury site | Local injection effective in rats | 2–4 weeks |
| **Oral** | 500 mcg/day (requires acid-stable formulation) | 10 μg–10 ng/kg oral effective in rats | 4–8 weeks |
| **Intravenous** | 10–20 mg studied in pilot (n=2) | Not established | Single-dose studied only |

### Key PK Considerations
- Half-life <30 min suggests frequent dosing may be needed for sustained effect — yet effects last hours to days (unexplained)
- Oral route is plausible due to gastric acid stability, but human oral bioavailability is uncharacterized
- IM bioavailability varies significantly by species (14–51%)

### Cycling
- No established cycling protocol. Most community users run 4–8 week courses.
- Some practitioners recommend time off equal to duration on (no evidence base).

### Synergistic Stacks (Research Context)
- **TB-500 / TB-4 ([[thymosin-beta-4]])**: Most common pairing. BPC-157 for localized healing, TB-500 for systemic recovery. Both promote angiogenesis and cell migration through complementary pathways.^[raw/articles/bpc-157-research-2025-01-10.md]
- **GH secretagogues** (e.g., [[ipamorelin]], [[cjc-1295]]): May amplify tissue remodeling via IGF-1 axis. Both animal and anecdotal support for this combination.
- **GHK-Cu ([[copper-peptide]])**: Emerging interest as a complementary wound-healing and collagen-synthesis peptide. Different mechanisms (copper-dependent vs. VEGF-driven).

## Regulatory & Legal Status

- **FDA:** Not approved. Not scheduled but falls under FDA's regulatory authority if marketed with health claims.
- **EMA:** Not approved.
- **WADA:** Prohibited under S0 (non-approved substances) since 2022. Applies at all times (in and out of competition).
- **Country-specific:** Some jurisdictions (e.g., certain US states, Australia) have restricted BPC-157 as prescription-only despite no approved prescription pathway. Sale via research chemical vendors and compounding pharmacies exists in a legal gray zone.
- **Patents:** Multiple patents filed and granted (Jozwiak et al., 2025 review). Original Croatian patents cover gastric juice-derived peptide sequences.

### Biggest Knowledge Gaps
1. **No human RCTs for any indication** — the single largest barrier to understanding
2. **Long-term cancer risk with chronic VEGF modulation unknown** — angiogenesis debate unresolved
3. **Oral bioavailability in humans uncharacterized** — despite gastric stability, actual absorption kinetics unknown
4. **Optimal human dosing entirely extrapolated** from animal data and community trial-and-error
5. **Receptor/primary target unidentified** — mechanism is downstream effects, not a known receptor binding
6. **Metabolite activity unknown** — prolonged effects despite short half-life suggest active metabolites or irreversible effects
7. **Research group concentration** — Sikiric group dominates publications (~80%+); independent replication growing but still limited
