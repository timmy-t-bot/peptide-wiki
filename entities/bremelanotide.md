# Bremelanotide (PT-141)

## Overview

Bremelanotide (PT-141, brand name Vyleesi) is a synthetic cyclic heptapeptide melanocortin receptor agonist approved by the FDA in June 2019 for the treatment of acquired, generalized hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in premenopausal women. It is administered via subcutaneous injection using a prefilled autoinjector pen. Bremelanotide is an active metabolite of [[melanotan-ii]] and was developed by Palatin Technologies. The FDA considers it a first-in-class medication.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**Key facts:**
- **Chemical structure**: Ac-Nle-cyclo[Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys]-OH (cyclic heptapeptide lactam analogue of α-MSH)
- **Molecular weight**: 1025.2 Da (free base)
- **Route**: Subcutaneous injection, 1.75 mg/0.3 mL via autoinjector
- **Half-life**: 2.7 hours (range 1.9–4.0 hours)
- **Bioavailability**: ~100% with SC administration
- **Legal status**: US: ℞-only (prescription); not a controlled substance
- **Current marketer**: Cosette Pharmaceuticals, Inc. (acquired from AMAG Pharmaceuticals)

Bremelanotide is **not indicated** for postmenopausal women, men, or sexual performance enhancement. It is not approved for any use outside HSDD in premenopausal women.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Mechanism of Action

Bremelanotide is a non-selective agonist at melanocortin receptors MC1 through MC5 (excluding MC2, the ACTH receptor). At therapeutic doses, binding to MC1R and MC4R is most relevant.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**MC4R — Sexual desire pathway (proposed):** Neurons expressing MC4R are present in the medial preoptic area (mPOA) of the hypothalamus. Activation of presynaptic MC4Rs on these neurons is thought to increase dopamine release, an excitatory neurotransmitter that enhances sexual desire. This is the proposed mechanism for HSDD treatment, though the FDA label states "the mechanism by which VYLEESI improves HSDD in women is unknown."^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**MC1R — Pigmentation:** MC1R is expressed on melanocytes; binding at this receptor leads to melanin expression and increased pigmentation, explaining the hyperpigmentation side effect.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**MC4R — Appetite regulation:** MC4R plays a central role in appetite regulation. Agonism at this receptor promotes satiety, which may explain observed reductions in caloric intake and body weight in obese women.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**MC3R/MC4R — Other effects:** Shown in cell-culture models: activation of MC3R and MC4R by bremelanotide reduced survivin expression and induced cell death in human glioblastoma cell lines, and sensitized cells to temozolomide.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Research Evidence

### Cell/In-Vitro Studies

- **Glioblastoma (Suzuki et al., 2024):** Bremelanotide reduced survivin expression and induced cell death in human glioblastoma cell lines at concentrations not toxic to normal human cells. Effects were blocked by MC3R/MC4R antagonists. Bremelanotide also promoted cell death induced by temozolomide and osimertinib. These are in-vitro findings only; no in-vivo or human cancer data exist.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Animal Models (Mouse/Rat)

- **Penile erection (rat, nonhuman primate):** Shown in rat and nonhuman primate models: systemic administration of PT-141 induces penile erections. Administration to rats activates neurons in the hypothalamus (c-Fos immunoreactivity). Neurons in the same CNS region take up pseudorabies virus injected into the corpus cavernosum.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Female sexual behavior (rat):** Shown in rat models: pre-copulatory behaviors analogous to sexual arousal are evoked by melanocortin agonists.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Developmental toxicity (dog, mouse):** In pregnant dogs, SC bremelanotide at ≥2 mg/kg/day (≥16x human AUC) produced embryofetal toxicity (3-8x post-implantation loss vs controls). In mice, developmental delays in F1 offspring at ≥30 mg/kg/day (≥125x human AUC). No developmental NOEL established in either species.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Fertility (mouse):** No effects on fertility in male (75 mg/kg/day, ~375x human AUC) or female (150 mg/kg/day, ~760x human AUC) mice.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Carcinogenicity (rat, mouse):** No significant increases in tumor incidence in 2-year carcinogenicity studies with intranasal (rat) and subcutaneous (mouse) administration.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Human Studies

#### Phase 3 RECONNECT Trials (Kingsberg et al., 2019)
Two identical 24-week randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials (n=1,267 premenopausal women with HSDD). Bremelanotide 1.75 mg SC as-needed vs placebo.

**Co-primary endpoints (both statistically significant):**
- FSFI Desire Domain score: +0.35 vs placebo (integrated, p<.001). Baseline ~2.1, scale 1.2–6.0.
- FSDS-DAO Item 13 (distress from low desire): -0.33 vs placebo (integrated, p<.001). Baseline ~2.9, scale 0–4.

**Secondary endpoint — Satisfying Sexual Events (SSEs):** No significant difference between treatment groups (p=0.76 and p=0.70 in the two studies).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**AE-related discontinuation:** 18% VYLEESI vs 2% placebo.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Efficacy Critiques
Independent re-analyses have raised substantial concerns about the RECONNECT trials:

- **Spielmans (2021):** 72.72% of protocol-listed outcomes were not reported. AE-induced discontinuation OR=11.98 (NNH=6). Participants preferred placebo: OR=0.30 (NNH=4) for completing trial + entering open-label extension.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Spielmans & Ellefson (2023):** Validity evidence for FSFI and FSDS-DAO in HSDD women is questionable. 8 of 11 clinicaltrials.gov-specified outcomes were unpublished. Effect sizes ranged from nil to small.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Mintzes et al. (2021):** Bremelanotide led to zero additional satisfying sexual events vs placebo. Contested indication; politicized approval process capitalizing on flibanserin precedent.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Phase 2 Studies
- **Diamond et al. (2006):** Phase 2a, 18 premenopausal women with FSAD, intranasal 20 mg. More women reported moderate/high desire vs placebo (p=0.0114). Vaginal vasocongestion (photoplethysmography) did not change significantly.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Althof et al. (2019):** Phase 2b dose-ranging. MCID established. 1.75 mg dose achieved statistical significance on all 7 endpoints vs placebo (p≤.03).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Molinoff et al. (2003):** Early study in normal men and ED patients. Dose-dependent increase in erectile activity with intranasal PT-141.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Weight Loss (Phase 1)
- **Spana et al. (2022):** Two Phase 1 RCTs in obese premenopausal women (BMI >30). Bremelanotide 3x daily for 16 days: -1.3 kg vs placebo (p<.0001). Caloric intake reduced ~400 kcal/day. Twice-daily dosing also produced significant weight loss. These are Phase 1 findings only; no Phase 2/3 weight loss trials have been conducted.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

#### Korean Bridging Study
- **NCT04943068 (completed 2023):** Phase 3 bridging trial in Korean premenopausal women with HSDD (n=193). Results not yet published.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Health Optimization Applications

**Approved indication only:** Bremelanotide is FDA-approved exclusively for acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women. All other uses are off-label and lack regulatory evaluation.

**Off-label areas with some research data (all preliminary):**

- **Male sexual function:** Early Phase 2 data showed erectile activity in men with ED (intranasal formulation). Development for male ED was halted by FDA in 2007 due to blood pressure concerns. No approved male indication.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Weight loss:** Phase 1 data in obese women showed ~400 kcal/day calorie reduction and ~1.3 kg weight loss over 16 days with 3x daily dosing. MC4R agonism is a validated weight loss mechanism (setmelanotide is FDA-approved for syndromic obesity). However, bremelanotide has no Phase 2/3 weight loss trials and is not approved for this use. Daily dosing substantially increases hyperpigmentation risk (38% incidence with 8 days of daily dosing).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

- **Libido enhancement in non-HSDD populations:** Not indicated. No trials in populations without diagnosed HSDD. The FDA label explicitly states "not indicated to enhance sexual performance."^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

**Gray-market use:** Bremelanotide (as PT-141) is sold through unregulated peptide vendors for off-label purposes including male sexual enhancement and tanning. These products are not FDA-reviewed, may differ from pharmaceutical-grade Vyleesi, and carry unknown purity and safety risks.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Safety Profile

Bremelanotide has an FDA-approved safety profile based on a clinical development program of ~3,500 subjects across 43 studies. The safety profile is extensively characterized, but tolerability limitations are significant.

| Vomiting | 4.8% | 0.2% |

### Key Safety Warnings (FDA Label)

1. **Transient blood pressure increase:** SBP +6 mmHg, DBP +3 mmHg, peaking 2–4 hours post-dose, resolving within ~12 hours. HR decrease ~5 bpm. Contraindicated in uncontrolled hypertension or known cardiovascular disease. Not recommended for patients at high cardiovascular risk.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

2. **Focal hyperpigmentation:** 1% incidence at ≤8 doses/month. 38% with 8 days of daily dosing; additional 14% with 8 more days. May involve face, gingiva, breasts. May not resolve after discontinuation. Higher risk with darker skin.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

3. **Nausea:** 40% incidence. Median onset within 1 hour, duration ~2 hours. 13% required anti-emetics. 8% discontinued due to nausea. Improves with second dose for most patients. Ondansetron pretreatment (8 mg PO 30 min prior) did NOT reduce nausea incidence in a Phase 4 study.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Serious Adverse Events
- 1.1% VYLEESI vs 0.5% placebo in Phase 3.
- One case of acute hepatitis reported in open-label extension (transaminases >40x ULN, bilirubin 6x ULN). Resolved 4 months after discontinuation. Causal role not definitively established.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Discontinuation
- 18% VYLEESI vs 2% placebo due to adverse reactions.
- Most common reasons: nausea (8%), headache (2%), vomiting (1%), flushing (1%).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Drug Interactions
- Slows gastric motility → may reduce rate/extent of oral drug absorption.
- Clinically significant reduction in naltrexone and indomethacin exposure.
- Avoid with oral naltrexone for alcohol/opioid addiction (treatment failure risk).
- No clinically significant alcohol interaction (unlike flibanserin).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Pregnancy & Lactation
- Animal studies: fetal harm at ≥16x human AUC (dogs). No human pregnancy data sufficient for risk assessment.
- Pregnancy exposure registry exists. Effective contraception recommended.
- Lactation: No human milk data. Phase 4 lactation study completed 2025, results pending.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Long-term Safety
- No carcinogenicity signal in 2-year rodent studies.
- No fertility impairment in mice at high multiples of human exposure.
- No REMS program required (unlike flibanserin's former alcohol REMS).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Dosing Protocols (Research Context)

### FDA-Approved Dosing (Vyleesi)
- **Dose:** 1.75 mg subcutaneously via autoinjector
- **Timing:** At least 45 minutes before anticipated sexual activity
- **Frequency:** Maximum 1 dose per 24 hours; maximum 8 doses per month
- **Duration:** Discontinue after 8 weeks if no improvement
- **Administration sites:** Abdomen or thigh
- **Onset:** Median Tmax ~1 hour (range 0.5–1.0 hours)
- **Duration of effect:** Unknown; optimal window not fully characterized

### Research Dosing (from clinical trials)
- **Phase 1 weight loss studies:** 2.5 mg SC twice daily or three times daily for 8–16 days. Note: daily dosing produced 38% hyperpigmentation rate.
- **Phase 2 ED studies (discontinued):** Intranasal formulation, various doses. Development halted 2007.
- **Phase 2a FSD study:** Intranasal 20 mg single dose.

### Gray-Market Dosing (anecdotal, not medically supervised)
Anecdotal reports from peptide forums describe PT-141 dosing ranging from 0.5–2.0 mg SC for male sexual enhancement. These protocols are not FDA-reviewed, not supported by clinical trials, and carry unknown risks. No systematic safety data exist for male use or for gray-market-sourced product.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Comparison with Related Peptides

### Bremelanotide vs [[melanotan-ii]]
Bremelanotide is an active metabolite of melanotan II, differing by a hydroxyl group where melanotan II has a C-terminal amide. Both are cyclic heptapeptide MC receptor agonists. Melanotan II was developed primarily as a tanning agent; bremelanotide was developed for sexual dysfunction. Melanotan II is unapproved in all jurisdictions; bremelanotide is FDA-approved (US only, prescription). Melanotan II has stronger MC1R tanning effects; bremelanotide was optimized for MC4R-mediated sexual effects.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Bremelanotide vs Setmelanotide
Both are MC4R agonists. Setmelanotide (Imcivree) is FDA-approved (2020) for syndromic obesity due to POMC, PCSK1, or LEPR deficiency. Bremelanotide is approved for HSDD. Setmelanotide is a more selective MC4R agonist; bremelanotide is non-selective. Both share nausea as a prominent side effect. Setmelanotide is administered daily; bremelanotide is as-needed (≤8 doses/month).^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

### Bremelanotide vs Flibanserin (Addyi)
Both are FDA-approved for HSDD in premenopausal women. Flibanserin is a daily oral 5-HT1A agonist/5-HT2A antagonist; bremelanotide is an as-needed SC MC receptor agonist. Flibanserin had an alcohol REMS (later removed); bremelanotide has no alcohol interaction. Flibanserin's primary side effects include hypotension, syncope, somnolence; bremelanotide's are nausea, flushing, BP increase. Both have modest efficacy that has been critiqued by independent researchers.^[raw/articles/bremelanotide-research-2026-06-09.md]

## Confidence Assessment

**Overall confidence: medium**

- **Mechanism of action:** High confidence. MC receptor pharmacology is well-characterized. The specific CNS pathway for sexual desire improvement is proposed but not definitively established (FDA label states mechanism is "unknown").
- **Safety profile:** High confidence. FDA-approved drug with ~3,500 subjects in clinical program, post-marketing surveillance, and detailed label.
- **Efficacy for HSDD:** Medium confidence. Statistically significant on co-primary endpoints in two Phase 3 trials, but effect sizes are modest, validity of outcome measures has been questioned, and there was no improvement in satisfying sexual events. Independent re-analyses raise substantial concerns about trial conduct and reporting.
- **Off-label optimization uses:** Low confidence. No Phase 2/3 trials for male sexual function, weight loss, or performance enhancement. Weight loss data is Phase 1 only (n=~57). Male ED development was halted. Gray-market use lacks any systematic safety or efficacy data.

The confidence rating reflects the weakest dimension: while bremelanotide is an approved drug with well-characterized pharmacology and safety, its efficacy is contested and its use for human optimization outside the narrow approved indication is unsupported by adequate clinical evidence.
