# Ipamorelin

## Overview

Ipamorelin (INN; development code NNC 26-0161) is a synthetic pentapeptide and selective agonist of the ghrelin/growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a). It was developed by **Novo Nordisk** as a growth hormone (GH) secretagogue, derived from GHRP-1 through a medicinal chemistry program that eliminated the central Ala-Trp dipeptide. The amino acid sequence is **Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2** (molecular formula: C~38~H~49~N~9~O~5, MW: 711.87 g/mol).

Ipamorelin is distinguished from other GHRPs (GHRP-2, GHRP-6, hexarelin) by its **high selectivity for GH release** — it does not significantly increase ACTH, cortisol, or prolactin levels, even at doses >200-fold above the ED~50~ for GH release. This property has made it the preferred GHRP in many research and non-clinical contexts.

Currently, ipamorelin is **not an approved pharmaceutical drug** in any jurisdiction. It is marketed as a "research chemical" and is a **WADA-prohibited substance** (class S2: Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances, and Mimetics).

## Mechanism of Action

Ipamorelin binds to and activates the **ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a)**, a G-protein-coupled receptor expressed in the pituitary and hypothalamus. Activation stimulates GH release from somatotroph cells in the anterior pituitary.

Key mechanistic features:

- **GHS-R1a agonism:** Like ghrelin, ipamorelin acts through the GHS-R1a receptor, distinct from the GHRH receptor.
- **Selectivity for GH:** Unlike GHRP-2 and GHRP-6, ipamorelin does not stimulate ACTH/cortisol or prolactin secretion. This selectivity is comparable to GHRH itself (Raun et al., 1998).
- **PK profile:** In humans, terminal half-life is approximately **2 hours**, clearance 0.078 L/h/kg, volume of distribution at steady-state 0.22 L/kg. GH peak occurs at ~0.67 hours post-dose (Gobburu et al., 1999).
- **Potential ghrelin-mediated mechanisms:** Animal data suggest ipamorelin may act partly by stimulating endogenous ghrelin release from gastric tissue (Ahnfelt-Rønne et al., 2001). [citation: raw/articles/ipamorelin-supplemental-sources-2026-06-09.md]
- **Insulin secretion:** Shown in rat pancreatic tissue to stimulate insulin release via calcium channel and adrenergic receptor pathways (Adeghate & Ponery, 2004).

## Animal Model Evidence

### Shown in Rat Models:

**Bone:**
- **Longitudinal bone growth:** Dose-dependent increase in tibial growth rate (42→52 µm/day) in adult female rats treated 3× daily for 15 days with ipamorelin (0, 18, 90, 450 µg/day SC). Body weight gain also increased dose-dependently. No changes in total IGF-I or bone turnover markers (Johansen et al., 1999). [PMID: 10373343]
- **Bone mineral content:** 12 weeks of continuous ipamorelin (0.5 mg/kg/day SC via osmotic minipump) increased total BMC by DXA and cross-sectional cortical bone area by pQCT in adult female rats. Volumetric BMD was unchanged — effects attributed to increased bone dimensions rather than increased mineral density (Svensson et al., 2000). [PMID: 10828840]
- **Counteracts glucocorticoid bone loss:** Ipamorelin (100 µg/kg 3× daily, 3 months) combined with methylprednisolone (9 mg/kg/day) produced a 4-fold increase in periosteal bone formation rate vs. steroid alone. Maximum tetanic tension of calf muscles also increased significantly (Andersen et al., 2001). [PMID: 11735244]

**Body Composition and Metabolism:**
- **Body weight gain:** Chronic ipamorelin treatment stimulated body weight gain and GH release in young female rats (Jiménez-Reina et al., 2002). [PMID: 12168778]
- **Counteracts catabolic steroids:** Ipamorelin (0.4 or 1.6 mg/kg/day IV, 10 days) reduced methylprednisolone-induced body weight loss, increased IGF-I levels (Malmlöf et al., 1999). [PMID: 10629165]
- **Nitrogen balance:** Ipamorelin (0.5 mg/kg/day) reduced prednisolone-induced urea synthesis by 20%, improved nitrogen balance, and normalized organ nitrogen content — similar but weaker than direct GH treatment (Aagaard et al., 2009). [PMID: 19231263]

### Shown in Mouse Models:

- **GH hypersecretion in diabetes:** Streptozotocin-diabetic mice showed exaggerated GH response to ipamorelin (150 vs. 62 µg/L in non-diabetic mice), with hepatic GH resistance and low IGF-I (Johansen et al., 2003). [PMID: 14630569]
- **GH-independent adiposity:** In GH-deficient (lit/lit) and GH-intact mice, ipamorelin increased body fat by GH-independent mechanisms — increased serum leptin and food intake. This is a notable finding: GHSs can increase adiposity via non-GH pathways (Lall et al., 2001). [PMID: 11162489]
- **CJC-1295 + ipamorelin combination:** Improved maximum tetanic tension in murine glucocorticoid-induced muscle loss model — cited in Mayfield et al. (2026) review. [PMID: 41476424]

### Shown in Other Species:

- **Swine:** Ipamorelin released GH with ED~50~ = 2.3 nmol/kg and E~max~ = 65 ng GH/mL plasma. Comparable to GHRP-6 but with no ACTH/cortisol elevation (Raun et al., 1998). [PMID: 9849822]
- **Ferrets:** Ipamorelin (1–3 mg/kg IP) inhibited cisplatin-induced weight loss (~24% reduction in delayed phase), but did not affect emesis (Lu et al., 2024). [PMID: 39043357]
- **Rats (GI motility):** Accelerated gastric emptying post-surgery; ipamorelin (0.014 µmol/kg IV) reduced gastric retention from 78% to 52% via cholinergic excitatory neurons (Greenwood-Van Meerveld et al., 2012). [PMID: 27186127]
- **Fish (tilapia):** Ipamorelin increased spermatogenesis, LH, and 11-ketotestosterone in Oreochromis mossambicus (Gouda & Ganesh, 2024). [PMID: 38996787]

## Human Evidence

### Phase II Clinical Trial (Postoperative Ileus)

**Study:** Beck et al. (2014) — NCT00672074. Multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase II trial.

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Sponsor | Helsinn Therapeutics (U.S.), Inc. |
| Enrollment | 117 patients |
| Population | Adults undergoing small/large bowel resection |
| Intervention | Ipamorelin 0.03 mg/kg IV twice daily × 7 days or until discharge |
| Comparator | Placebo |
| Primary endpoint | Time to first tolerated solid meal |

**Results:**
- Median time to first tolerated meal: **25.3 h (ipamorelin) vs. 32.6 h (placebo)** — p = 0.15 (NOT statistically significant)
- No significant differences in any secondary efficacy endpoints
- Adverse events: 87.5% (ipamorelin) vs. 94.8% (placebo) — well tolerated
- **Conclusion:** Ipamorelin was safe but showed **no significant efficacy** for postoperative ileus

**Outcome:** The development program was **discontinued** due to lack of efficacy (AdisInsight, Springer). No further clinical development has been reported.

Source: Beck et al. (2014). [PMID: 25331030]; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT00672074

### Human Pharmacokinetic Study

Gobburu et al. (1999) — 40 healthy male volunteers. Dose-escalation design (5 infusion rates, 8 subjects each). Demonstrated dose-proportional PK, 2-hour half-life, and single-episode GH release profile. This remains the primary human PK/PD reference. [PMID: 10496658]

### Athletic Doping Cases

- **Jodie Meeks** (NBA, Washington Wizards): Suspended 25 games in April 2018 for ipamorelin use.
- **Tyson Chandler** (NBA, Nets): Suspended 25 games in August 2019 for ipamorelin use.
- Multiple athletes investigated by MLB (Perez, USA TODAY, 2016).

### Anti-Doping Detection

Ipamorelin is on the WADA Prohibited List. Detection methods include:
- Urine LC-MS/MS for parent compound and metabolite Ipamorelin (1-4) free acid (Semenistaya et al., 2015). [PMID: 25869809]
- Glycine-modified analogs identified in seized doping materials (Gajda et al., 2019). [PMID: 30136411]
- Metabolites detectable for extended windows after nasal administration.

## Safety Profile

### ⚠️ SAFETY CONCERNS

1. **Not an approved pharmaceutical:** Ipamorelin has not received regulatory approval from the FDA, EMA, or any other major regulatory body.
2. **Discontinued development:** The only Phase II clinical trial showed **no significant efficacy**; no further clinical development is ongoing.
3. **Unknown long-term safety:** No long-term human safety data exist. All long-term data are from animal studies.
4. **GH-independent adiposity:** Animal data show ipamorelin can increase body fat through non-GH pathways (Lall et al., 2001). The clinical significance of this in humans is unknown.
5. **Gray-market product quality:** As a research chemical, ipamorelin products are not subject to pharmaceutical quality control. Seized products have contained modified analogs (glycine-extended versions) and unknown contaminants (Gajda et al., 2019).
6. **WADA prohibition:** Ipamorelin is banned in competitive sport. Athletes face suspension.
7. **Legal status:** Unapproved research chemical in most jurisdictions. Not scheduled as a controlled substance in the US, but sold only for "research purposes." Prescription status varies by country.
8. **Insulin effects:** Animal data show ipamorelin stimulates insulin release from pancreatic tissue — implications for glucose homeostasis in humans are unknown (Adeghate & Ponery, 2004).
9. **Recent review consensus:** 2026 sports medicine reviews uniformly classify ipamorelin as **investigational** with **uncertain safety profiles**, **product quality concerns**, and **widespread antidoping restrictions** (Villegas Meza et al., 2026; Mendias & Awan, 2026; Mayfield et al., 2026).

### Known Side Effects from Clinical Trial (Beck et al., 2014):
- No statistically significant increase in adverse events vs. placebo
- Well tolerated at 0.03 mg/kg IV twice daily for up to 7 days in postoperative setting
- Specific side effect profile not comprehensively characterized

## Common Research Dosing (Anecdotal/Animal-Extrapolated)

> ⚠️ **For educational purposes only. Not medical advice.** Ipamorelin is not an approved drug. No established human therapeutic dosing exists. The following reflects common research and anecdotal protocols only.

Anecdotal protocols circulating in research communities typically involve:

- **Subcutaneous injection:** 100–300 µg, 1–3× daily (often fasted or pre-bed)
- **Combination with CJC-1295 (no DAC):** 100–200 µg each, usually pre-bed, as a "classic stack"
- **Cycle duration:** 8–12 weeks on, followed by breaks

These protocols are **not validated** in clinical trials. The 2026 sports medicine reviews explicitly note that "information regarding the indications, dosing, frequency, and duration of treatment remains unknown" (Mayfield et al., 2026).

Dosing extrapolation note: The Phase II trial used 0.03 mg/kg IV (~2.1 mg for 70 kg person) — much higher than anecdotal SC doses, and via IV not SC.

## Key Knowledge Gaps

1. No rigorous human efficacy trials for any musculoskeletal, metabolic, or performance indication
2. No long-term (>7 days) safety data in humans
3. Optimal dosing, frequency, and cycle duration unknown
4. No studies on interaction with CJC-1295 or other GHRH analogs in humans
5. GH-independent adiposity effect from animal data has not been investigated in humans
6. Insulin/glucose homeostasis effects not studied in humans
7. Product quality and purity of gray-market research chemicals is unregulated
8. No biomarker validation for monitoring efficacy or safety

## Recent Research Trends (2024–2026)

The PubMed database returned 52 results as of June 2026, with 7 papers indexed in 2026 alone. The recent literature shows a notable shift:

- **2024–2026:** Multiple review papers in orthopaedic and sports medicine journals (JBJS Reviews, American Journal of Sports Medicine, Sports Medicine, JAAOS Global Research Reviews) examining injectable peptides — all concluding there is **insufficient evidence** for clinical use of ipamorelin.
- **Aging/longevity:** Appears in gerontology reviews as a growth hormone modulator for healthy aging, but classified as investigational with lack of long-term safety data (Mavrych et al., 2026).
- **Doping:** Growing concern about gray-market peptides in recreational and professional sports (Coutinho et al., 2026).
- **Combination research:** Increasingly studied as part of the CJC-1295 + ipamorelin combination rather than as monotherapy, though no human combination trials exist.
