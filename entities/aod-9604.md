# AOD-9604

## Overview

AOD-9604 (Anti-Obesity Drug-9604) is a synthetic 16-amino acid peptide fragment of the C-terminal domain of human growth hormone (hGH), corresponding to residues 177–191 of hGH with a tyrosine substitution at the N-terminus (replacing the native phenylalanine at position 176). It was developed by **Metabolic Pharmaceuticals Pty Ltd** (Australia) in the late 1990s as an anti-obesity therapeutic, based on the observation that this hGH fragment retained the lipolytic (fat-burning) properties of full-length growth hormone without stimulating IGF-1 production or causing the diabetogenic side effects associated with hGH. [1][2]

**Molecular details:**
- **Sequence:** H-Tyr-Leu-Arg-Ile-Val-Gln-Cys(1)-Arg-Ser-Val-Glu-Gly-Ser-Cys(1)-Gly-Phe-OH
- **Abbreviation:** YLRIVQCRSVEGSCGF
- **CAS:** 221231-10-3
- **Formula:** C₇₈H₁₂₃N₂₃O₂₃S₂
- **Molar mass:** 1815.10 g·mol⁻¹

Development was terminated in **2007** after a 24-week clinical trial failed to demonstrate sufficient weight-loss efficacy, despite a promising 12-week Phase IIa trial (mean additional weight loss of 1.8 kg vs placebo). The company burned through approximately **$50 million** in development before abandoning the drug. [1][3]

Despite its failure as a pharmaceutical, AOD-9604 has persisted on the **gray market** and in sports doping. It is currently **banned by WADA** under category S0 (non-approved substances) and is routinely tested for in anti-doping controls. [4][5]

### Notable Doping Cases

- **Essendon Football Club supplements saga (2012–2016):** AOD-9604 was a central compound in the Australian Football League's largest doping scandal. The peptide was administered to Essendon players as part of a supplement program. WADA clarified on 22 April 2013 that AOD-9604 had been considered banned under S0 since 2011. However, the Australian Sports Anti-Doping Authority (ASADA) ultimately did not pursue AOD-9604-specific charges, determining that achieving a guilty verdict for a substance not yet explicitly declared banned would be too difficult. The case instead focused on [[TB-500]] (thymosin beta-4). [6]

- **Belgium seizure (2014):** Belgian authorities confiscated unknown pharmaceutical preparations containing AOD9604, demonstrating its presence on the European black market. [7]

## Mechanism of Action

### β₃-Adrenergic Receptor Upregulation (Mouse Model)

The foundational mechanistic study (Heffernan et al., 2001) demonstrated that AOD-9604:

1. **Increases β₃-adrenergic receptor (β₃-AR) mRNA expression** in adipose tissue of obese mice
2. β₃-AR is the major lipolytic receptor on adipocytes (fat cells)
3. In **β₃-AR knockout mice**, long-term AOD-9604 treatment **failed** to produce weight loss or increased lipolysis
4. However, in an **acute experiment**, AOD-9604 still increased energy expenditure and fat oxidation even in β₃-AR knockout mice

**Critical nuance:** The lipolytic action is NOT mediated *directly* through β₃-AR binding. Rather, AOD-9604 upregulates β₃-AR expression, which then sensitizes adipose tissue to catecholamine-driven lipolysis. An additional acute mechanism may exist independent of β₃-AR. [2]

Shown in mouse models:
- ↑ β₃-AR mRNA expression in white adipose tissue
- ↓ Body weight and body fat in obese (ob/ob) mice over 14 days of chronic IP administration
- ↑ Lipolytic sensitivity
- Acute ↑ in energy expenditure and fat oxidation (β₃-AR-independent pathway)
- Failure of chronic effects in β₃-AR knockout mice

Human evidence:
- The presumed mechanism (lipolysis without IGF-1 elevation) was the basis for human trials but was never directly confirmed mechanistically in humans [citation needed]

### Non-IGF-1, Non-Diabetogenic Profile

Unlike full-length hGH, AOD-9604 was specifically designed to avoid:
- **IGF-1 stimulation** — confirmed in initial human safety studies [1]
- **Diabetogenic effects** — hGH induces insulin resistance; AOD-9604 was reported not to do this (Cox et al., 2015) [4]
- **Growth-promoting/mitogenic effects** — the fragment lacks the receptor domains required for chondrocyte/osteoblast proliferation

However, a 2026 review (Mendias & Awan) cautions that **"rigorous human safety data are scarce"** and that **"there is potential for serious harm to patients"** from unapproved peptides like AOD-9604. [8]

## Research Evidence

### Cell Studies

No specific in vitro mechanistic studies on isolated adipocytes were identified in PubMed. The mechanism is inferred from whole-animal studies.

### Animal Studies

| Study | Model | Key Finding |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Heffernan et al., 2001 | Obese (ob/ob) mice; β₃-AR KO mice | ↓ Body weight, ↓ body fat, ↑ β₃-AR mRNA. Effects abolished in β₃-AR KO. Acute fat oxidation preserved in KO. [2] |
| Kwon & Park, 2015 | Collagenase-induced knee OA rabbit model | Intra-articular AOD9604 enhanced cartilage regeneration. Combined AOD9604 + HA most effective. ↓ Lameness period. [9] |

Shown in mouse models:
- Weight loss and fat reduction comparable to full hGH in obese mice
- Upregulation of lipolytic receptor expression
- β₃-AR-dependent for chronic effects, but acute β₃-AR-independent pathway also exists

Shown in rabbit models:
- Cartilage regeneration when injected intra-articularly (OA model) — a finding unrelated to the original lipolytic indication

### Human Studies

**Phase IIa (12-week trial):**
- Subjects receiving AOD-9604 lost, on average, **1.8 kg more** than those receiving placebo [3]
- Reported in Valentino et al. (2010) review of anti-obesity pharmacotherapy

**Phase IIb (24-week trial):**
- **Failed** to demonstrate sufficient lipolytic effect
- Development terminated in 2007 by Metabolic Pharmaceuticals [1][3]
- Across all six clinical trials conducted by Metabolic (reportedly ~925 patients total), the drug **failed to meet its efficacy endpoints** and development was terminated [10]

**Safety study (Stier et al., 2013):**
- Published safety and tolerability data from the human trials
- Reported that AOD-9604 retained lipolytic properties of hGH without stimulating IGF-1 [1]
- **Note:** This publication post-dated the termination of development and did not overturn the efficacy failure

Human evidence:
- ~~Large-trial efficacy~~ → Failed in Phase IIb
- ~1.8 kg additional weight loss over placebo in Phase IIa (modest)
- No IGF-1 elevation (differentiating from hGH)
- **No long-term human safety data** beyond the 24-week trial period

## Health Optimization Applications

**⚠️ AOD-9604 is a research compound that failed in human clinical trials for its primary indication. It is not approved for any medical use and is banned in sport.**

### Fat Loss (Failed Indication)

The original therapeutic premise — selective lipolysis without growth effects — was mechanistically intriguing but **failed to translate to meaningful human outcomes**. The 1.8 kg advantage over placebo in a 12-week trial was not replicated in the longer 24-week confirmatory trial. [3]

### Cartilage/Joint Health (Preclinical Only)

The Kwon & Park (2015) rabbit study finding that intra-articular AOD-9604 enhanced cartilage regeneration is an isolated preclinical observation. It has not been followed up in human studies. Given the development history, this finding should be treated as an exploratory signal only. [9]

### Stacking Context

In the biohacking/research community, AOD-9604 is sometimes discussed in combination with growth hormone secretagogues ([[ipamorelin]], [[modified-grf-1-29]]) or [[tesamorelin]], with the theory that it adds a lipolytic component to GHRH/GHRP stacks. There is:
- **No published human data** on such combinations
- **No pharmacokinetic interaction data**
- All stacking information is anecdotal (Reddit/forum) and should be treated as speculative

## Safety Profile

### Known Safety Data

- **No increase in IGF-1** — confirmed in human studies, suggesting absence of mitogenic/growth-promoting signaling [1]
- **No diabetogenic effects** — AOD-9604 was reported not to induce insulin resistance (in contrast to hGH) [4]
- **No interference with WADA hGH isoform test** — confirmed analytically (Orlovius et al., 2013) [10]

### Unknowns and Concerns

- **Cancer risk:** While AOD-9604 does not elevate IGF-1 (the primary mitogenic pathway for hGH), the lack of long-term safety data means the theoretical question of whether any hGH fragment retains mitogenic potential is **unanswered**
- **Only 24 weeks of human exposure data** across all terminated trials
- **Gray market sourcing risk:** AOD-9604 has been identified in seized, unauthorized pharmaceutical preparations (Belgium, 2014). Purity/sterility of gray-market products is unknown. [7]
- **2026 review by Mendias & Awan:** Classified AOD-9604 among unapproved peptides with "potential for serious harm to patients" due to scarce rigorous human safety data [8]

**Safety designation:** Research-only. Never progressed beyond Phase IIb. No long-term human safety data exists.

## Dosing Protocols

**⚠️ All dosing information below is from research contexts and anecdotal reports. AOD-9604 failed clinical trials and is not approved for human use. No dosing protocol can be recommended.**

### Research Trial Dosing

The human clinical trials used oral administration (AOD-9604 was developed as an orally active peptide), but specific doses from published trials are not clearly detailed in accessible abstracts. The Phase II program tested multiple dosing regimens before termination. [1][3]

### Anecdotal Reports (Reddit/Forums)

The following is compiled from community discussion and should be treated as **unverified and potentially dangerous**:

- Typical research discussion mentions 250–500 mcg/day via subcutaneous injection
- Some anecdotal protocols discuss 300 mcg 1–2× daily
- Oral bioavailability claims exist but are inconsistent with injection-dominant community use

**Source note:** These dosing figures come from Reddit (r/Peptides) and bodybuilding forums. No peer-reviewed dosing guidance exists. These are not recommendations.

### Comparison Summary

AOD-9604 occupies a unique conceptual niche: a **non-GH-releasing lipolytic peptide**. Unlike [[modified-grf-1-29]], [[tesamorelin]], and [[ipamorelin]] — all of which work by stimulating endogenous GH release (and thus involve IGF-1 elevation) — AOD-9604 was designed to directly activate lipolytic pathways without GH receptor signaling. This avoids:
- IGF-1-mediated growth effects (and theoretical cancer concerns)
- Insulin resistance (diabetogenic effect of GH)
- Pituitary desensitization

However, this theoretical advantage was **not validated in human trials**. The confirmatory Phase IIb trial failed, and the drug was abandoned. In contrast, [[tesamorelin]] succeeded in clinical trials and received FDA approval. [[ipamorelin]] and [[modified-grf-1-29]] have more robust human data as diagnostic agents and in research settings.

**Summary:** AOD-9604 is a scientifically interesting peptide fragment that demonstrated a clean mechanistic separation of lipolysis from growth effects in mice, but **failed to translate this to meaningful human outcomes**. The contrast between its theoretical elegance and clinical failure makes it a cautionary example in peptide development. Its persistence on the gray market and in doping scandals is driven by the appeal of "fat loss without growth effects" — a compelling narrative unsupported by confirmatory evidence.
